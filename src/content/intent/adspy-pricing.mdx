## AdSpy pricing at a glance

AdSpy costs **$149/month** (as of April 2026). There's one tier, one price, and no free trial. AdSpy claims 200M+ ads indexed across Facebook and Instagram with Boolean search and a decade-plus of historical depth. Whether that's worth $149/month depends on your use case — which is what this page unpacks.

AdSpy's pricing model is one of the simplest in the ad spy category: no freemium, no multi-tier confusion, no enterprise upsell. You either pay $149/month or you don't use AdSpy. That simplicity is a feature for experienced buyers and a barrier for first-timers who want to test before committing.

## AdSpy tier breakdown

AdSpy operates on a single-tier subscription model (as of April 2026):

**Standard — $149/month**
- Full database access (AdSpy claims 200M+ ads)
- Meta coverage (Facebook + Instagram only)
- Boolean search operators
- Unlimited saved searches
- Advertiser page tracking
- Comment tracking on competitor ads
- Historical data back to ~2015
- Single-user access

There is no lower-tier plan. There is no higher enterprise tier publicly listed. If you need team seats or API access, contact AdSpy sales for custom pricing — it's not self-serve.

Compare to multi-tier competitors: BigSpy has 4 tiers from free to $249/month. PowerAdSpy has tiers from $49/month to $299/month. Foreplay has tiers from $49/month to enterprise. AdSpy's single-price simplicity is either refreshing or frustrating depending on your position on pricing transparency.

## Refund policy

AdSpy offers a **3-day money-back guarantee** (as of April 2026). You pay the full $149 upfront, use the tool for up to 72 hours, and can request a refund if you're not satisfied.

Honest read: 3 days is tight. Most serious research workflows take a week or two to establish before you know if a tool fits. The short refund window and lack of free trial are the most common complaints about AdSpy's commercial terms. Budget for at least the first month as a committed spend — treat the refund window as an escape hatch, not a planning horizon.

## ROI math: when AdSpy pays for itself

At $149/month ($1,788/year), AdSpy needs to drive at least $149/month in research value to break even. For most working Meta affiliates, that bar is modest — one useful competitor insight per month that wouldn't have come from your existing research usually clears $149 in margin.

Where the math works:

- **Solo Meta affiliate with $3,000+ ad testing budget/month:** AdSpy is a 5% line item on your research costs. One winner per quarter pays for the year.
- **Agency managing $50k+/month in Meta ad spend:** AdSpy is rounding error. The time saved on research justifies it per client.
- **Experienced operator with mature search workflow:** Boolean search depth earns the subscription by itself.

Where the math fails:

- **Side-hustle affiliate testing under $500/month:** $149/month is 30% of your testing budget. The math doesn't work.
- **TikTok-first buyer:** AdSpy doesn't cover TikTok. You're paying for features you can't use.
- **Budget-constrained solo operator:** BigSpy's free tier gets you 60% of the value at 0% of the cost.

## The AdRecon comparison

We run AdRecon, so take this section with that disclosure — we've tried to keep it factual.

**AdRecon price:** $299 or $599 one-time lifetime (as of April 2026 — still confirm at checkout).

**What AdRecon does that AdSpy doesn't:**
- Automatic affiliate-network classification (ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, WarriorPlus) via regex-based pattern matching
- Landing Page Ripper (Puppeteer + SingleFile full HTML/CSS/asset archives)
- Winning Lander Vault (landing pages grouped by linked ads + days-running)
- Proven Creatives (identical creatives grouped across advertisers with 15+/30+/60+/90+ proof levels)
- Winning Offers Directory across 300,000+ records
- Dead Creative Tracking (surfaces ads that stopped running after long runs — rare in the category)

**What AdSpy does that AdRecon doesn't:**
- Deeper Meta historical index (AdSpy claims 200M+ ads since ~2015)
- Boolean search operators (AdRecon uses filter-chip search)
- Comment tracking on ads

Both tools are Meta-only. AdRecon does not cover TikTok, YouTube, Google, LinkedIn, or Pinterest. AdSpy doesn't either.

**When AdSpy still wins:** you need Boolean search specifically, or you need the deepest possible Meta historical index across a multi-year window, or you already know the AdSpy UI and switching costs are real.

## Hidden costs to consider

- **No free trial upfront cost:** $149 to evaluate the tool. Budget for at least one month as a committed spend.
- **Single-user pricing:** If your team needs access, you're paying per-seat or running shared accounts (against terms).
- **No API access on standard tier:** Programmatic access is custom/enterprise pricing.
- **No landing page archive:** If you need LP research, you're paying a second tool (or AdRecon) on top of AdSpy.
- **Meta-only coverage:** If you need any other platform, you're layering additional subscriptions.

## Our verdict on AdSpy pricing

AdSpy's $149/month is defensible for its target buyer: experienced Meta-only affiliate marketers with mature research workflows and budgets that can absorb $1,788/year as a fixed research line item. For that persona, the pricing is in line with the value delivered.

For most 2026 affiliate marketers, the honest calculation tilts toward AdRecon's lifetime pricing or BigSpy's lower entry tiers. The math is not close over 2+ year horizons — $299 one-time beats $3,576 over 24 months by roughly 12x. Unless Boolean search or historical depth is specifically critical to your workflow, the comparative pricing is hard to justify.

If you're on the fence: use BigSpy's free tier to learn the category, then make the lifetime-vs-monthly call based on your actual weekly research minutes. The more you research, the more a lifetime license pencils out.
