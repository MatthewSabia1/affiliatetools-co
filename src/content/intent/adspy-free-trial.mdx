## Does AdSpy have a free trial?

**No.** AdSpy does not offer a free trial in 2026 (as of April 2026). This is one of the most common complaints from prospective users and one of the primary reasons buyers evaluate alternatives. If you want to test AdSpy before committing, your only option is the 3-day money-back guarantee — which means paying the full $149 upfront and requesting a refund within 72 hours.

AdSpy has held this policy consistently for years. Unlike BigSpy (which has a genuinely free tier) or Foreplay (which typically offers a 7-day trial), AdSpy positions itself as a commitment-first tool. The implicit assumption is that experienced buyers already know they need Meta ad research and don't need to "try before buying."

For first-time ad spy buyers, this posture is a barrier. For experienced operators who already know what AdSpy does, it's a non-issue.

## The 3-day money-back guarantee explained

AdSpy's refund window works like this:

1. Sign up and pay $149 for one month of access.
2. Use the tool for up to 72 hours.
3. If unsatisfied, contact AdSpy support to request a refund.
4. Refund is processed if requested within the 3-day window.

Important caveats (as of April 2026):

- **The 72-hour clock starts at payment**, not at first login. If you pay on Monday at 9am, your refund window closes Thursday at 9am regardless of when you actually used the tool.
- **Refund eligibility is at AdSpy's discretion.** The 3-day window is their stated policy, but individual cases have varied. Document usage and dissatisfaction clearly.
- **Partial refunds are not offered** — it's full refund or no refund, typically based on the 72-hour window.

Always verify current terms directly with AdSpy support before signing up. Policies change and we can only report what's publicly stated as of April 2026.

## ROI math: is paying $149 to evaluate worth it?

Honest calculation. If you pay $149 to test AdSpy and decide it's worth continuing, you've paid month 1 of a subscription you'd have paid anyway — zero dollars wasted.

## Our verdict on AdSpy's trial policy

AdSpy's no-free-trial stance is a genuine friction point in 2026. The 3-day money-back guarantee is tight, and the $149 upfront commitment is substantial for first-time buyers. For experienced Meta affiliates who already know the category, this is a non-issue — the money-back guarantee is enough.

For first-time ad spy buyers, the honest recommendation is to start with BigSpy's free tier to learn the category, then evaluate whether AdSpy's Meta depth is worth the $149/mo commitment. If you're a serious Meta-only affiliate, the commitment usually makes sense. If you're exploring the category, lower-risk entry points exist.

Bottom line: if you want to try AdSpy without risk, you can't — the closest option is the 3-day refund window. If trial access is important, use BigSpy's free tier, Foreplay's 7-day trial, or evaluate AdRecon's lifetime license as alternatives.
