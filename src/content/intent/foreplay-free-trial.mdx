## Does Foreplay have a free trial?

**Yes.** Foreplay typically offers a **7-day free trial** (as of April 2026). Trial length has varied historically — sometimes shorter at 3–5 days, sometimes extended during promotions — so verify current terms on Foreplay's pricing page before signing up.

The Foreplay trial is one of the genuinely useful evaluation windows in the ad spy category. Because Foreplay is built around creative workflow (brief builder, swipe files, team collaboration), 7 days is enough to run one full cycle: reference hunt on Monday, brief assembly by Wednesday, team review by Friday. That's the actual use case for a creative strategist, and 7 days lets you test it.

Credit card is typically required. The trial auto-converts to paid at end. Cancel if not continuing.

## What's included in Foreplay's trial

Trial access typically (as of April 2026) includes:

- **Ad research** across Meta (Facebook, Instagram), TikTok, and potentially other platforms Foreplay indexes
- **Swipe files** — create, organize, and tag reference ads
- **Brief builder** — assemble references into client-ready creative briefs
- **Team collaboration** (depending on tier selected for trial)
- **Modern UI** with design-tool-adjacent workflows
- **Saved searches** and filters

What typically differs from full paid access:

- **Time-limited** to 7 days
- **Auto-conversion** to paid at trial end
- **Tier-specific** — Inspiration trial gets Inspiration features; Agency trial gets Agency features
- **Potentially limited** export options or seat count

Verify your specific trial terms on Foreplay's signup page.

## How to evaluate Foreplay in 7 days

**Day 1: Reference hunting.**
- Run 10 searches across platforms for a hypothetical client brief
- Build a swipe file with 30–50 reference ads
- Tag and organize by concept, hook, or creative angle

**Days 2–3: Brief assembly.**
- Use the brief builder to turn your swipe file into a client-facing creative brief
- Test adding annotations, design notes, or creator-direction language
- Evaluate the deliverable — would you send this brief to a creative team?

**Days 4–5: Team collaboration (if applicable).**
- Invite one teammate to test multi-user features
- Test permissions, commenting, and shared swipe files
- Evaluate whether collaboration is smoother than your current workflow (Figma + Airtable + Slack, etc.)

**Day 6: Comparison and decision.**
- Compare Foreplay workflow vs your current reference-gathering process
- Calculate: would Foreplay save 2+ hours per brief?
- Check pricing: Inspiration $49/mo, Agency $99/mo — which tier matches your actual team size?

**Day 7: Cancel or commit.**
- If workflow fit is strong: commit and let subscription convert
- If not: cancel at least 24 hours before trial end
- Verify cancellation via email confirmation and card statement

7 days is a real, usable evaluation window. Treat it deliberately.

## Trial gotchas to watch for

**Auto-conversion to paid.** Foreplay's trial converts automatically at end. Set a calendar reminder for day 6 to decide cancel-or-continue.

**Credit card required.** Standard for trials. Budget for potential charges if you miss cancellation.

**Tier trial vs full product.** The tier you select for trial determines which features you can evaluate. If you're an agency considering the Agency tier, start your trial at Agency tier, not Inspiration.

**Annual vs monthly conversion.** Verify whether trial converts to monthly or annual billing. Some promotional trials push toward annual plans for discounted rates.

**Refund policy post-conversion.** If you miss the trial cancellation window, refund eligibility is at Foreplay's discretion. Don't rely on post-conversion refunds as a safety net.
