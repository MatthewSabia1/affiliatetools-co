## PowerAdSpy pricing at a glance

PowerAdSpy runs a multi-tier subscription model starting at **$49/month** (Basic) and scaling up to **$299/month** (Ultimate) as of April 2026. Every tier covers PowerAdSpy's full 8-platform set: Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, Google Display, Native (Taboola/Outbrain style), Quora, Reddit, and TikTok.

The primary reason to pick a higher tier is research volume and filter depth — not additional platforms. This is an important distinction from tools like BigSpy where tier gates feel more arbitrary. On PowerAdSpy, higher tiers unlock meaningfully more usable research volume.

## PowerAdSpy tier breakdown

Exact tier names and boundaries have changed historically — PowerAdSpy restructures pricing periodically. The below reflects the structure as of April 2026. Verify current tiers on PowerAdSpy's pricing page before purchasing.

**Basic — $49/month**
- All 8 platforms
- Limited daily searches
- Standard filters
- Saved searches (limited count)
- Single-user access

Basic is the entry tier for solo multi-platform buyers. It's priced competitively against Foreplay Inspiration ($49/month as of April 2026) and PiPiADS Starter ($77/month as of April 2026).

**Standard / Premium tiers (typically $99–$149/month)**
- Expanded query volume
- Full filter access including advanced
- More saved searches
- Bulk export
- Some team features

These are the "real work" tiers where PowerAdSpy's multi-platform breadth actually gets used. Most paying users land in this range.

**Ultimate — $299/month**
- Maximum query volume
- Full feature access
- Team seats
- API access (check current tier)
- Priority support

Ultimate is priced for enterprise buyers and agencies. It matches AdSpy's $149/month point for price-to-depth on Meta — you're paying the premium for platform breadth plus enterprise features.

## Trial terms

PowerAdSpy typically offers a **7-day trial on select tiers** (as of April 2026). Trial availability has varied — some tiers have included trials, others required full upfront payment. Verify on PowerAdSpy's current pricing page.

If a trial is available on your chosen tier, use it. A week is enough to validate whether the multi-platform breadth matches your workflow.

## Which PowerAdSpy tier should you pick?

**Basic ($49/month) if:**
- You actively use 3+ of the supported platforms (Meta, Native, Google Display, YouTube, TikTok, Quora, Reddit)
- You're a solo operator
- You do 2–5 hours of ad research per week

**Standard/Premium (mid-tier) if:**
- You're an agency with multiple client accounts
- You do 10+ hours of research per week
- You need bulk export or advanced filters

**Ultimate ($299/month) if:**
- You have 3+ team members needing seats
- You need API access for integrations
- You're running enterprise-scale multi-platform research

Honest read: most solo operators should stay on Basic. The jump to Ultimate is typically justified only by team size or technical integration needs, not by research depth alone.

## ROI math by tier

**Basic at $49/month ($588/year):** Break-even requires one useful insight per month worth $49+ in margin. For a working multi-platform affiliate, this is trivial to clear. Basic pays for itself with minimal use.

**Mid-tier at ~$99–$149/month ($1,188–$1,788/year):** Break-even requires the tier's incremental features over Basic to drive $600–$1,200/year in additional value. For agencies with multi-client workflows, this usually works. For solo operators, it often doesn't.

**Ultimate at $299/month ($3,588/year):** Break-even requires enterprise-scale research — daily usage across multiple team members or campaigns. If you're not a 3+ person team, the ROI math doesn't support Ultimate over Basic or mid-tier.

## The AdRecon comparison

AdRecon is $299 or $599 one-time lifetime (as of April 2026 — confirm at checkout). For Meta-only affiliate marketers, the pricing comparison is dramatic over multi-year horizons.

**What AdRecon adds vs PowerAdSpy:**
- Automatic affiliate-network classification (ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, WarriorPlus) via regex pattern matching — PowerAdSpy does not classify networks
- Landing Page Ripper (Puppeteer + SingleFile) for full HTML/CSS/asset archive downloads — PowerAdSpy has no LP capture
- Winning Lander Vault grouping landers by ads/days-running — PowerAdSpy has no LP archive
- Proven Creatives grouping across advertisers (15+/30+/60+/90+ proof levels) — PowerAdSpy doesn't group identical creatives
- Winning Offers Directory across 300,000+ records — PowerAdSpy doesn't aggregate offers
- Dead Creative Tracking — rare in the category, surfaces ads that stopped running after long runs

**What PowerAdSpy has that AdRecon doesn't:**
- 8-platform coverage including Native, Google Display, YouTube, Quora, Reddit, TikTok — AdRecon is Meta-only
- Native ad coverage (Taboola/Outbrain style) — genuinely differentiated
- Multi-tier team features

For multi-platform buyers who specifically need Native or Google Display coverage, PowerAdSpy is not directly replaceable by AdRecon. For Meta-only affiliates, AdRecon replaces PowerAdSpy at a fraction of the long-term cost.

## Hidden costs to consider

- **Data quality variance:** Multi-platform coverage means per-platform quality varies. Some affiliate communities have reported mixed data freshness.
- **No affiliate-network classification:** Manual tagging required for CPA work — factor in the time cost.
- **No landing page archive:** LP research requires a second tool.
- **Tier upgrade pressure:** Basic's query limits can nudge serious users toward mid-tier faster than expected.

## Our verdict on PowerAdSpy pricing

PowerAdSpy's Basic tier at $49/month is competitively priced for its target buyer — a multi-platform media buyer who genuinely uses Native, Google Display, or other non-Meta platforms. For that persona, $49/month delivers real value.

At higher tiers, PowerAdSpy's pricing stretches. Ultimate at $299/month is priced against enterprise needs that most solo operators don't have. If you're considering Ultimate, ask whether the marginal features over mid-tier justify $200+/month — they usually don't unless you have team or API requirements.

For Meta-only affiliate work, the comparative math tilts heavily toward AdRecon's lifetime license over any long-term horizon. If your work is genuinely multi-platform with Native or Display in the mix, PowerAdSpy Basic is the right answer.

Bottom line: start on Basic if you qualify as a multi-platform buyer. Use the 7-day trial if available. Upgrade only when you hit concrete constraints. For Meta-focused CPA work, evaluate AdRecon first.
