## The short answer
For agencies, Foreplay is the superior choice if your primary goal is creative strategy, mood-boarding, and streamlining the hand-off between researchers and designers. AdSpy is the better choice if you are a "data miner" who needs to perform complex Boolean searches across a decade of Meta's historical ad data to find obscure winning patterns. In short: Foreplay manages the creative process; AdSpy manages the raw data.

## Context — what's actually being asked
When an agency asks "Foreplay vs AdSpy," they aren't actually asking about feature lists. They are asking about the friction in their internal production pipeline. An agency doesn't just need to *find* a winning ad; they need to communicate *why* it's winning to a creative director, a copywriter, and a client, and then execute a version of it.

The naive answer is to compare the size of the databases. But for an agency, a database of 100 million ads is useless if the process of saving those ads, categorizing them by "hook" or "angle," and presenting them in a brief takes four hours of manual screenshotting and Google Doc formatting.

The real tension here is between **Discovery** (AdSpy) and **Workflow** (Foreplay). AdSpy is a powerful telescope for spotting things far away in the Meta ecosystem. Foreplay is a high-end filing cabinet and presentation deck that ensures the things you spot actually get turned into high-performing assets.

## The full breakdown

### Foreplay
<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay is designed specifically to solve the "screenshot fatigue" that plagues agency creative strategists. Instead of manually saving images and links into a messy folder, Foreplay allows users to save ads directly from the Meta Ad Library into organized "Boards." This creates a permanent archive; even if the advertiser turns the ad off, the agency still has the creative and the landing page reference saved in their internal library.

The core value proposition for agencies is the creative brief workflow. Foreplay allows you to move from "inspiration" to "execution" without leaving the platform. You can tag ads by specific attributes—such as "UGC," "Comparison," or "Problem/Solution"—and then share these boards with your design team. This eliminates the back-and-forth emails and fragmented Slack messages that typically accompany a creative pivot.

Pricing for Foreplay is tiered based on the size of the agency: Inspiration at $49/mo, Strategist at $99/mo, and Agency at $199/mo (as of April 2026). 

However, Foreplay is not a "spy tool" in the traditional sense of having a massive, searchable historical database that you can query with complex logic. It is more of a sophisticated saving and organizing layer that sits on top of existing ad libraries. If you need to find every ad in the "Weight Loss" niche that ran between 2017 and 2019 and contained the word "Keto" but NOT the word "Supplement," Foreplay cannot do that. It is a tool for the *now* and the *future* of your creative, not a historical archive.

### AdSpy
<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

AdSpy is the "old guard" of Meta ad intelligence. Its primary strength is its sheer volume of data and its search capabilities. For an agency that specializes in deep market research or competitive intelligence, AdSpy is an indispensable tool because of its Boolean search operators. You can use AND, OR, and NOT operators to filter through millions of ads with surgical precision.

If you are tasked with finding a "winning angle" for a client in a saturated market, AdSpy allows you to see what worked five years ago and what is working today. This historical perspective is something Foreplay simply does not provide. You can filter by likes, comments, and shares to find "viral" content, which serves as a strong proxy for engagement.

The downside for agencies is the UX and the workflow. AdSpy feels like software from 2012. It is a raw data dump. Once you find an ad you like, you are on your own to save it, organize it, and present it to your team. There are no "boards," no "briefs," and no integrated way to collaborate with a creative team. You find the ad, you screenshot it, and you move it into another tool (like Notion or Trello).

Pricing is a flat $149/mo (as of April 2026), which can be a steep recurring cost for smaller agencies, especially since there is no free trial to test the waters.

### AdRecon
<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

While Foreplay handles the brief and AdSpy handles the history, AdRecon targets the specific needs of the CPA (Cost Per Acquisition) affiliate agency. It is a Meta-only stack, but it does something neither Foreplay nor AdSpy does: it classifies ads by affiliate network.

For agencies running offers from ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, or WarriorPlus, AdRecon is a massive shortcut. Instead of guessing if an ad is for an affiliate offer or a brand-owned product, AdRecon tells you exactly which network the offer belongs to. This allows agencies to reverse-engineer the entire funnel in seconds.

The "Landing Page Ripper" is the standout feature here. While Foreplay saves the ad, AdRecon allows you to download the full landing page HTML, CSS, and assets as a ZIP file. For an agency that needs to rapidly prototype a high-converting landing page based on a competitor's success, this eliminates hours of manual rebuilding.

Pricing is a significant disruptor: $299 or $599 for a lifetime license (one-time payment, not subscription) as of April 2026. This removes the monthly "software tax" that agencies hate.

### BigSpy
<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

BigSpy is the "generalist" option. For agencies that manage clients across multiple platforms—Meta, TikTok, Twitter/X, YouTube, and Pinterest—BigSpy provides a single pane of glass. It is not as deep as AdSpy on Meta, nor as workflow-oriented as Foreplay, but it covers more ground.

The biggest draw for small agencies or freelancers is the pricing. With a real free tier (no credit card required) and a Basic plan at $9/mo (as of April 2026), it is the lowest barrier to entry in the category. If you just need to occasionally check what a competitor is doing on Pinterest or TikTok without spending $200/mo, BigSpy is the logical choice.

However, "broad" often means "shallow." BigSpy lacks the sophisticated filtering of AdSpy and the organizational power of Foreplay. It also lacks any affiliate-specific classification or landing page archiving. It is a tool for sampling the market, not for building a professional-grade creative production pipeline.

### PowerAdSpy
<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

PowerAdSpy is the tool for agencies that venture into Native advertising. While most of the tools in this list focus on Social, PowerAdSpy provides significant coverage for Taboola, Outbrain, and RevContent. If your agency's strategy involves a "Meta for awareness, Native for conversion" approach, this is the tool you need.

It covers almost every major surface, including Reddit, Quora, and LinkedIn. This makes it a powerful tool for B2B agencies that need to see what's working on professional networks.

The weaknesses are similar to BigSpy: the UX is dated, and the depth of data on any single platform is thinner than what you'd get from a specialist. It doesn't offer creative brief workflows or affiliate network classification. Pricing ranges from $49/mo to $249/mo (as of April 2026), depending on the level of access required.

### PiPiADS
<ToolLogo slug="pipiads" size="lg" showName />

If your agency has pivoted heavily toward TikTok, PiPiADS is non-negotiable. While Foreplay and BigSpy offer TikTok coverage, PiPiADS is a specialist. It provides the deepest TikTok ad database in the industry and integrates directly with the TikTok Creative Center.

For agencies running "TikTok-first" campaigns, the ability to see real-time trending products and specific TikTok-native creative hooks is more valuable than Meta's historical data.

The downside is that it is very weak on Meta. If you are a full-service agency, you cannot replace Foreplay or AdSpy with PiPiADS; you would likely use PiPiADS *alongside* one of them. Pricing starts at $77/mo and goes up to $155/mo (as of April 2026).

## The honest answer for each buyer profile

**The Creative-Heavy Agency:** You have a team of designers and copywriters. You spend more time in Figma than in Ads Manager. **Pick: Foreplay.** The ability to build mood boards and briefs will save you 10+ hours of project management per week.

**The Market Research Agency:** You are hired to find "the gap" in a market. You need to know what worked in 2018 to predict what will work in 2026. **Pick: AdSpy.** The Boolean search and 10-year archive are unmatched for deep-dive intelligence.

**The CPA/Affiliate Agency:** You run high-volume Meta ads for ClickBank or MaxWeb offers. You care about the landing page and the offer classification more than the "vibe" of the creative. **Pick: AdRecon.** The lifetime pricing and Landing Page Ripper are built specifically for this business model.

**The Multi-Platform Boutique:** You manage small budgets across Meta, TikTok, and Pinterest for local businesses. You can't justify $200/mo in software. **Pick: BigSpy.** The free tier and low-cost entry point make it the only viable option for low-margin accounts.

**The B2B/Native Agency:** You run lead-gen campaigns on Taboola and LinkedIn. Social ads are only a small part of your mix. **Pick: PowerAdSpy.** It is the only tool that gives you a professional look at Native ad surfaces.

**The TikTok-First Agency:** Your clients are Gen Z brands. You don't care about Facebook; you only care about the "For You" page. **Pick: PiPiADS.** Its TikTok depth is far superior to the generalist tools.

## Common mistakes buyers make

### Buying for "Features" instead of "Workflow"
Many agency owners buy AdSpy because it has "more ads." But they forget that "more ads" doesn't equal "more revenue." If your team spends three hours a day digging through a massive database but has no system to save and implement those findings, you've bought a library but you've forgotten to hire a librarian. Before buying, ask: "Do I have a problem finding ads, or a problem implementing them?"

### Ignoring the "Platform Lock-in"
Agencies often commit to a Meta-only tool (like AdSpy) and then panic when a client asks them to launch on TikTok. While you can always add another tool, the mental overhead of managing two different "spy" interfaces is high. If you are a generalist, start with a multi-platform tool or a workflow tool like Foreplay that handles both.

### Overlooking Landing Page Friction
The biggest mistake in "spying" is focusing only on the ad. A great ad with a terrible landing page is a losing campaign. Tools like Foreplay and AdSpy show you the ad, but they don't help you *clone* the success of the landing page. Agency owners often spend $150/mo on a spy tool and then spend another 10 hours of a developer's time trying to manually recreate a competitor's funnel.

## When NOT to buy this tool

Do NOT buy Foreplay if you are a solo operator who doesn't work with a creative team. If you are the one doing the research AND the one making the ads, the "briefing" and "board" features are overkill. You don't need a mood board for yourself; you just need to know what works. In that case, a raw search tool like AdSpy or a budget tool like BigSpy is more efficient.

Do NOT buy AdSpy if you are strictly running TikTok or Native ads. AdSpy is a Meta specialist. Using it for a non-Meta strategy is a waste of $149/mo. If your focus is outside the Meta ecosystem, look toward PiPiADS or PowerAdSpy.
