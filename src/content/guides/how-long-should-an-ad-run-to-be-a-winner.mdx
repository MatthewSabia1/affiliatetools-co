## TL;DR
* **The Core Signal:** The longer an ad runs, the higher the probability it is profitable.
* **The Tiers:** 1-14 days (Testing), 15-30 days (Promising), 31-90 days (Proven), 90+ days (Legendary).
* **The Logic:** Affiliate marketers are ruthless with budgets; we kill losing ads instantly. Longevity equals ROI.
* **The Strategy:** Don't just copy the creative; analyze the "days-running" to determine how much budget you should risk on your first test.

## Why this matters
Most beginner affiliate marketers use spy tools incorrectly. They search for a keyword, find an ad that looks "cool," and immediately launch a campaign based on that single creative. This is gambling, not media buying.

The real value of a spy tool isn't the image or the copy—it's the metadata. Specifically, the "days-running" metric. In the world of CPA and affiliate marketing, where we don't own the product and operate on tight margins, longevity is the only honest signal of profitability. If an ad has been active for 120 days, it has survived the "learning phase," weathered platform volatility, and consistently generated more revenue than it cost to run.

## The workflow / framework

### Step 1: Filter for the "Survival Threshold"
The first step in any research session is to eliminate the noise. Your feed is likely cluttered with "test" ads—creatives that an operator launched yesterday and will kill tomorrow because the CPA is too high.

Set your initial filter to a minimum of 15 to 30 days. This removes the "noise" of the initial testing phase. By filtering for ads that have survived at least two weeks, you are only looking at creatives that have passed the first basic sanity check of the advertiser.

### Step 2: Categorize by Profitability Tiers
Once you have a clean feed, categorize the results into tiers to determine your risk level.

* **15–30 Days (Promising):** These are likely "scaling" ads. The operator has found a winner and is pushing the budget. These are great for finding fresh trends before they become saturated.
* **31–90 Days (Proven):** These are stable winners. These ads have a consistent conversion rate and a predictable ROI. This is the "safe zone" for most affiliates.
* **90+ Days (Legendary):** These are the "Evergreen" winners. These creatives have tapped into a fundamental psychological trigger that works regardless of minor market shifts. If you see an ad running for 120+ days, the hook is almost certainly bulletproof.

### Step 3: Cross-Reference with Advertiser Volume
A single ad running for 60 days is a strong signal. Five different advertisers running the same creative (or slight variations of it) for 60 days is a "gold mine."

When you find a long-running ad, look for "Proven Creatives" groupings. If you see multiple accounts from the same affiliate network (e.g., ClickBank or MaxWeb) using the same angle, you have found a winning offer-creative combination. This removes the risk that the longevity was just due to one operator having an abnormally high budget or a "lucky" account.

### Step 4: Analyze the "Burn Rate" vs. Longevity
Compare the days-running to the current date. An ad that ran for 100 days but stopped two weeks ago is a lesson in what *used* to work. An ad that has been running for 100 days and is *still active today* is a blueprint for what is working *now*. 

Always prioritize the "still active" status. The goal is to find the intersection of high longevity and current activity.

## Pitfalls to avoid

### The "High Budget" Delusion
Some big agencies or "whales" can afford to run a losing ad for 30 days just to gather data. This is a false positive. This is why Step 3 (Cross-Referencing) is vital. If only one massive account is running the ad, be cautious. If ten small-to-medium accounts are running it, it's profitable.

### The "Creative Fatigue" Trap
Just because an ad has run for 180 days doesn't mean it will work if you launch it today. The market may have developed "banner blindness" to that specific image. When you find a legendary winner, don't copy it pixel-for-pixel. Instead, identify the *core hook* and the *emotional trigger*, then create a fresh visual that delivers that same message.

### Ignoring the Landing Page
A winning ad is only 50% of the equation. An ad might run for 90 days not because the creative is amazing, but because the landing page is a high-converting masterpiece. If you copy the ad but use a generic bridge page, you will fail. You must analyze the entire funnel.

## Which tools fit which step

For the initial "wide net" search across multiple platforms (TikTok, YouTube, Google), **bigspy** or **poweradspy** are the logical choices. They provide the breadth needed to see if a trend is moving across different surfaces. If you are specifically targeting TikTok, **pipiads** is the undisputed leader for depth of data.
