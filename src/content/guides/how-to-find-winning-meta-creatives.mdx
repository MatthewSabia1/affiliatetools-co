## TL;DR
* **Prioritize Longevity:** Use "days running" as your primary filter to separate tests from winners.
* **Look for Clusters:** Find "Proven Creatives" where multiple different advertisers are running the same image/video.
* **Deconstruct, Don't Copy:** Break the ad into Hook $\rightarrow$ Angle $\rightarrow$ CTA and iterate.
* **Analyze the Funnel:** The ad is only half the battle; rip the landing page to see the full conversion path.

## Why this matters
Most affiliate marketers fail because they guess. They spend $500 on a "hunch" about a creative, only to find out the market has already moved on. In the Meta ecosystem, the cost of entry is high, and the penalty for bad creative is an immediate ban or a drained budget.

The goal of professional research isn't to find a "magic" ad to copy, but to identify a proven *pattern* of success. When you find a creative that has been running for 60 days across five different accounts, you aren't looking at a fluke—you're looking at a validated market demand.

## The workflow / framework

### Step 1: Filter for Longevity (The Profitability Signal)
The first mistake beginners make is searching for "new" ads. New ads are often just tests that will be killed in 48 hours. To find winners, you must filter for duration.

Set your filters to show ads that have been active for at least 14 to 30 days. If an advertiser is spending money on a specific creative for a month, the ROI is positive. This "days-running" signal is the most honest metric in affiliate marketing because it represents actual capital at risk.

### Step 2: Identify Cross-Advertiser Grouping
A single advertiser running an ad for 30 days is a good sign. Five different advertisers running the same creative (or slight variations of it) is a goldmine. This is what I call "Cross-Advertiser Grouping."

When you see a cluster of different accounts pushing the same asset, it means the creative is so potent that it's being ripped and shared across the affiliate community. This validates that the creative works regardless of the specific account's seasoning or pixel data.

### Step 3: Creative Decomposition
Once you've found a winning asset, do not simply download it and re-upload it. Meta's algorithms can detect duplicate hashes, and your audience will recognize a recycled ad. Instead, decompose the creative into three parts:

1. **The Hook:** The first 3 seconds of the video or the first line of the copy. What is the pattern interrupt?
2. **The Angle:** What is the core emotional trigger? Is it fear of loss, desire for gain, or a "secret" discovery?
3. **The CTA:** How are they transitioning the user to the click? Is it a "Learn More" soft sell or a "Get it Now" hard sell?

Map these elements out and create a "version 2.0" that improves on the original.

### Step 4: The Landing Page Teardown
The ad is just the "handshake"; the landing page is where the money is made. A winning ad paired with a mediocre LP will still fail. 

You need to see exactly what happens after the click. Look for the bridge page structure: Does it use a VSL (Video Sales Letter), an advertorial, or a direct-to-offer quiz? Analyze the headline, the social proof placement, and the urgency triggers. The most efficient way to do this is to download the full HTML/CSS of the page so you can study the layout without the distractions of live tracking scripts.

## Pitfalls to avoid

### The "Newest First" Trap
Searching by "Newest" is a waste of time. You will find thousands of ads that were launched an hour ago and will be turned off by tomorrow. Always sort by longevity or "most seen" to find the actual winners.

### Ignoring the Network
Not all winners are created equal. An ad that works for a low-ticket WarriorPlus offer might fail for a high-ticket MaxWeb health offer. Always identify which affiliate network the offer belongs to so you can gauge the payout structure and compliance level.

### Over-Optimizing the Creative while Ignoring the Offer
You can have the best creative in the world, but if the offer is "burnt" or the payout is too low to cover your CPMs, you will lose money. Validate the offer's current viability before spending a week perfecting the creative.

## Which tools fit which step

Depending on your budget and goals, different tools serve different parts of this workflow.

If you need a massive historical archive to see how an angle has evolved over the last decade, **adspy** is the industry standard for Boolean search and deep Meta history. If you are a generalist who needs to see if a Meta winner is also working on TikTok or Pinterest, **bigspy** or **poweradspy** provide the necessary multi-platform breadth.
