## TL;DR
* **Filter by Network:** Don't guess keywords; use a tool that auto-classifies BuyGoods traffic to see exactly what is running.
* **The Longevity Signal:** Prioritize ads that have been active for 14+ days; this is the primary indicator of profitability.
* **Rip and Reverse-Engineer:** Download the landing page (LP) to analyze the sales psychology and offer structure.
* **Iterate, Don't Clone:** Use winning hooks as a baseline, then create 3-5 variations to beat the original.

## Why this matters
BuyGoods is a powerhouse for health and wellness offers, but the competition is fierce. If you enter a campaign by simply picking a "top offer" from the BuyGoods dashboard, you are flying blind. You don't know which hooks are currently converting, which landing pages are bypassing Meta's compliance bots, or if the offer is currently saturated.

The difference between a $100/day loss and a $1,000/day win is usually found in the "bridge" between the ad and the checkout page. To win on BuyGoods, you need to see the full funnel—the ad, the pre-lander, and the VSL—before you spend a single cent of your own capital.

## The workflow / framework

### Step 1: Isolate BuyGoods Traffic
The biggest mistake beginners make is searching for generic keywords like "weight loss" or "blood sugar." This returns thousands of results from every network imaginable. To find BuyGoods-specific winners, you need to filter by the network itself.

By isolating BuyGoods traffic, you can see the specific patterns that the top operators in that ecosystem are using. You'll notice if the current trend is "advertorial style" or "direct-to-VSL." This step removes the noise and lets you focus on the offers that are actually available on the BuyGoods platform.

### Step 2: Identify the "Scaling" Signal
Once you have a list of BuyGoods ads, ignore the ones that launched yesterday. In affiliate marketing, the most valuable data point is time. If an advertiser is paying for traffic on a BuyGoods offer for 30, 60, or 90 days, it is almost certainly profitable.

Look for the "days running" metric. A "Winning Offer" isn't the one with the most likes; it's the one that has survived the longest. Your goal is to find these "evergreen" winners and analyze why they are still working.

### Step 3: Map the Full Funnel
An ad is only 20% of the equation. The real magic happens on the landing page. Once you find a winning ad, you need to see exactly where it leads. 

Don't just click the link in your browser; use a landing page ripper to download the full HTML, CSS, and image assets. This allows you to study the page offline and see exactly how the operator is structuring their claims and calls to action (CTAs). Analyze the "hook" of the pre-lander and how it transitions the user into the BuyGoods VSL.

### Step 4: Create "Angle Variations"
Now that you have the winning ad and the winning page, do not copy them verbatim. Meta's algorithms can detect duplicate creatives, and users get "ad blindness" when they see the same image for the tenth time.

Instead, take the winning "angle" (e.g., "The 30-second morning ritual") and create three variations:
1. **The Visual Swap:** Keep the copy, change the image/video.
2. **The Hook Swap:** Keep the image, change the first line of text.
3. **The Format Swap:** Turn a static image ad into a short-form video.

## Pitfalls to avoid

### The "New Offer" Trap
It is tempting to jump on a brand-new BuyGoods offer because it feels "fresh." However, new offers are often unstable. The payout might change, or the landing page might get flagged by Meta within 48 hours. Stick to offers that have a proven track record of stability.

### Ignoring Compliance
BuyGoods offers—especially in the health niche—often push the boundaries of Meta's policies. If you see a winning ad, look closely at how they worded their claims. Are they using "bridge pages" to soften the transition? Are they avoiding "before and after" photos? If you ignore these nuances, your ad account will be disabled before you see your first conversion.

### Over-Optimizing Too Early
Don't kill an ad after 24 hours just because it hasn't converted. BuyGoods offers often have higher price points, meaning the conversion window can be longer. Give your variations at least 3-5 days of consistent spend before deciding if a hook is a failure.
