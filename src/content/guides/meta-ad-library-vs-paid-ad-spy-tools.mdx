## TL;DR
* **Use Meta Ad Library when:** You have a specific competitor's name and want to see their *current* active creatives for free.
* **Use Paid Spy Tools when:** You need to find new offers, analyze dead ads, rip landing pages, or filter by affiliate network.
* **The "Profitability Signal":** In both cases, look for ads running for 30+ days. If they are spending money for a month, the offer is likely converting.
* **The Trade-off:** Free is great for verification; paid is required for discovery and competitive intelligence.

## Why this matters
Most new affiliate marketers make the mistake of thinking "spying" is just about seeing the ad image. They spend hours in the Meta Ad Library, find a cool video, and try to copy it. But they are missing 90% of the equation: the offer, the funnel, and the historical data.

The Meta Ad Library is a transparency tool mandated by regulators; it is not designed for market research. Paid spy tools, on the other hand, are designed to help you find "the gap"—the intersection of a high-converting offer and a creative angle that isn't saturated yet. Knowing when to switch from the free library to a paid stack saves you hundreds of dollars in wasted ad spend.

## The workflow / framework

### Step 1: The "Manual Pulse" (Meta Ad Library)
Start here. If you have a hunch about a niche (e.g., keto supplements) or a specific competitor, search the Meta Ad Library. Filter by "Active Ads" and look for patterns. 

The goal in this step is not to find a "winner," but to identify the current *language* of the market. Are people using "User Generated Content" (UGC) style videos? Are they using long-form storytelling captions? This costs you nothing and gives you a baseline of what the current "standard" looks like.

### Step 2: Hunting for the "Evergreen" Signal
Once you see a pattern, you need to verify if it's actually profitable. In the Ad Library, look at the "Started running on" date. If an ad has been active for 60 days, it is almost certainly making money. 

However, the Ad Library is limited because it only shows you what is active *right now*. You can't see the 50 failed variations the competitor ran before they found that one winner. This is where you hit the "free wall." To see the evolution of a campaign, you need a database that archives dead ads.

### Step 3: Network Classification and Offer Discovery
This is the biggest hurdle for affiliates. In the Meta Ad Library, you see an ad for a "Weight Loss Secret," but you don't know if it's a ClickBank offer, a Digistore24 offer, or a private JV deal. You have to click the ad, go to the landing page, and hunt through the footer or the URL for a vendor ID.

A professional workflow uses a tool that classifies ads by network. Instead of searching for "Weight Loss," you filter for "ClickBank + Health" and sort by "Days Running." This allows you to find offers that are scaling across multiple different advertisers simultaneously—the ultimate signal of a "hot" offer.

### Step 4: Funnel Deconstruction (The Rip)
The ad is only the "hook." The money is made on the landing page. In the free Ad Library, you have to manually screenshot every page of a funnel. If the competitor changes the page or takes it down, that data is gone.

The senior operator's move is to use a "Landing Page Ripper." This allows you to download the full HTML, CSS, and assets of a winning page as a ZIP file. You can then host it locally to analyze the copywriting, the trigger points, and the technical structure without having to guess.

## Pitfalls to avoid

### Trusting "Recent" Ads
Beginners often see an ad that looks amazing and launched yesterday and assume it's a winner. In reality, that ad might be a total failure that gets paused in 48 hours. Never model your entire campaign after an ad that hasn't survived at least 14 days.

### The "Copy-Paste" Trap
Spying is for *inspiration*, not duplication. If you rip a landing page and use the exact same creative, Meta's algorithm often recognizes the duplicate assets, and you'll compete for the same audience as the original advertiser, driving your CPMs up. Use spy tools to find the *angle*, then create your own version of it.

### Over-reliance on Multi-Platform Tools
Many affiliates buy a tool that covers 10 different platforms (TikTok, Pinterest, Google, etc.) when they only actually run Meta ads. While these tools are convenient, they are often "shallow." They might have fewer ads per platform than a dedicated Meta specialist tool. If Meta is your primary traffic source, prioritize depth over breadth.
