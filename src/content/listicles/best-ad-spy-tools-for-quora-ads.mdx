## TL;DR
If your primary goal is spying on Quora ads, PowerAdSpy is the only viable professional choice, starting at $49/mo (as of April 2026). BigSpy is a secondary, cheaper option for surface-level research. For those who realize their offers actually perform better on Meta, AdRecon provides the best affiliate-specific intelligence for a one-time lifetime fee of $299–$599 (as of April 2026).

## Why this ranking exists
Quora is a unique beast in the ad world. Unlike Facebook or TikTok, where the "scroll" is driven by visual stimulation, Quora is an intent-based platform. People are on Quora to find answers to specific problems. Consequently, the ads that work there aren't flashy videos; they are high-utility, text-heavy responses that mimic the natural flow of the platform.

The problem for most affiliate marketers is that the "big name" spy tools are almost exclusively focused on Meta and TikTok. If you search for "best ad spy tools" generally, you'll get a list of tools that are useless for Quora. You cannot apply a TikTok creative strategy to a Quora question thread. You need a tool that can actually index the Quora ad surface and allow you to filter by keywords and demographics.

Most "generalist" tools claim to cover everything, but they often have "shallow" data—meaning they might show you an ad that ran for two days and failed, rather than the winners. For Quora, where the volume is lower than Meta, you need a tool that can distinguish between a fluke and a proven campaign.

### 1. PowerAdSpy — The only serious option for deep Quora ad intelligence
<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

When it comes to Quora, PowerAdSpy is the heavy hitter. While other tools treat Quora as an afterthought or a "bonus" platform, PowerAdSpy has built a legitimate infrastructure for indexing non-social, intent-based ad surfaces. For an affiliate marketer, this is critical because Quora campaigns are won or lost on the copy and the specific question the ad is attached to.

The real power here is the ability to filter by platform specifically for Quora. A typical workflow for a senior operator looks like this: First, you identify a high-intent keyword related to your offer (e.g., "best weight loss supplement for women over 40"). You plug this into PowerAdSpy and filter specifically for Quora. Instead of seeing a million random images, you see the exact text-based ad copy that competitors are using to bridge the gap between a Quora question and a sales page.

Second, you can use PowerAdSpy to analyze the "Native" feel of the ads. Since Quora ads perform best when they don't look like ads, you can use the tool to see which competitors are using a "story-telling" approach versus a "direct-offer" approach. By analyzing the duration the ad has been running, you can infer profitability—if an ad has been active for 30+ days on Quora, it's almost certainly printing money.

However, PowerAdSpy isn't perfect. The UX feels dated, reminiscent of software from the mid-2010s. It isn't as sleek as Foreplay or Minea. Furthermore, while it shows you the ads and the destination URLs, it doesn't provide the deep landing page archiving or "ripping" capabilities that a specialist tool like AdRecon offers for Meta. You'll have to manually visit the landing pages to see the funnel.

Pricing starts at a Basic plan of $49/mo (as of April 2026), scaling up to $249/mo for the Premium tier. Compared to BigSpy, PowerAdSpy is more expensive, but the depth of the Quora database justifies the cost for anyone spending more than $1,000/month on Quora ad spend.

### 2. BigSpy — A budget-friendly way to sample Quora ad trends
<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

BigSpy is the "generalist's" tool. It doesn't go as deep as PowerAdSpy on any single platform, but it covers a massive amount of ground. For a marketer who is just dipping their toes into Quora and isn't ready to commit to a $49+/mo subscription, BigSpy is the logical starting point.

The standout feature here is the real free tier. Unlike many tools that offer a "free trial" requiring a credit card (which usually ends in an unwanted charge), BigSpy's free tier is actually free (as of April 2026). This allows you to run a few searches on Quora to see if there is even any viable competition in your niche before you spend a dime on software.

A common workflow with BigSpy is "cross-platform validation." You might find a winning offer on Meta using AdRecon, and then jump over to BigSpy to see if anyone is successfully running that same offer on Quora. If you see a few active ads on Quora for a Meta-winner, you've just found a massive arbitrage opportunity.

The downside is the "shallowness" of the data. BigSpy claims to cover a wide array of platforms, but the number of ads indexed for Quora is significantly lower than what you'll find in PowerAdSpy. You'll often find that the most recent or most successful ads are missing, or the filters are less granular. If you are a power user, you will hit the limits of the free and basic tiers very quickly.

Pricing is highly accessible: Free, Basic at $9/mo, Pro at $99/mo, and VIP Enterprise at $249/mo (as of April 2026). It is the lowest entry price in the category, making it a great "sampling" tool, but rarely the primary tool for a high-scale operator.

### 3. AdRecon — Irrelevant for Quora, but the gold standard for Meta affiliate offers
<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

I am including AdRecon here with a massive ** If you only need to spy on Quora, stop reading and go buy PowerAdSpy. However, most affiliate marketers aren't just running one platform. They are running a stack.

The reason AdRecon ranks in the top 3 for an "ad spy" list—even one focused on Quora—is because of how it handles the *backend* of the affiliate funnel. While PowerAdSpy tells you *what* the Quora ad looks like, AdRecon tells you *how* the top Meta affiliates are actually converting the traffic.

For example, if you find a winning Quora ad in PowerAdSpy, you'll notice it leads to a specific type of VSL (Video Sales Letter) or a quiz funnel. You can then use AdRecon to find the same offer on Meta, where the volume is 100x higher. Once you find the top-performing Meta ads for that offer, you can use AdRecon's Landing Page Ripper to download the entire HTML/CSS and asset package of the landing page as a ZIP file. 

This allows you to see the exact technical setup, the tracking pixels, and the conversion triggers being used by the biggest players on ClickBank, Digistore24, and BuyGoods. PowerAdSpy cannot do this. AdRecon's affiliate-network classification allows you to filter by the actual network, not just keywords, which is a massive time-saver.

The trade-off is the limitation: No TikTok, no YouTube, no Google, and definitely no Quora. It is a laser-focused Meta tool. It also lacks any AI or LLM features—it's a raw data engine.

### 4. AdSpy — The Meta archive king, but useless for Quora research
<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

AdSpy is the "old guard" of the industry. If you've been in affiliate marketing for a decade, you've used AdSpy. It has a 10-year historical database of Meta ads that is virtually unmatched. But for the specific purpose of Quora ads, it is a non-starter.

AdSpy focuses exclusively on Meta (Facebook and Instagram). There is no Quora coverage. The reason it remains on this list is for those who are looking for "deep historical data" to inform their Quora copy. If you want to see how a specific offer has evolved over the last five years to understand the psychological triggers that work, AdSpy's Boolean search (AND/OR/NOT) is the best in the business.

However, at $149/mo (as of April 2026), the price is steep for a tool that doesn't offer landing page archives or multi-platform support. If you are already paying for AdSpy and hoping it will help with your Quora campaigns, it won't. You'll need to supplement it with PowerAdSpy.

### 5. Foreplay — Elite creative workflow, but no Quora support
<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay is not a "spy tool" in the traditional sense; it is a creative research and briefing tool. It is designed for agencies that need to build mood boards and swipe files for clients. Like AdSpy, it has zero Quora coverage.

Where Foreplay wins is UX. It is the most beautiful and intuitive tool in the category. It allows you to save ads into organized folders and create briefs for designers. If you are running a high-end agency where you design custom assets for Meta and TikTok, Foreplay is essential.

But for the "gritty" work of CPA affiliate marketing on Quora—where you're hunting for a specific text-based angle—Foreplay provides no value. It's a tool for visual strategists, not for intent-based search marketers. Pricing starts at $49/mo (as of April 2026).

### 6. Minea — Built for dropshipping, not for Quora-based CPA
<ToolLogo slug="minea" size="lg" showName />

Minea is a powerhouse for the e-commerce and dropshipping crowd. It integrates supplier research and influencer content into its workflow, which is fantastic if you're selling a physical product from AliExpress.

However, Quora is rarely the primary driver for dropshipping; it's more often used for high-ticket CPA or lead gen. Minea's focus on TikTok and Pinterest makes it a poor fit for Quora research. It lacks the text-centric filtering you need to find winning Quora responses.

Pricing starts at €49/mo (as of April 2026). If you're a dropshipper, keep it. If you're a Quora affiliate, look elsewhere.

### 7. PiPiADS — TikTok dominance, but zero utility for Quora
<ToolLogo slug="pipiads" size="lg" showName />

PiPiADS is the undisputed king of TikTok ad spying. If you are running TikTok Shop or short-form video ads, this is your primary tool. Its integration with the TikTok Creative Center is a massive advantage.

But TikTok and Quora are polar opposites. One is high-energy, visual, and impulsive; the other is slow, text-based, and analytical. PiPiADS offers no meaningful Quora data. Including it here serves as a reminder that "best" is relative to the platform. The best TikTok tool is a useless Quora tool.

Pricing starts at $77/mo (as of April 2026).

## What we left off — and why
You'll notice we didn't include a dozen of the "white label" spy tools that pop up every month. Most of these are just wrappers for the Facebook Ad Library with a few basic filters added on top. They don't have their own databases, and they certainly don't have the infrastructure to index Quora.

We also left off general SEO tools like Ahrefs or SEMRush. While they can tell you which pages are ranking on Quora organically, they cannot show you the *paid* ads, the spend, or the specific ad creatives. For an affiliate, organic data is a hint, but ad spy data is a blueprint.
