## TL;DR
For small agencies with under 10 clients, the choice comes down to whether you prioritize creative workflow or raw data. **Foreplay** is the #1 pick for those needing a professional briefing system for clients, starting at $49/mo (as of April 2026). If you are a Meta-focused affiliate agency looking to kill monthly subscriptions, **AdRecon** is the best value with its $299 lifetime option (as of April 2026). For deep historical data, go with **AdSpy**, and for TikTok-first clients, **PiPiADS** is the only serious choice.

## Why this ranking exists
Running a small agency—specifically one with fewer than 10 clients—creates a unique tension between the need for "enterprise-grade" intelligence and a "solo-operator" budget. When you have 2 or 3 clients, you can't justify a $500/mo enterprise seat, but you also can't afford to waste your clients' ad spend on creatives that haven't been validated in the wild.

Most "Top 10" lists for ad spy tools are written by generalists who treat a dropshipper the same as a CPA affiliate or a creative agency. That is a mistake. A creative agency needs a way to present a "mood board" to a client to get sign-off on a direction. A CPA affiliate needs to know exactly which ClickBank offer is scaling and how the landing page is structured. A dropshipper needs to find a winning product on TikTok and a supplier on AliExpress.

This ranking is designed for the "small agency" tier. We are looking for tools that bridge the gap between raw data scraping and actionable client deliverables. We prioritize tools that allow you to organize research by client, validate offers quickly, and minimize the time spent on "guessing" what will work.

## How we ranked
We evaluated these tools based on eight weighted dimensions: **coverage** (which platforms are tracked), **dataFreshness** (how quickly new ads appear), **price** (TCO over 12 months), **ux** (ease of navigation), **landingPages** (ability to see/save the destination), **offers** (ability to identify the specific offer/network), **affiliateFocus** (specialized filters for CPA/CPL), and **search** (the power of the query engine).

For this specific agency angle, we weighted **UX** and **Landing Pages** higher than usual. Agencies need to move fast and present findings to clients; a tool with a dated interface or no way to archive the landing page slows down the briefing process.

 For example, we will be honest: AdRecon is Meta-only. If your agency handles TikTok or Google Ads, AdRecon cannot be your only tool. We also lack the 10-year historical depth that a veteran like AdSpy provides. We value honesty over marketing fluff because that's how operators actually make buying decisions.

## The ranking

### 1. Foreplay — The gold standard for creative organization and agency briefing
<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

For an agency with under 10 clients, the biggest bottleneck isn't usually "finding" an ad—it's organizing that ad into a coherent strategy that a client will actually approve. Foreplay solves this by moving away from the "search and find" model and toward a "save and strategize" model. It is less of a database and more of a professional swipe-file system.

The workflow for a small agency here is seamless. Instead of sending a client a messy list of links to the Meta Ad Library (which often break or disappear), you create a dedicated board for each client. You can save ads directly from your browser, and Foreplay archives them permanently. This means even if the advertiser turns the ad off, your client can still see the creative that inspired the new campaign.

A concrete workflow example: You're onboarding a new skincare client. You spend two hours on Meta and TikTok, saving 20 high-performing hooks and 10 winning CTA styles into a "Skincare Q3" board. You then use their briefing tools to annotate exactly *why* these ads work—pointing out the specific pacing of the UGC or the use of a "problem/solution" frame. You share this board with the client for a 15-minute sign-off call. No more endless back-and-forth emails with screenshots.

However, Foreplay is not a "spy" tool in the traditional sense of having a massive, searchable database of every ad ever run. It is a research and organization tool. If you need to find every ad containing the word "Keto" across the last five years, Foreplay isn't the tool for that.

Pricing starts at $49/mo for the Inspiration tier, moving up to $199/mo for the Agency tier (as of April 2026). Compared to AdRecon, Foreplay is far superior for client presentation and creative briefing, but it lacks the ability to "rip" landing pages or classify ads by affiliate network.

### 2. AdRecon — The most cost-effective stack for Meta-focused affiliate agencies
<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

The standout feature here is the Affiliate-Network Classification. While other tools make you guess if an ad is for a ClickBank or Digistore24 offer, AdRecon labels them. If you are managing clients who run offers on MaxWeb, BuyGoods, or WarriorPlus, this saves you hours of manual detective work. You can filter by network, find the "Winning Offers Directory," and see exactly which creatives are being used by the top earners in that specific niche.

Another critical workflow is the Landing Page Ripper. In the affiliate world, the ad is only 30% of the battle; the landing page (LP) is the other 70%. AdRecon allows you to download the full LP HTML, CSS, and assets as a ZIP file. For a small agency, this means you can analyze the exact conversion triggers, layout, and script placements of a competitor's page without having to manually "Save Page As" and hope the assets load.

The honest limitation: AdRecon is a specialist tool. It is Meta-only (Facebook, Instagram, Threads). It does not cover TikTok, YouTube, or Google. If your agency is a generalist shop, you will need a second tool. It also lacks the AI-assisted analysis found in Foreplay.

Pricing is the biggest differentiator here. Instead of a monthly drain on your agency's margins, AdRecon offers a lifetime deal at $299 or $599 (as of April 2026). For a small agency, removing a $149/mo subscription fee is a significant win for the bottom line.

### 3. AdSpy — The deepest historical archive for Meta research
<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

If your agency's strategy relies on "pattern recognition" over long time horizons, AdSpy is the industry standard. It boasts a 10-year historical database of Meta ads, which is significantly larger than what AdRecon or Foreplay can offer.

The power of AdSpy lies in its Boolean search. For a power user, the ability to use AND, OR, and NOT operators allows for surgical precision. For example, you can search for ads that mention "weight loss" AND "discount" but NOT "pill," allowing you to find specific angles that are currently underserved in the market.

A typical workflow in AdSpy involves "mining" for evergreen creatives. You don't just look at what's running today; you look at what has been running for 6 months straight. If an ad has been active since 2023 and is still spending, it is a mathematical certainty that it is profitable. You can then present these "evergreen" examples to your clients as a baseline for their creative strategy.

The downside is the user experience. AdSpy feels like a tool from 2015. The interface is dated, and the lack of landing page archiving is a major pain point. You'll find a great ad, click the link, and find that the advertiser has already killed the page or redirected it.

Pricing is steep for a small agency: $149/mo recurring (as of April 2026). There is no free trial, which makes it a higher-risk investment than some of the other options.

### 4. PiPiADS — Essential for agencies pivoting to TikTok-first strategies
<ToolLogo slug="pipiads" size="lg" showName />

You cannot run a modern agency without a TikTok strategy, and you cannot run a TikTok strategy effectively using the native Creative Center alone. PiPiADS is the most comprehensive TikTok ad spy tool available.

For an agency with under 10 clients, PiPiADS is used primarily for "trend spotting." TikTok moves faster than Meta. An angle that works on Monday might be dead by Friday. PiPiADS allows you to filter by "Ad Spend" (claimed) and "Engagement," helping you identify which UGC (User Generated Content) styles are actually converting.

A common workflow here is the "Creative Bridge." You find a viral TikTok ad in PiPiADS, analyze the first 3 seconds (the hook), and then use that as a blueprint to script a similar ad for your client. Because PiPiADS integrates closely with TikTok's ecosystem, you get a better sense of the "native" feel required for the platform.

The weakness is its Meta coverage. While it has some Meta data, it is a shadow of what AdSpy or AdRecon provide. It is a specialist tool; use it for TikTok, and look elsewhere for Facebook.

Pricing starts at $77/mo for the Starter plan (as of April 2026).

<ToolLogo slug="pipiads" size="lg" showName />

### 5. PowerAdSpy — The best choice for multi-channel agencies using Native ads
<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

Most agencies ignore Native advertising (Taboola, Outbrain, RevContent) because it's harder to crack than Meta. However, for clients in the health, finance, or insurance niches, Native is where the real scale happens. PowerAdSpy is the only tool in this list that provides serious coverage of these surfaces.

If your agency manages a client who wants to move beyond social media and into "advertorial" style traffic, PowerAdSpy is the only viable option. It allows you to see the exact headlines and images being used on native networks, which are fundamentally different from the "disruptive" style of social ads.

A typical workflow involves finding a high-performing Native ad, clicking through to the advertorial, and then analyzing the bridge page. While PowerAdSpy doesn't have the "ripping" capabilities of AdRecon, its breadth of platforms (including LinkedIn, Reddit, and Quora) makes it a great "generalist" tool for agencies that don't want to pay for five different subscriptions.

The trade-off is depth. Because they cover everything, they aren't as deep on any single platform as the specialists. The UX is also somewhat clunky, requiring a learning curve to master the filters.

Pricing starts at $49/mo for the Basic plan (as of April 2026).

<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

### 6. BigSpy — A low-friction entry point for agencies on a tight budget
<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

BigSpy is the "sampler" of the ad spy world. For an agency just starting out—perhaps with only 1 or 2 clients—the financial risk of a $149/mo subscription is too high. BigSpy solves this with a genuine free tier that doesn't require a credit card.

It provides a broad overview of Meta, TikTok, Pinterest, and Google Display. It's not the most powerful tool, but it's enough to get a general sense of what the competition is doing.

The workflow here is usually "surface-level research." You use BigSpy to see if a specific product category is even being advertised on a certain platform before committing to a more expensive tool. For example, if you're not sure if Pinterest is a viable channel for a client's home decor brand, you check BigSpy first.

The limitations are significant. The free tier has hard limits on searches, and the data depth is shallow. There is no affiliate-network classification or landing page archiving.

Pricing ranges from Free to $249/mo for VIP Enterprise (as of April 2026).

<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

### 7. Minea — Highly specialized for e-commerce and dropshipping clients
<ToolLogo slug="minea" size="lg" showName />

If your agency specifically serves DTC (Direct-to-Consumer) brands or dropshippers, Minea is a better fit than a general spy tool. It is built around the "product" rather than the "ad."

Minea's strength is its ability to link an ad to a supplier. For an agency helping a client launch a new physical product, Minea allows you to find winning products on TikTok and Pinterest and then research the suppliers behind them.

The workflow is product-centric: Find a trending product $\rightarrow$ Analyze the ad creative $\rightarrow$ Locate the supplier $\rightarrow$ Build a better offer. This is a completely different approach than the CPA affiliate workflow.

However, for a standard agency running lead gen or high-ticket affiliate offers, Minea is overkill in the wrong areas and underpowered in others. It lacks the professional agency briefing tools of Foreplay and the network intelligence of AdRecon.

Pricing starts at €49/mo (as of April 2026).

## Who should pick what — by use case

- **The "Creative-First" Agency:** If you spend more time in Figma and Canva than in a spreadsheet, pick **Foreplay**. The mood board and briefing workflow is unmatched.
- **The Meta CPA Specialist:** If you run ClickBank, MaxWeb, or BuyGoods offers, pick **AdRecon**. The network classification and LP ripper are built for this exact business model.
- **The TikTok Growth Agency:** If your clients are Gen Z brands and your primary surface is the For You Page, pick **PiPiADS**.
- **The "Omnichannel" Media Buyer:** If you are running a mix of Meta, Taboola, and LinkedIn for a corporate client, pick **PowerAdSpy**.
- **The Budget-Constrained Beginner:** If you have zero budget and just want to see what's out there, start with **BigSpy**.
- **The E-commerce/DTC Agency:** If you are helping clients source products and scale Shopify stores, pick **Minea**.
- **The Data Historian:** If you need to see what worked in the "Golden Age" of Facebook ads to find evergreen patterns, pick **AdSpy**.

## What we left off — and why
You will notice we didn't include "free" tools like the Meta Ad Library. While the Ad Library is a great resource, it isn't an "ad spy tool" in the professional sense. It doesn't allow for advanced filtering, it doesn't archive ads once they are turned off, and it doesn't provide intelligence on offer profitability. For an agency, relying solely on the Ad Library is like trying to do accounting with a calculator that deletes your history every 24 hours.

We also excluded several "AI-powered" spy tools that have popped up recently. Most of these are simply wrappers around existing databases with a GPT-4 prompt on top. They claim to "analyze" the ad for you, but as an operator, I don't want an LLM to tell me if an ad is winning—I want to see the days-running signal and the landing page. We prioritize raw data and workflow over AI "insights" that often hallucinate.
