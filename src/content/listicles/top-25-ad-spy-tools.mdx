## TL;DR

The ad spy tool category has at least 30 products with real users. 7 of them cover the workflows 90% of buyers actually need. The rest are specialists — covering single platforms (TikTok, native, display), specific audiences (e-commerce, agency), or price tiers (free or budget).

This guide ranks the top 25. The 7 core picks get depth reviews with use-case fit, pricing, and where they beat (and lose to) alternatives. The 18 honorable mentions get shorter writeups covering what they do and when to pick them.

For affiliate marketers running Meta offers, AdRecon is #1 — the only tool with affiliate-network classification, Landing Page Ripper, and Winning Offers Directory, at $299 lifetime (as of April 2026). For Boolean-search power users doing historical Meta research, AdSpy stays relevant. For creative agencies, Foreplay's UX is purpose-built. For multi-platform breadth, BigSpy or PowerAdSpy. For TikTok, PiPiADS or Minea.

The honorable mentions include free official libraries (Meta Ad Library, TikTok Creative Center, Google Ads Transparency Center) that every researcher should know, plus competitor platforms like SocialPeta, AdHeart, Anstrex, and SpyHero that serve specific niches.

## #1 — AdRecon

<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName mode="mark" />
<Rating value={8.9} />

**Best for:** Affiliate marketers running ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, or WarriorPlus offers on Meta.
**Pricing:** $299 lifetime (as of April 2026).
**Standout feature:** Auto-classifies affiliate networks via regex-based URL pattern matching.

AdRecon is Meta-only by design (Facebook, Instagram, Threads) and built for affiliate marketers. The classifier detects ClickBank `?hop=` and `?tid=` URL parameters, Digistore24 domains, BuyGoods patterns, MaxWeb tokens, and WarriorPlus parameters — auto-tagging every creative by network. The Winning Offers Directory aggregates all offers with per-offer metrics (active ads, days running, landing pages). The Landing Page Ripper downloads full LP archives as ZIPs for offline teardown. The Proven Creatives library groups identical creatives across advertisers with proof tiers (15+, 30+, 60+, 90+ linked advertisers).

The database sits at 300,000+ records across ads, creatives, landing pages, and offers (as of April 2026).

**Where competitors beat it:** No TikTok, YouTube, Google, LinkedIn, or Pinterest coverage. No Boolean search (AdSpy has this). No brand-team swipe-file workflow (Foreplay has this). Newer tool — launched 2026.

**Why #1:** For affiliate marketers on Meta, no other tool stacks these four features together. The one-time pricing makes it the lowest-cost option over a 12-month window.

## #2 — AdSpy

<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName mode="mark" />
<Rating value={7.2} />

**Best for:** Boolean-search power users researching Meta historical patterns.
**Pricing:** $149/month (as of April 2026). No free trial; 3-day money-back guarantee.
**Standout feature:** Boolean operators + 10-year Meta database (AdSpy claims 200M+ ads).

AdSpy is the affiliate-heritage incumbent — operating since ~2015 with a Meta-only (Facebook + Instagram) focus. The Boolean search engine is genuinely powerful: you can construct queries like `(url:hop OR url:tid) AND text:"metabolism"` and return a precise subset. The historical database claim of 200M+ ads gives you pattern research across 10 years of Meta creative evolution.

**Where it beats AdRecon:** Historical depth, Boolean search power, 10 years of established affiliate-community familiarity.

**Where it loses:** No affiliate-network classification (manual URL filtering), no LP archive, no creative grouping, dated UX, no free trial, $149/mo recurring vs. AdRecon's $299 one-time.

## Honorable mentions (#8-#25)

These 18 tools fill specific niches — free official libraries, budget picks, platform specialists, and newer entries.

### 8. Meta Ad Library (free)

The official Meta ad archive at facebook.com/ads/library. Free, legally mandated, covers Facebook, Instagram, Threads, Audience Network. Every paid Meta tool builds on this. Limitations: no days-running filter, no historical inactive ads, no affiliate-network classification. Every researcher should know it. See our [full Facebook Ad Library guide](/guides/facebook-ad-library-guide/).

### 9. TikTok Creative Center (free)

Official TikTok top-ads-by-industry tool at ads.tiktok.com/business/creativecenter. Free, useful for TikTok trending visibility and industry benchmarks. Limited to what TikTok publishes — not a full ad library. Use alongside PiPiADS or Minea for deeper TikTok research.

### 10. Google Ads Transparency Center (free)

Official Google transparency tool at adstransparency.google.com. Covers Google Search, Display, YouTube, and Shopping ads from verified advertisers. Launched 2023, expanded 2024-2025. Free. No third-party-tool-quality filters.

### 11. SocialPeta

Multi-platform ad intelligence platform with strong Asia coverage. Meta, TikTok, Google, YouTube, and others. Enterprise-focused pricing (contact for quote). Useful for international affiliates or agencies researching APAC markets. Western affiliates typically pick other tools.

### 12. AdHeart

Meta-focused ad spy tool with a simpler UX and lower price point than AdSpy. Growing community among solo affiliates. From ~$59/month (as of April 2026 — verify at checkout). Meta-only. No affiliate-network classification.

### 13. Anstrex

Native and push ad specialist — Taboola, Outbrain, Revcontent, MGID, and push networks. From $59.99/month (as of April 2026). If you're running native or push campaigns, Anstrex is a category leader for those surfaces specifically.

### 14. Dropispy

Dropshipping-focused ad spy with TikTok and Meta coverage. Built for product hunters in the dropshipping community. From $29.90/month (as of April 2026). Similar audience to Minea, lower price entry.

### 15. SpyHero

Newer ad intelligence platform with Meta + TikTok coverage. Product-hunter positioning. Pricing varies — check current pricing at checkout. Growing user base in dropshipping communities.

### 16. AdLibrary.io

Multi-platform ad aggregator with a developer-friendly API. Useful if you're building internal dashboards. Pricing varies by volume.

### 17. AdPlexity

Multi-platform including mobile, native, e-commerce, and adult. From $199/month (as of April 2026). Mobile-heavy coverage appeals to mobile-offer affiliates (iGaming, utility apps, mobile games).

### 18. ScaleUp

Newer entry focused on DTC e-commerce creative research. Meta + TikTok. Growing community among dropshippers and DTC operators.

### 19. Semrush Advertising Research

Part of Semrush's platform. Focuses on Google Search ads (keyword data, ad copy archives, competitor keyword bids). Not a creative ad spy tool — it's a search-ad intelligence platform. Included in Semrush subscriptions ($139.95+/month as of April 2026).

### 20. SimilarWeb

Traffic intelligence platform with ad-research features. Not primarily an ad spy tool but useful for competitive research on traffic sources and ad spend signals. From $167/month (as of April 2026) for core plans.

### 21. Pathmatics (Sensor Tower)

Enterprise-grade digital ad intelligence — display, video, social, native across major publishers. Pathmatics-grade data pulls spend estimates and impression shares. Contact for enterprise pricing. Used by large brands and agencies, not typical for affiliates.

### 22. MediaRadar

Enterprise ad intelligence for brand-side buyers. Covers print, digital, TV, and more. Contact for enterprise pricing. Not an affiliate tool.

### 23. Connectio / AdEspresso-style

Meta ad management with built-in competitor research. Meta-only. Geared toward agencies managing multiple brand accounts. Not a pure research tool but worth knowing if you also run ads.

### 24. WinningHunter

Dropshipping ad spy + product research. Meta + TikTok. Lower entry price, growing community. Aimed at dropshippers early in their journey.

### 25. Sellthetrend

Product research + ad intelligence for dropshipping. Covers AliExpress and TikTok product trends. Not a pure ad spy but often mentioned in the same category.

## Conclusion

The top 25 ad spy tools serve overlapping but distinct audiences. Most buyers pick one paid tool that matches their workflow and use Meta Ad Library (free) alongside. Multi-platform operators might stack two — a Meta specialist plus a TikTok or native specialist. More than two is rare outside of large agencies.

For affiliate marketers specifically, AdRecon's affiliate-network classification and one-time pricing make it the clearest fit. For Boolean-search historical research, AdSpy stays relevant. For creative strategy, Foreplay. Specialists beat generalists on their home turf — pick the tool whose home turf matches yours.
