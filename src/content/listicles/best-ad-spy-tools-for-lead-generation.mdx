## TL;DR
For most lead-gen affiliates, AdRecon is the top pick because it allows you to rip the actual landing page assets. If you need a decade of historical data, go with AdSpy; for Native ad coverage (Taboola/Outbrain), PowerAdSpy is the only viable option.

## Why this list exists
Lead generation is won or lost on the landing page. While most spy tools show you the ad creative, they leave you guessing about what happens after the click. If the advertiser kills the campaign or changes the URL, your research is gone. I've put this list together to highlight which tools actually help you reverse-engineer the entire funnel, not just the image or video.

## How we ranked
We tested these tools based on eight specific dimensions: platform coverage, data freshness, pricing structure, UX/UI, landing page archiving capabilities, offer identification, affiliate-specific focus, and search granularity. For lead gen, we weighted landing page depth and offer classification higher than general "platform count."

## The ranking

### 1. AdRecon — The only tool that lets you rip the full lead-gen funnel

<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

In lead gen, the "magic" is usually in the opt-in form and the bridge page. AdRecon wins here because of the Landing Page Ripper. Instead of just seeing a screenshot, you can download the full HTML, CSS, and assets as a ZIP file. This allows you to see exactly how a competitor is structuring their lead capture.

It also handles the "who" part of the equation. It classifies ads by affiliate networks like ClickBank, MaxWeb, and BuyGoods. If you know a specific lead-gen offer is killing it on MaxWeb, you can find it in seconds. 

<Screenshot slug="adrecon" variant="hero" />

### 2. AdSpy — The gold standard for historical Meta data

<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

If you are a power user who knows how to use Boolean search (AND, OR, NOT), AdSpy is still the heavy hitter. Their database is massive, spanning 10 years of Meta ads. For lead gen, this is useful for spotting long-term seasonal trends in B2B or insurance niches.

However, the UX feels like it's from a different era, and they don't offer landing page archiving. You get the ad, but you have to manually visit the link and hope it's still live. The pricing is also a recurring sting at $149/mo (as of April 2026), which adds up quickly for solo operators.

<Screenshot slug="adspy" variant="hero" />

### 3. PowerAdSpy — Best for Native and Multi-Platform lead gen

<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

Lead gen isn't just about Facebook. If you're running high-ticket B2B leads, you're likely looking at Taboola, Outbrain, or LinkedIn. PowerAdSpy is the only tool in this group with serious Native coverage.

The depth per platform is thinner than a specialist tool, and the interface is clunky. But if your strategy involves a "surround sound" approach—hitting a lead on Meta and then retargeting them on a Native network—this is the right choice. Pricing ranges from $49/mo to $249/mo (as of April 2026).

<Screenshot slug="poweradspy" variant="hero" />

### 4. BigSpy — A low-cost entry point for generalists

<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

BigSpy is where you start if you don't want to drop $150+ a month immediately. They have a legitimate free tier that doesn't require a credit card, which is rare in this industry.

It covers a lot of ground (TikTok, Meta, Google Display), but it's shallow. You won't find the same level of affiliate-network classification or landing page depth here. It's a sampling tool. If you find a winning angle here, you'll likely move to a specialist tool to refine it. Prices range from Free to $249/mo (as of April 2026).

### 5. Foreplay — High-end creative organization for agencies

<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay isn't really a "spy" tool in the traditional sense; it's a workflow tool. It's designed for agencies that need to build mood boards and creative briefs for clients. 

The UX is the best in the category, and their AI-assisted analysis helps you break down why a lead-gen creative is working. But for a CPA affiliate, it lacks the "meat"—there's no landing page ripping and no network classification. It's for the strategist, not the operator. Plans run from $49/mo to $199/mo (as of April 2026).

## How to pick
If you are a solo affiliate running Meta lead-gen on the major networks, the ability to rip landing pages makes AdRecon the logical choice. If you are a media buyer running diversified traffic across Native and Social, PowerAdSpy is the only one that covers the necessary surfaces. For those who need deep historical archives for research, AdSpy remains the industry standard.
