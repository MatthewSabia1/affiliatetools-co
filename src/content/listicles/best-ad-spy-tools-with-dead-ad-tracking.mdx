## TL;DR
For most affiliate operators, the "best" tool is the one that separates noise from signal by identifying ads that have actually scaled. **AdRecon** is the top pick for Meta affiliates due to its specific affiliate-network classification and "days-running" profitability signals, available for a one-time payment of $299 or $599 (as of April 2026). Power users needing deep historical archives should stick with **AdSpy**, while TikTok-first operators require **PiPiADS**.

## Why this ranking exists
In the world of affiliate marketing, the biggest waste of budget isn't bad creative—it's copying "new" ads that never actually scaled. Most ad spy tools act as massive libraries; they show you everything that was ever uploaded. However, seeing 1,000 ads for a weight loss offer is useless if 990 of them were turned off after spending $20 because they didn't convert.

This is where "dead ad tracking" (or active-status tracking) becomes the primary lever for profitability. A professional operator doesn't look for "pretty" ads; they look for ads that have been running for 30, 60, or 90 days. If a media buyer is spending their own money on a creative for two months straight, that creative is printing money.

The problem is that the "most popular" spy tools often bury this data or ignore it entirely. They focus on volume rather than velocity and longevity. For a CPA affiliate, knowing an ad is "active" is a baseline requirement, but knowing *how long* it has been active and which affiliate network (ClickBank, MaxWeb, etc.) it's tied to is the real edge. This ranking prioritizes tools that treat "run-time" as a first-class citizen.

## How we ranked
We evaluated these tools through the lens of a senior media buyer. We didn't care about "AI-generated summaries" or "pretty dashboards." We cared about the ability to find a winning offer, verify its longevity, and rip the funnel. We scored each tool across eight weighted dimensions:

1. **Coverage:** The breadth of platforms and geographic regions.
2. **Data Freshness:** How quickly new ads appear and how accurately "active" status is updated.
3. **Price:** Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) over a 3-year horizon.
4. **UX:** The speed of the search-to-result workflow.
5. **Landing Pages:** Whether the tool archives the actual HTML/CSS of the destination page.
6. **Offers:** The ability to filter by specific affiliate networks.
7. **Affiliate Focus:** Features specifically for CPA/CPL marketers versus DTC brands.
8. **Search:** The precision of the filtering system (Boolean, keyword, etc.).

For this specific "dead ad tracking" angle, we weighted **Data Freshness**, **Offers**, and **Landing Pages** the highest. If a tool tells you an ad is active but doesn't let you see the landing page it's driving to, the intelligence is incomplete. If it shows you a "winning" ad but doesn't tell you it's from Digistore24, you spend too much time guessing the offer.

## The ranking

### 1. AdRecon — The affiliate marketer's Meta-only ad intelligence stack
<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

The core differentiator here is the "days-running" signal. In a standard spy tool, you see an ad and assume it's working. In AdRecon, you can filter for ads that have been running for 30+ days. This immediately strips away the "test" ads and leaves you with the winners. When you combine this with their affiliate-network classification, the workflow becomes lethal. You can filter for "MaxWeb" + "Health" + "Running 45+ days." This gives you a shortlist of creatives that are mathematically proven to convert for a specific network.

Another critical component is the Landing Page Ripper. Most tools provide a screenshot or a live link. The problem is that by the time you decide to model the page, the advertiser has often killed the ad and the page is a 404. AdRecon allows you to download the full LP HTML, CSS, and assets as a ZIP file. This turns "spying" into "deployment," as you can host the mirrored page on your own server in minutes.

**Workflow Example 1: The "Proven Winner" Hunt**
An operator wants to enter the Keto niche on ClickBank. Instead of searching "Keto" and scrolling through thousands of random ads, they filter by the ClickBank network classification and set the "days running" filter to 21 days. They identify three ads that have survived for nearly a month. They use the Landing Page Ripper to download the exact VSL (Video Sales Letter) page and the opt-in sequence, then tweak the copy for their own angle.

The trade-off is the scope. AdRecon does not cover TikTok, Google, or Native. It has no AI features because operators don't need a chatbot; they need raw data. It is a specialist tool. If you are running a multi-channel agency, this is a supplement, not a replacement.

Pricing is a major disruptor here. Instead of the industry-standard monthly drain, AdRecon offers lifetime licenses at $299 or $599 (as of April 2026), which drastically lowers the overhead for solo affiliates.

### 2. AdSpy — The 10-year Meta ad archive with Boolean search
<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

AdSpy is the "old guard" of the industry. Its primary value proposition is the sheer volume of its archive. While newer tools focus on the *now*, AdSpy allows you to look back a decade. This is invaluable for identifying seasonal trends—for example, seeing exactly which "tax season" or "New Year's resolution" ads worked in 2018, 2020, and 2022 to predict 2026.

The powerhouse feature here is the Boolean search. Most spy tools use simple keyword matching. AdSpy allows for complex logic (AND, OR, NOT), which lets you carve out very specific niches. If you want to find ads that mention "weight loss" AND "coffee" but NOT "Keto," AdSpy handles that with precision.

However, from a "dead ad tracking" perspective, AdSpy is a blunt instrument. It tells you if an ad has been seen, but it lacks the granular affiliate-network classification found in AdRecon. You have to manually hunt for affiliate links in the destination URLs to figure out if an ad is using ClickBank or BuyGoods. Furthermore, AdSpy does not archive landing pages. If the advertiser deletes the page, it's gone. You are left with a screenshot of the ad and a dead link.

**Workflow Example: Historical Trend Analysis**
An operator wants to launch a summer-themed offer. They use AdSpy to search for the top-performing creatives from July of the last three years. By analyzing the common visual elements of ads that stayed active during those periods, they build a creative brief based on historical winners rather than current guesses.

**Workflow Example: Competitor Mapping**
An operator finds a successful competitor's Facebook Page. They use AdSpy to pull every ad that competitor has ever run. By sorting by date, they can see the evolution of the competitor's funnel—how they changed their hooks and which images they eventually stopped using (the "dead" ads).

The pricing is the main friction point. At $149/mo (as of April 2026), it is a significant recurring expense. For a solo affiliate, the annual cost ($1,788) is nearly three times the cost of a lifetime license for a specialized tool.

### 3. PiPiADS — The TikTok ad specialist
<ToolLogo slug="pipiads" size="lg" showName />

If your traffic source is TikTok, PiPiADS is non-negotiable. TikTok's ad ecosystem is fundamentally different from Meta's; the lifespan of a creative is shorter, and the "vibe" is more organic. PiPiADS is the only tool that effectively tracks the "dead vs active" cycle on TikTok at scale.

The tool integrates directly with the TikTok Creative Center, giving you a glimpse into what the platform itself considers "top performing." For an affiliate, the value lies in the ability to filter by "Ad Spend" (estimated) and "Date Range." While it doesn't have the same network-specific classification as AdRecon, its ability to track the viral trajectory of a TikTok ad is unmatched.

The "dead ad" logic on TikTok is different. An ad that runs for 14 days on TikTok is often equivalent to an ad that runs for 45 days on Meta. PiPiADS allows you to spot these "micro-winners" before the rest of the market saturates the creative.

**Workflow Example: The Viral Hook Rip**
An operator sees a TikTok ad for a gadget that has been active for 10 days and has a high engagement rate. They use PiPiADS to find similar ads from other advertisers. They identify a common "hook" (the first 3 seconds of the video) that is used across all winning ads in that category. They then film their own version of that hook.

**Workflow Example: Product Validation**
Before committing to a new offer, an operator searches PiPiADS for the product name. If they see 50 different advertisers all running ads that have been active for 2+ weeks, they know the product is currently "hot" and the market is receptive.

The limitation is the Meta coverage. PiPiADS has some Meta data, but it's a secondary feature. If you are a Meta-first affiliate, using PiPiADS as your primary tool will leave you blind to the largest ad surface on the web. Pricing starts at $77/mo (as of April 2026), which is reasonable for the depth of TikTok data provided.

### 4. BigSpy — Multi-platform ad spy with a real free tier
<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

BigSpy is the "Swiss Army Knife" of the category. It doesn't do any one thing as deeply as the specialists, but it covers almost everything. It's the only tool in this list that provides a legitimate free tier (no credit card required), making it the default starting point for beginners.

For dead ad tracking, BigSpy is useful for "cross-platform validation." If you see an ad that is active on Meta, TikTok, and Pinterest simultaneously, you've found a massive winner. The ability to see a campaign's footprint across multiple surfaces is a strong signal of profitability.

However, BigSpy is shallow. It lacks landing page archiving and affiliate-network classification. You get the "what" (the ad) but not the "how" (the funnel) or the "who" (the network). It's a research tool, not an execution tool.

Pricing is the most accessible in the industry, ranging from Free to $249/mo (as of April 2026), which allows users to scale their plan as their budget grows.

### 5. PowerAdSpy — Broadest multi-platform coverage, including Native
<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

PowerAdSpy is designed for the media buyer who doesn't want to be locked into a single platform. Its standout feature is its coverage of Native ad networks like Taboola, Outbrain, and RevContent.

For the affiliate, Native ads are where the "big money" often hides, especially for health and finance offers. PowerAdSpy allows you to see which Native ads are staying active. Because Native ads often have a different "burn rate" than social ads, having a tool that can track them alongside Meta and YouTube is a huge advantage.

The downside is the UX. The interface feels dated, and the search process can be clunky compared to the streamlined experience of AdRecon or Foreplay. Like BigSpy, it lacks the specific "affiliate-network" filters that save hours of manual research.

Pricing ranges from $49/mo to $249/mo (as of April 2026). It's the best pick for those running a diversified traffic strategy.

### 6. Foreplay — Creative research + brief workflow for agencies
<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay is not a "spy tool" in the traditional sense of mining a database for dead ads. Instead, it is a workflow tool for creative strategists. It allows you to save ads into "boards" and create briefs for designers.

If you are an agency owner, Foreplay is superior because it solves the "client communication" problem. You can build a mood board of active winning ads and send it to a client or a creative team. It also includes AI-assisted analysis to help break down why a certain ad is working.

However, for a solo affiliate looking for "dead ad tracking" to find a winning offer, Foreplay is overkill and under-powered. It doesn't provide the raw data mining or the landing page ripping capabilities needed for fast CPA deployment.

Pricing starts at $49/mo (as of April 2026), positioning it as a professional service tool rather than a solo operator's utility.

### 7. Minea — Multi-platform ad spy tuned for dropshipping
<ToolLogo slug="minea" size="lg" showName />

Minea is an excellent tool, but it's built for a different animal: the dropshipper. Its entire workflow is optimized for finding "winning products" and connecting them to suppliers.

It has great TikTok and Pinterest coverage, which is where most DTC (Direct-to-Consumer) brands live. But for a CPA affiliate running offers from MaxWeb or BuyGoods, Minea's features are misaligned. It doesn't care about affiliate networks; it cares about product margins and shipping times.

If you are doing dropshipping, Minea is a top-three choice. If you are doing CPA affiliate marketing, it's a distant seventh.

Pricing starts at €49/mo (as of April 2026).
