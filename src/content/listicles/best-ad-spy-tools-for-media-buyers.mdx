## How we ranked these for media buyers

Media buyers make launch decisions with money on the line. The tool they use has to answer one question efficiently: "Is this creative worth testing?" Everything else — swipe files, collaborative workflows, creative inspiration — is secondary to that judgment call.

The signals that actually inform that decision:

- **Days running.** A creative live 30+, 60+, 120+ days on Meta is vastly stronger signal than a week-old ad. Meta's auction pauses unprofitable creatives fast.
- **Cross-advertiser replication.** If 20+ advertisers are running the same creative, it's likely converting.
- **Offer context.** Knowing the offer type (ClickBank Health VSL vs. DTC supplement vs. quiz-funnel lead-gen) changes how you interpret ad data.
- **Landing page structure.** The ad is half the funnel. Without the LP, you can't estimate CVR.
- **Network context.** For performance and affiliate media buyers, knowing which offer network matters.

We weighted:

- **Days-running + profitability signals (30% weight)** — Primary media-buyer input.
- **Creative grouping + proof tiers (20% weight)** — Cross-advertiser validation.
- **Landing-page + funnel research (20% weight)** — Half the funnel lives below the ad.
- **Offer + network intelligence (15% weight)** — Relevant for performance buyers.
- **Speed of use (15% weight)** — Filter response, search latency, export speed.

## #1 — AdRecon

<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName mode="mark" />
<Rating value={9.1} />

**Best for:** Meta media buyers running affiliate, CPA, or performance campaigns.
**Pricing:** $299 lifetime (as of April 2026). $599 tier available.
**Standout feature:** 4-pillar stack with days-running + proof-tier creative grouping across advertisers.

AdRecon is built around the signals media buyers actually use, which is why it wins this list. Every pillar maps to a media-buying decision:

- **Ad Feed sorted by days running.** Default sort is days-running ascending (newest first), with descending as the primary profitability filter. Over 120 days on Meta = strong profitability signal. Over 60 days = worth testing.
- **Proven Creatives.** Identical creatives grouped across advertisers with proof tiers — 15+, 30+, 60+, and 90+ linked ads. A 60-advertiser cluster is much stronger signal than a single ad, because 60 advertisers running the same creative isn't coincidence.
- **Winning Lander Vault.** Landing pages grouped by the Meta ads linking to them, with days-running rollups per lander. If one LP has 5 different Meta ads running over 90 days, that's a proven funnel.
- **Winning Offers Directory.** Every affiliate offer tracked across Meta ads, with ad count, active-ad count, days running, and network / niche classification. For performance media buyers, this is offer-landscape intelligence in one view.
- **Landing Page Ripper.** Download a complete landing page (HTML, CSS, assets) as ZIP. Rate limit: 10 rips / 15 min per user. Max 100MB per capture. 110s timeout. SSRF-protected.
- **Dead creative tracking.** `is_active` flag surfaces creatives that stopped running after long lifetimes — rare signal that only works with ongoing crawling.

The classifiers are regex-based URL pattern matching — not AI, not ML. That's a feature for media buyers: deterministic classification means predictable behavior, not black-box confidence scores.

**Where AdRecon loses points for media buyers:** Meta-only. No TikTok (use PiPiADS alongside). No agency-style collaborative workflow. Smaller historical archive than AdSpy.

**Why #1 for media buyers:** The signal quality. Days-running, cross-advertiser proof tiers, and landing-page archive are the exact signals media buyers need before launching a test.

## #2 — AdSpy

<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName mode="mark" />
<Rating value={7.4} />

**Best for:** Senior Meta media buyers running Boolean research workflows.
**Pricing:** $149/month (as of April 2026). 3-day money-back guarantee per AdSpy's terms. No free trial.
**Standout feature:** Boolean operators + claimed 200M+ Meta ads in the archive.

AdSpy fits the analyst-style media buyer who thinks in Boolean queries. A query like `("limited time" OR "sale ends") AND country:US AND days_running:>90 AND platform:instagram` is more efficient than clicking through filters, and AdSpy is the only tool on this list that supports it.

The second asset is historical depth. AdSpy's archive spans roughly a decade of Meta creatives. For pattern research — how have winning weight-loss VSL angles evolved from 2017 to 2024 — that history is genuinely useful.

**Where AdSpy loses points for media buyers:** Dated UI. No free trial. No affiliate-network classification. No landing-page archive. No cross-advertiser creative grouping. $149/month vs. AdRecon's $299 lifetime is weaker math for long-term Meta buyers.

**When to pick AdSpy over AdRecon:** Boolean query workflow + historical research heavy.
