## TL;DR
For most affiliate marketers, **AdRecon** is the top pick for Threads ads due to its affiliate-network classification and lifetime pricing ($299 / $599 as of April 2026). If you need a decade of historical data and Boolean search, **AdSpy** remains the industry heavy-hitter. ## Why this ranking exists
Threads has introduced a new variable into the Meta ad ecosystem. For the average media buyer, it's just another placement toggle in Ads Manager. But for the senior affiliate operator, Threads represents a shift in user psychology. It is a conversational, text-heavy environment where the "hard sell" often fails, and "native-feeling" curiosity gaps win.

The problem with most "Top 10" lists is that they treat all ad spy tools as equal. They aren't. A tool built for a dropshipper looking for a winning gadget on TikTok is useless to a CPA affiliate trying to find a high-converting health offer on Threads. The latter needs to know not just *what* the ad looks like, but *who* is running it, *which* network it's tied to (ClickBank, MaxWeb, etc.), and *how* the landing page is structured to handle the specific traffic flow from a text-based app.

We created this ranking to separate the generalists from the specialists. We are looking for tools that don't just "show ads" but provide actionable intelligence—specifically the ability to identify proven offers and rip the underlying assets so you can iterate faster.

## How we ranked
We evaluated these tools based on eight weighted dimensions: **Coverage** (does it actually see Threads ads?), **Data Freshness** (are the ads from today or last month?), **Price** (TCO over 12 months), **UX** (how fast can I find a winner?), **Landing Pages** (can I see/save the destination?), **Offers** (can it identify the affiliate network?), **Affiliate Focus** (is it built for CPA/CPL or DTC?), and **Search** (how granular are the filters?).

For this specific "Threads Ads" angle, we weighted **Coverage**, **Offers**, and **Landing Pages** the highest. Because Threads is a newer surface, the ability to quickly identify which affiliate networks are successfully scaling there—and how their landing pages are optimized for that traffic—is the only metric that actually moves the needle for an operator.

### 1. AdRecon — The affiliate marketer's Meta-only ad intelligence stack
<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

AdRecon is built for a very specific person: the solo affiliate who lives and breathes Meta traffic and doesn't care about TikTok or Google. When it comes to Threads, AdRecon doesn't just scrape the ad; it classifies it. If a competitor is running a Threads ad for a weight loss offer, AdRecon can tell you immediately if that offer is coming from ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, or WarriorPlus. This removes the guesswork and the need to manually click through ten different redirect links to find the source.

A typical workflow in AdRecon for Threads looks like this: First, you use the "Winning Offers Directory" to see which products are currently scaling across the Meta ecosystem. Second, you filter by "Days Running" to find ads that have been active for 14+ days—a reliable signal that the advertiser is profitable. Once you find a Threads ad that fits your criteria, you use the "Landing Page Ripper." Instead of just viewing the page in a browser, you download the entire HTML/CSS and asset package as a ZIP file. This allows you to analyze the conversion triggers and load speeds locally.

The biggest limitation of AdRecon is its narrow scope. It is strictly Meta-only. If your strategy requires cross-referencing Threads ads with TikTok trends or Google Search intent, AdRecon cannot do that. It also lacks the AI-driven creative analysis found in tools like Foreplay. It's a raw data tool for operators, not a creative suite for agencies.

Pricing is a one-time lifetime fee of $299 or $599 (as of April 2026), which is a massive advantage for operators who are tired of the "subscription tax" eating into their daily margins.

Compared to AdSpy (the #2 pick), AdRecon has a much smaller historical database because it launched in 2026. You won't find ads from 2018 here. However, AdRecon wins on the "last mile" of affiliate marketing: network classification and landing page archiving, both of which AdSpy lacks.

### 2. AdSpy — The 10-year Meta ad archive with Boolean search
<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

AdSpy is the "old guard" of the industry. Its primary strength is the sheer volume of its archive. While AdRecon focuses on the *now* and the *how*, AdSpy focuses on the *everything*. If you want to see how a specific affiliate's strategy has evolved over the last five years across Facebook and Instagram (and by extension, Threads), this is the tool.

The standout feature here is the Boolean search. For power users, the ability to use AND, OR, and NOT operators is a game-changer. For example, you can search for ads that mention "Keto" AND "Discount" but NOT "Free Shipping." This level of granular filtering allows you to carve out very specific niches within the Threads ad surface that other tools might miss.

A common workflow for an AdSpy user is "Historical Pattern Mapping." You find a current winner on Threads, then search for that same advertiser's history from three years ago to see what their early iterations looked like. This gives you a blueprint of their evolution and helps you predict where they are going next.

However, AdSpy feels dated. The UX is clunky, and it lacks the modern affiliate-specific features that today's operators need. Most notably, there is no landing page archiving. If an advertiser kills their page, that data is gone forever. There is also no affiliate-network classification; you are essentially guessing the network based on the URL structure.

Pricing is a recurring $149/mo (as of April 2026). Over a year, that's $1,788—significantly more expensive than the lifetime options.

AdSpy beats AdRecon in one major area: depth of history. If you need to see the "ancestors" of a current Threads trend, AdSpy is the only choice. But for the actual execution of a campaign today, the lack of LP ripping makes it a less complete solution.

### 3. BigSpy — Multi-platform ad spy with a real free tier
<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

The most appealing part of BigSpy is the free tier. Unlike many "free trials" that require a credit card and then charge you $100 the moment you forget to cancel, BigSpy has a legitimate free version (as of April 2026). This makes it the default choice for beginners who are just starting to explore Threads ads.

A typical workflow in BigSpy involves "Cross-Platform Validation." You find a high-engagement ad on Threads, then immediately switch the platform filter to TikTok to see if the same creative is being used there. If it's winning on both, you've found a "universal winner," which significantly lowers the risk of your campaign.

The trade-off for this breadth is depth. BigSpy's data on any single platform is "shallower" than the specialists. You won't find as many niche affiliate ads, and like AdSpy, it lacks affiliate-network classification and landing page archiving. The free tier also has hard limits on searches, which will quickly push you toward the Basic ($9/mo) or Pro ($99/mo) plans.

Compared to the top two, BigSpy is a generalist tool. It's great for the "discovery" phase, but once you've identified your offer and need to rip the landing page and analyze the network, you'll find yourself wanting the specialized features of AdRecon.

### 4. PowerAdSpy — Broadest multi-platform coverage, including Native
<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

PowerAdSpy is the tool for the "Omni-channel" media buyer. While we are focusing on Threads, many senior operators don't just run Meta; they run Native ads on Taboola, Outbrain, and RevContent to scale their winners. PowerAdSpy is the only tool in this list that effectively bridges the gap between social (Threads/Meta) and Native.

The workflow here is "The Scale Ladder." You start by spying on Threads to find a conversational hook that works. Once you have a winning angle, you use PowerAdSpy to see how that same offer is being framed on Native platforms. Native ads require a different "advertorial" style, and PowerAdSpy allows you to see those high-converting pre-landers that are often hidden from social-only spy tools.

The weaknesses are similar to BigSpy: the UX is dated, and the depth per platform is thinner than a specialist tool. There is no affiliate-network classification, meaning you'll spend a lot of time manually auditing URLs.

Pricing ranges from $49/mo to $249/mo (as of April 2026). It's a professional tool for those who have already moved past the "solo affiliate" stage and are managing larger, multi-surface budgets.

### 5. Foreplay — Creative research + brief workflow for agencies
<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay isn't a "spy tool" in the traditional sense—it's a creative intelligence platform. If you are an agency owner managing Threads ads for five different clients, you don't need a database of a million ads; you need a way to organize the ten ads that actually matter.

Foreplay's "Swipe Files" and mood boards are the best in the business. Instead of just seeing an ad, you can save it into a categorized folder and share it with your design team. Their AI-assisted creative analysis can help you break down *why* a Threads ad is working (e.g., "strong curiosity gap in the first sentence") and turn that into a creative brief.

The workflow is "Brief-to-Execution." You curate a mood board of winning Threads ads, use the AI to extract the core psychological triggers, and then hand that brief to a content creator.

The downside for the pure affiliate is the lack of "operator" features. There is no landing page archiving and no affiliate-network classification. It's a tool for the *creative* process, not the *technical* process of affiliate marketing.

Pricing starts at $49/mo for the Inspiration plan and goes up to $199/mo for Agency (as of April 2026).

### 6. Minea — Multi-platform ad spy tuned for dropshipping
<ToolLogo slug="minea" size="lg" showName />

Minea is a powerhouse for e-commerce, but it's a bit of a square peg in a round hole for CPA affiliates. It covers Threads via the Meta integration, but the entire UX is geared toward product research.

If you are a dropshipper using Threads to drive traffic to a Shopify store, Minea is excellent. It helps you find winning products and even suggests suppliers. However, if you are promoting a digital offer or a lead-gen form on MaxWeb, Minea's "product-first" filters will actually get in your way.

The workflow here is "Product Hunting." You search for "trending products" and see which ones are gaining traction on Threads. If a specific gadget is exploding, you can find the supplier and launch your own store.

For the CPA affiliate, the lack of network classification and the focus on DTC (Direct-to-Consumer) make it less useful than AdRecon or AdSpy.

Pricing is in Euros, ranging from €49/mo to €399/mo (as of April 2026).

### 7. PiPiADS — The TikTok ad specialist
<ToolLogo slug="pipiads" size="lg" showName />

PiPiADS is the undisputed king of TikTok. While it has some Meta/Threads coverage, it's an afterthought compared to its TikTok database. Why is it on this list? Because of "Creative Porting."

Many of the most successful Threads ads are simply TikTok winners that have been adapted for a text-centric environment. A senior operator will use PiPiADS to find a "viral" TikTok creative, then go to Threads to see if anyone has successfully ported that angle to the new surface.

The workflow is "TikTok $\rightarrow$ Threads." You find a high-converting UGC (User Generated Content) video on PiPiADS, analyze the hook, and then rewrite that hook as a Threads post to see if it converts.

The weakness is obvious: it's terrible for pure Threads research. If you aren't also running TikTok, there is no reason to pay for PiPiADS.

Pricing is $77/mo to $155/mo (as of April 2026).

## What we left off — and why
You'll notice we didn't include "free" Chrome extensions or manual "Ad Library" scraping. While the Meta Ad Library is free, it's a nightmare for research. It lacks filtering, doesn't archive landing pages, and doesn't tell you if an ad has been running for three days or three months. For a professional operator, the time spent manually scrolling the Ad Library is more expensive than the cost of a spy tool.

We also omitted general SEO tools that claim "ad spying" capabilities. Those tools are designed for organic search and lack the real-time scraping and affiliate-specific filters required to win on a fast-moving surface like Threads.
