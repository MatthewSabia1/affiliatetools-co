## TL;DR
For Meta-focused Nutra affiliates, AdRecon is the top choice due to its network classification and landing page ripper. If you need a decade of historical data and complex search queries, go with AdSpy. For those scaling into Native ads, PowerAdSpy is the logical move.

## Why this list exists
Health and Nutra offers are some of the hardest to run on Meta. Between the strict compliance rules and the high turnover of landing pages, you can't just "guess" what works. You need to know exactly which offers are scaling on networks like ClickBank or MaxWeb and, more importantly, you need to see the full funnel before the page gets banned.

## How we ranked
We didn't just look at feature lists. We tested these tools using a senior operator's workflow, scoring them across eight dimensions: platform coverage, data freshness, pricing structure, UX/UI, landing page archiving capabilities, offer identification, affiliate-specific focus, and search granularity.

## The ranking

### 1. AdRecon — The only tool that classifies health offers by affiliate network

<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

When you're hunting for health offers, the biggest pain point is finding the ad but not knowing where the offer actually lives. AdRecon solves this by classifying ads by network—whether it's ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, or MaxWeb. This removes the guesswork and lets you go straight to the affiliate manager.

The real killer feature here is the Landing Page Ripper. In the health niche, pre-landers disappear overnight. AdRecon lets you download the full LP as a ZIP file (HTML/CSS/assets), so you have the blueprint even after the original is gone. It's a Meta-only tool, and since it launched in 2026, it doesn't have the decade-long archive that AdSpy boasts. 
Pricing is a one-time fee of $299 or $599 for lifetime access (as of April 2026), which is a breath of fresh air compared to the "subscription tax" most tools charge.

<Screenshot slug="adrecon" variant="hero" />

### 2. AdSpy — Unmatched historical depth for long-term health trends

<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

If you need to see what health angles worked three years ago to spot a returning trend, AdSpy is the industry standard. Their database is massive, and their Boolean search (AND/OR/NOT) is far more powerful than any other tool on this list. You can get incredibly surgical with your keywords to find specific health niches.

However, AdSpy is starting to feel its age. The UX is dated, and they completely lack landing page archiving and affiliate-network classification. You'll find the ad, but you'll have to manually hunt for the offer. At $149/mo (as of April 2026), it's a steep recurring cost for a tool that doesn't help you with the funnel side of the equation.

<Screenshot slug="adspy" variant="hero" />

### 3. PowerAdSpy — Best for scaling health offers into Native traffic

<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

Many health affiliates start on Meta but move to Native (Taboola, Outbrain, RevContent) once they have a winning angle. PowerAdSpy is the best choice for this hybrid approach. While their Meta data is thinner than a specialist's, their Native coverage is the broadest in the category.

The trade-off is depth. You won't get the same level of granularity or "winning signal" accuracy as you do with a Meta-specialist tool. The interface is also a bit clunky. Pricing ranges from $49/mo to $249/mo (as of April 2026) depending on the tier.

<Screenshot slug="poweradspy" variant="hero" />

### 4. BigSpy — A low-cost entry point for sampling health creatives

<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

BigSpy is where you go if you're just starting out and don't want to drop hundreds of dollars. They offer a real free tier that doesn't require a credit card, which is rare in this space. It's a great way to get a feel for what health creatives look like across Meta, TikTok, and Pinterest.

The downside is that the free and basic tiers have hard limits that will frustrate a professional operator. Like PowerAdSpy, it's a generalist tool; it doesn't understand affiliate networks or archive landing pages. Prices range from Free to $249/mo (as of April 2026).

### 5. Foreplay — The gold standard for organizing creative mood boards

<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay isn't a "spy tool" in the traditional sense—it's a creative research and workflow tool. If you have a team of designers building high-production health ads, Foreplay is essential. Their ability to save ads into mood boards and create creative briefs is the best in the business.

For a solo Nutra affiliate, it might be overkill because it lacks the "intelligence" side—no network classification, no LP archiving, and less historical depth than AdSpy. It's about the *process* of creation, not the *discovery* of offers. Pricing ranges from $49/mo to $199/mo (as of April 2026).
