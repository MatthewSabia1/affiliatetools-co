## Why this ranking exists
The fitness and supplement vertical is one of the most aggressive spaces in affiliate marketing. Between strict compliance rules on Meta and the sheer volume of "body transformation" clones, the difference between a winning campaign and a banned ad account often comes down to the nuance of the landing page and the specific angle of the creative. 

Most "top 10" lists for ad spy tools are written by SEO writers who have never actually launched a campaign. They suggest generalist tools that show you a million ads but don't tell you which network the offer belongs to or how to actually replicate the funnel. For a fitness affiliate, seeing a "weight loss" ad is useless unless you know if it's a ClickBank offer, a private CPA deal, or a direct-to-consumer brand.

We created this ranking to separate the "generalist" tools from the "operator" tools. In the fitness niche, you don't need a tool that covers 15 platforms poorly; you need a tool that allows you to find a winning supplement offer on Meta, rip the landing page, and analyze how many days it has been running to confirm profitability.

## The ranking

### 1. AdRecon — The affiliate marketer's Meta-only ad intelligence stack
<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

AdRecon is built specifically for the CPA affiliate who doesn't care about "brand awareness" or "agency mood boards" and only cares about finding offers that convert. In the fitness vertical, this is a game-changer because of the **Affiliate-Network Classification**. Instead of searching for "weight loss" and sifting through thousands of organic DTC brands, you can filter specifically for ads running on ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, or WarriorPlus. This immediately isolates the "money-making" affiliate funnels from the corporate brand ads.

For a fitness operator, the workflow looks like this: First, you navigate to the Winning Offers Directory to see which fitness supplements are currently trending across the major networks. Second, once you find a high-converting offer, you use the "Proven Creatives" grouping to see every single ad variation different affiliates are using for that specific product. This allows you to see the common denominators—whether they are using "before and after" images (risky) or "weird food trick" hooks (safer).

The most powerful feature for fitness marketers is the **Landing Page Ripper**. Most spy tools just give you a URL that might be dead by the time you click it. AdRecon allows you to download the entire landing page—HTML, CSS, and assets—as a ZIP file. If you find a fitness VSL that is crushing it, you can have the exact structure on your own server in seconds. Additionally, the "days-running" signal tells you exactly how long an ad has been active. In the volatile fitness niche, if an ad has been running for 20+ days without being banned, it's a massive signal of both compliance and profitability.

The honest limitation here is the scope. AdRecon is Meta-only. It does not cover TikTok, YouTube, or Google. If your fitness strategy relies on TikTok Shorts or Google Search, you'll need a secondary tool. It also lacks the massive 10-year archive that AdSpy possesses; it is a newer tool (launched 2026) and focuses on current, actionable data rather than historical archives.

Pricing is a one-time payment: $299 for a standard license or $599 for a lifetime license (as of April 2026). Compared to AdSpy’s monthly subscription, the TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) is significantly lower for a solo operator.

### 2. AdSpy — The 10-year Meta ad archive with Boolean search
<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

AdSpy is the "old guard" of Meta spying, and for a specific type of fitness researcher, it is still the best. Its primary advantage is the sheer volume of its database. While AdRecon focuses on the "now," AdSpy allows you to go back years. This is invaluable for fitness marketers who want to study the evolution of "weight loss" hooks over a decade to see which angles are timeless and which are fads.

The standout feature here is the **Boolean search**. You can use operators like AND, OR, and NOT to surgically isolate ads. For example, you can search for "Keto AND supplement NOT 'free trial'" to find high-intent purchase ads while filtering out the low-quality lead-gen offers. This level of search granularity is something AdRecon does not currently offer.

However, for the modern affiliate, AdSpy has some glaring gaps. It has no landing page archiving. You can see the ad, but you have to manually click the link and hope the affiliate hasn't turned off the page or blocked your IP. There is also no network classification; you won't know if an ad is a ClickBank offer unless you recognize the URL or the product. The UX is dated, feeling more like a database from 2015 than a modern SaaS tool.

Pricing is steep: $149/mo recurring (as of April 2026). For a solo fitness affiliate, paying nearly $1,800 a year just to see creatives is a hard pill to swallow when lifetime alternatives exist. But if you are a power user who needs to know exactly what worked in the fitness niche in 2019 to predict what will work in 2026, AdSpy is the only tool with that depth.

### 3. PowerAdSpy — Broadest multi-platform coverage, including Native
<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

If you are a fitness marketer who has graduated from Meta and is now scaling into Native advertising, PowerAdSpy is the necessary choice. Fitness supplements are a staple of Native platforms like Taboola, Outbrain, and RevContent. These platforms allow for the "advertorial" style of marketing—long-form articles that lead into a supplement offer—which often converts better than a direct Meta ad.

PowerAdSpy is one of the few tools that effectively covers these Native surfaces. The workflow here is different: you search for "weight loss" or "belly fat" and filter by "Native" to see which advertorials are getting the most traction. You can then see which headlines are winning on Taboola and mirror those on your own Native campaigns. 

Beyond Native, it covers almost everything—YouTube, Google Display, Reddit, and LinkedIn. This makes it a "Swiss Army Knife." However, the trade-off is depth. While it covers more platforms, it isn't as deep on any single one as the specialists. It lacks the network classification of AdRecon and the historical depth of AdSpy. The UX is also somewhat clunky, requiring a learning curve to find the most useful filters.

Pricing varies by tier: Basic $49/mo, Standard $99/mo, Professional $149/mo, and Premium $249/mo (as of April 2026). It's a subscription model, which adds to your monthly overhead, but for those running $10k+/month in Native spend, the intelligence it provides on Taboola/Outbrain is worth the cost.

### 4. PiPiADS — The TikTok ad specialist
<ToolLogo slug="pipiads" size="lg" showName />

Fitness is a visual, high-energy niche, which makes TikTok a goldmine for "body transformation" and "fitness hack" offers. PiPiADS is the undisputed leader for TikTok spying. While other tools have "TikTok coverage," PiPiADS is built from the ground up for this platform.

For fitness affiliates, the value is in the **TikTok Creative Center integration**. You can find the exact trending sounds and visual hooks that are driving sales for supplement offers. The workflow is simple: find a viral fitness product, see the top-performing ads, and analyze the "hook-rate" (the first 3 seconds of the video). 

The weakness is that it's very narrow. It's weak on Meta and completely useless for Native or Search. It also lacks any affiliate-network classification or landing page ripping. You are paying for the TikTok data and nothing else.

Pricing starts at $77/mo for the Starter plan and goes up to $155/mo for VIP (as of April 2026). If your fitness offer is a "viral" product intended for Gen Z or Millennials, this is your tool.

Foreplay isn't really a "spy tool" in the traditional sense—it's a creative management tool. For fitness agencies that manage multiple supplement brands, Foreplay is the best for organizing "swipe files." Instead of just searching for ads, you save them into mood boards and create creative briefs for your designers.

The UX is the best in the category. It feels like a modern design tool rather than a database. If you're building a fitness brand and need to present "creative directions" to a client, Foreplay is the tool you use. However, for a solo affiliate, it's overkill. It doesn't help you find the offer or rip the landing page; it only helps you organize the ads you've already found.

Pricing starts at $49/mo for Inspiration, $99/mo for Strategist, and $199/mo for Agency (as of April 2026).

### 7. Minea — Multi-platform ad spy tuned for dropshipping
<ToolLogo slug="minea" size="lg" showName />

Minea is a powerhouse for dropshippers. If your "fitness offer" is actually a physical product you're sourcing from AliExpress—like a waist trainer or a massage gun—Minea is excellent. It integrates supplier research directly into the ad spying workflow.

But for CPA affiliates running ClickBank or Digistore24 fitness offers, Minea is a poor fit. It's built for DTC (Direct-to-Consumer) e-commerce, not affiliate marketing. It lacks network classification and landing page archiving. You'll find plenty of "fitness gadgets," but you won't find the high-converting VSL funnels that make CPA fitness marketing profitable.

Pricing starts at €49/mo for Starter, €99/mo for Premium, and €399/mo for Business (as of April 2026).
