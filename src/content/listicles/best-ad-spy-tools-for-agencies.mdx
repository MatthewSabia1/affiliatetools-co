## How we ranked these for agencies

Agencies have a different research workflow than solo affiliate marketers, media buyers, or DTC operators. Agency workflows typically include:

- A creative strategist researching and saving references into swipe files
- A brief writer composing briefs with embedded references
- A designer or editor executing on those briefs
- A media buyer or account manager reviewing competitor landscape
- A client-facing export or presentation

The tool that fits that workflow is different from the tool that fits a solo CPA affiliate. Foreplay was built for creative teams — swipe files, briefs, folder-sharing — and that's why it wins this list. AdRecon was built for affiliate research, and it's the right tool for performance agencies specifically (where the brief is an offer teardown, not a brand moodboard).

Our weighting for this list:

- **Collaborative workflow (30% weight)** — Swipe files, briefs, folder sharing, comments, multi-seat.
- **Platform coverage (25% weight)** — Agencies typically serve clients across Meta, TikTok, and adjacent platforms.
- **Creative handling (20% weight)** — Video preservation, format fidelity, cross-advertiser grouping.
- **Cost per seat (15% weight)** — Agencies scale seats, so per-seat economics matter.
- **Export + reporting (10% weight)** — Ability to feed outputs into client-facing decks.

## #1 — Foreplay

<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" />
<Rating value={8.6} />

**Best for:** Agencies running creative strategy and production across multiple clients.
**Pricing:** $49/month Inspiration, $99/month Agency, higher tiers for teams (as of April 2026). Free trial available.
**Standout feature:** Swipe file + brief builder + folder-sharing workflow built for creative teams.

Foreplay is the default agency pick in 2026 for good reason. The product is designed around the actual workflow creative strategists use — see an ad, save it to a folder, write a brief that references the folder, share the brief with the designer, iterate. No other tool in this category replicates that workflow with the same polish.

Specific features that matter for agencies:

- **Swipe files with folders and tags.** Organize references by client, campaign, vertical, or hypothesis.
- **Brief builder.** Write briefs with embedded creative references — no more screenshots dropped into Google Docs.
- **Comment threads.** Creative strategists and designers discuss specific references inline.
- **Multi-seat tiers.** Agency tier supports more seats at per-seat pricing vs. per-user flat rate.
- **Cross-platform coverage.** Foreplay sources from the Meta Ad Library plus TikTok Creative Center — the two platforms agencies ship to most.

**Where Foreplay loses points:** Smaller Meta historical index than AdSpy. No affiliate-network classification. No offer aggregation. No landing-page ripper. The product is creative-first — not built for affiliate research, offer teardowns, or CPA workflows.

**When to pick Foreplay:** Default pick for brand, DTC, and creative agencies. Not the right pick for performance-affiliate shops — see AdRecon below.

## #2 — Minea

<ToolLogo slug="minea" size="lg" />
<Rating value={7.6} />

**Best for:** E-commerce and DTC-focused creative agencies.
**Pricing:** From EUR 49/month Starter, Premium EUR 99/month, Business EUR 399/month (as of April 2026). Limited free surface.
**Standout feature:** Multi-platform product research with influencer ad coverage.

Minea is the e-commerce agency's specialist pick. The tool covers Meta, TikTok, Pinterest, and influencer ads in one subscription — the exact platform mix most DTC agencies serve. The product-research framing (which product is winning, where is it sold, who's running it) fits DTC campaign briefs better than pure ad-database framing.

For agencies with Shopify clients, supplement-brand clients, or dropshipping-adjacent accounts, Minea is often a better daily driver than Foreplay specifically because the data is framed around products, not just ads.

**Where Minea loses points:** Not affiliate-network-aware (irrelevant for most DTC agencies, dealbreaker for performance agencies). Less collaborative workflow than Foreplay. EU-focused defaults. Higher-tier pricing escalates faster than Foreplay.

**When to pick Minea over Foreplay:** E-commerce / DTC / dropshipping agencies with product-first client portfolios.

## #3 — AdSpy

<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" />
<Rating value={7.2} />

**Best for:** Senior creative strategists who work in Boolean queries.
**Pricing:** $149/month (as of April 2026). 3-day money-back guarantee. No free trial.
**Standout feature:** Boolean operators + claimed 200M+ ads in a decade-long Meta archive.

AdSpy fits a specific agency persona — the senior creative strategist or research director who thinks in Boolean queries and uses ad research as an analyst tool. For that workflow, AdSpy's Boolean search and historical archive are genuinely differentiated. Running a query like `("personalized" OR "customized") AND country:US AND platform:instagram AND day_running:>60` returns a structured result set that Foreplay can't replicate.

**Where AdSpy loses points for agencies:** Dated UI. No collaborative workflow — it's a single-analyst tool by design. No multi-seat collaboration. No brief builder. No swipe file in the Foreplay sense.

**When to pick AdSpy for an agency:** As a secondary research tool alongside Foreplay, used by the research lead. Not as the primary tool for the whole team.

## How to choose for your agency

Pick by client portfolio:

- **Brand / creative agencies with DTC clients** → Foreplay. Standard pick.
- **E-commerce / DTC / dropshipping-focused agencies** → Minea.
- **Performance / CPA-affiliate agencies on Meta** → AdRecon.
- **Multi-surface agencies (Meta + Native + Display + YouTube)** → PowerAdSpy or stack specialists.
- **Research-heavy senior strategists needing Boolean queries** → AdSpy as secondary.
- **Freelance creative strategist or 1-2 person shop** → BigSpy free tier or Foreplay Inspiration.

Common agency stacks:

- **Foreplay + Motion** — Creative research plus ad performance reporting. Standard creative agency stack.
- **Foreplay + PiPiADS** — Meta creative workflow plus TikTok depth. Agencies running both platforms.
- **Minea + Foreplay** — DTC product research plus agency workflow. E-commerce shops.
- **AdRecon + Foreplay** — Affiliate research plus creative workflow. Hybrid performance-and-creative shops.

## Our verdict

For most agencies — brand, DTC, creative — Foreplay is the right default. The swipe-file and brief-builder workflow is built for creative teams, and no other tool in this category matches its collaborative UX.

For e-commerce and DTC agencies specifically, Minea is often a better daily driver because of the product-research framing. For performance and CPA-affiliate agencies on Meta, AdRecon is the specialist pick. AdSpy fits senior research strategists as a secondary tool. PowerAdSpy fits multi-surface consolidation. BigSpy fits freelancers and pre-funded boutiques.

The biggest mistake agencies make in this category is buying one tool and expecting it to serve both creative workflow and affiliate research. Those are different jobs — pay for both if your work spans both.
