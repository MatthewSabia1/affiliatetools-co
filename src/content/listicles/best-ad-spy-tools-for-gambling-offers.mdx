## TL;DR
For gambling and iGaming affiliates, the choice comes down to your traffic source and your need for funnel data. If you are running Meta and need to clone winning funnels, **AdRecon** is the top pick due to its Landing Page Ripper and lifetime pricing ($299 / $599 as of April 2026). For those scaling via Native ads (Taboola/Outbrain), **PowerAdSpy** is the only viable option. If you need 10 years of historical Meta data to find "evergreen" casino creatives, **AdSpy** remains the industry standard.

## Why this ranking exists
Gambling is one of the most volatile verticals in affiliate marketing. Between strict platform policies, constant account bans, and heavy geo-restrictions, the "standard" way of spying on ads doesn't work. Most generalist tools show you the creative, but in iGaming, the creative is only 20% of the battle. The real magic happens in the bridge page—the pre-lander that warms up the user and bypasses the ad network's compliance bots.

The naive approach is to pick the tool with the "most ads." But for a gambling operator, 1 million random e-commerce ads are useless. You need to know which offers are running on specific networks like MaxWeb or BuyGoods, and you need to see the exact HTML of the landing page that survived the review process.

Furthermore, gambling traffic is fragmented. A winning "Crash Game" offer on TikTok looks nothing like a "Sportsbook Bonus" offer on Taboola. This ranking exists to categorize tools not by their general popularity, but by their utility in the specific, high-stakes environment of iGaming and sportsbook promotion.

## How we ranked
We evaluated these tools using a weighted scoring system across eight dimensions: coverage, dataFreshness, price, ux, landingPages, offers, affiliateFocus, and search. For the gambling vertical, we weighted **landingPages** and **affiliateFocus** the highest. In a vertical where accounts are banned daily, the ability to archive a landing page before it disappears is a critical competitive advantage.

## The ranking

### 1. AdRecon — The best choice for Meta-focused iGaming affiliates who need to rip landing pages.
<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

In the gambling vertical, the "bridge page" is everything. If you've ever had a Meta account disabled within ten minutes of launching a casino offer, you know that the secret isn't just the image—it's the pre-lander. AdRecon is built specifically for this workflow. While other tools show you a screenshot of the ad, AdRecon's Landing Page Ripper allows you to download the full HTML, CSS, and assets of the destination page as a ZIP file. For an iGaming affiliate, this means you can analyze the exact compliance triggers, the layout of the "Wheel of Fortune" game, or the specific wording of the T&Cs that allowed the ad to pass review.

Consider this workflow: You search for "Casino" or "Slots" and filter by "Days Running." You find an ad that has been live for 45 days. In the gambling world, that is an eternity. That ad is printing money. Instead of trying to manually recreate the funnel, you use the Landing Page Ripper to pull the entire site. You can then tweak the copy, swap the affiliate link, and deploy a proven funnel in minutes.

Another powerful workflow is the Affiliate-Network Classification. AdRecon identifies ads running offers from networks like MaxWeb, BuyGoods, and ClickBank. Instead of guessing if a creative is for a private offer or a public network, you can filter specifically for MaxWeb gambling offers to see what the top players in that specific ecosystem are running right now.

Pricing is a one-time fee: $299 for a standard license or $599 for a lifetime professional license (as of April 2026). Compared to AdSpy's recurring monthly cost, this is a massive TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) advantage for solo operators.

### 2. PowerAdSpy — Essential for gambling operators who need Native coverage.
<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

While Meta is great for volume, the "big money" in gambling often moves to Native advertising. Platforms like Taboola, Outbrain, and RevContent allow for more aggressive gambling creatives that would be banned instantly on Facebook. PowerAdSpy is the strongest tool in this category for the gambling vertical because it covers these Native surfaces alongside the standard social platforms.

For a sportsbook affiliate, the workflow here is different. You aren't looking for "viral" content; you're looking for "advertorials." You use PowerAdSpy to find high-performing native ads that lead to long-form articles (e.g., "5 Reasons Why This New Casino is Paying Out More"). By spying on the Native side, you can see the exact headlines that are driving high-intent traffic to gambling offers—headlines that often use a more "news-style" approach than social media ads.

Another workflow involves cross-platform validation. You might find a winning creative on Facebook via AdRecon, then jump into PowerAdSpy to see if that same offer is being scaled on Taboola. If an offer is winning on both social and native, it's a signal that the offer itself is incredibly strong, regardless of the traffic source.

The downside to PowerAdSpy is the depth of data. Because they try to cover everything—from LinkedIn to Reddit to Native—they aren't as deep on any single platform as a specialist tool. The UX also feels dated, and they lack the specific affiliate-network classification that makes AdRecon faster for CPA marketers.

Pricing starts at $49/mo for the Basic plan, scaling up to $249/mo for Premium (as of April 2026). It is a subscription model, which adds up over time, but for those who cannot survive without Native data, it is a necessary expense.

### 3. AdSpy — The gold standard for deep historical research.
<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

If you are looking for "evergreen" gambling creatives—the types of ads that work year after year—AdSpy is the only tool with the historical depth to provide those answers. With a database spanning 10 years of Meta ads, AdSpy allows you to see the evolution of gambling creatives. You can see how "Online Casino" ads looked in 2018 versus 2026, which helps you identify patterns that consistently convert across different market cycles.

The standout feature here is the Boolean search. For a gambling operator, this is a superpower. You can run a search like `(Casino OR Slots) NOT "Free Spins"` to find high-intent ads that aren't relying on the standard "freebie" hook. This allows you to find more sophisticated angles that your competitors, who are just using basic keyword searches, will completely miss.

A typical workflow in AdSpy involves "pattern mining." You search for a specific gambling keyword and filter by the oldest ads that are still running. This reveals the "immortal" creatives—ads that have survived multiple algorithm updates and policy shifts.

The weaknesses are significant for the modern affiliate. AdSpy has no landing page archiving. You can see the ad, but once the ad is gone or the link is dead, the funnel is lost. There is also no affiliate-network classification; you are searching by keywords and patterns, not by network. The pricing is also steep at $149/mo recurring (as of April 2026), which is a hard pill to swallow when lifetime alternatives exist.

### 4. PiPiADS — The only serious choice for TikTok gambling.
<ToolLogo slug="pipiads" size="lg" showName />

Gambling on TikTok is a completely different beast. It's all about UGC (User Generated Content), "big win" reactions, and fast-paced editing. AdSpy and AdRecon won't show you this. PiPiADS is the specialist here. If you are targeting Gen Z or Millennials with "Crash" games or sports betting apps, this is where you start.

The workflow in PiPiADS centers on the TikTok Creative Center integration. You can find the fastest-growing gambling ads in specific geos (like Brazil or India) and see exactly which hashtags and sounds are trending. Because TikTok is so trend-driven, the "days-running" signal is less important than the "velocity" signal—how quickly is this ad gaining views?

The limitation is that PiPiADS is very narrow. It is great for TikTok, but its Meta coverage is weak. It also lacks landing page archiving and affiliate-network classification. You are spying on the creative, but you're still guessing at the funnel.

Pricing starts at $77/mo for the Starter plan (as of April 2026).

### 5. BigSpy — A low-cost entry point for sampling.
<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

BigSpy is the "sampler" tool. It doesn't have the depth of AdSpy or the utility of AdRecon, but it has a real free tier that doesn't require a credit card. For a beginner who wants to see what gambling ads look like across Meta, TikTok, and Pinterest without spending $150, BigSpy is the place to start.

The workflow here is broad exploration. You can quickly toggle between platforms to see if a specific gambling hook (e.g., "Win your first bet") is working across different surfaces. It's a great tool for "vibe checking" a market before committing to a more expensive, specialized tool.

The trade-off is the hard limits on the free and basic tiers. You will hit a wall quickly. Like most generalist tools, it lacks landing page archiving and network classification.

Pricing is the most accessible in the category: Free, Basic $9/mo, Pro $99/mo, and VIP $249/mo (as of April 2026).

### 6. Foreplay — Best for agency-level creative briefs.
<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay isn't a "spy tool" in the traditional sense—it's a creative workflow tool. If you are an agency managing multiple iGaming clients, you don't just need to find ads; you need to organize them into mood boards and briefs for your designers.

The workflow in Foreplay involves creating "swipe files." When you find a high-performing gambling ad, you save it into a board. You can then use their AI-assisted analysis to break down why the ad works (e.g., "Strong hook in first 3 seconds," "Clear CTA"). This makes the hand-off to a video editor much smoother.

The weakness for affiliates is the lack of "hard data." There is no landing page ripping and no affiliate-network classification. It's a tool for designers and strategists, not for the "rip-and-run" CPA affiliate.

Pricing starts at $49/mo for the Inspiration plan (as of April 2026).
