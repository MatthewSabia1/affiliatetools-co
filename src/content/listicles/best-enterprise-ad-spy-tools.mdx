## TL;DR
For agencies requiring total market coverage across Native and Social, PowerAdSpy is the top choice. Creative-heavy teams should go with Foreplay, while CPA-focused Meta operators will find the most utility in AdRecon.

## Why this list exists
Most ad spy reviews are written for the "get rich quick" beginner. Enterprise operators and agency owners have different problems: they need multi-seat collaboration, cross-platform data to inform omnichannel strategies, and ways to organize thousands of creatives without losing their minds. You aren't looking for a "magic button"; you're looking for a reliable data pipeline.

## How we ranked
We evaluated these platforms based on eight specific dimensions: platform coverage, data freshness, pricing structures, user experience (UX), landing page archiving capabilities, offer identification, affiliate-specific focus, and search granularity.

## The ranking

### 1. PowerAdSpy — The most comprehensive platform coverage for multi-channel agencies

<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

If your agency manages budgets across multiple surfaces, PowerAdSpy is the logical starting point. Unlike the specialists, they've cast a wide net. They cover the basics (Facebook, Instagram) but add the heavy hitters for enterprise media buying: Google Display, YouTube, and most importantly, Native networks like Taboola and Outbrain.

The ability to see what's working on Native is a massive advantage for high-ticket affiliate offers where Meta might be too restrictive. However, the trade-off for this breadth is depth. You won't find the same level of granular Meta-specific intelligence here as you would in a dedicated Meta tool.

Pricing for the Professional and Premium tiers ranges from $149/mo to $249/mo (as of April 2026).

<Screenshot slug="poweradspy" variant="hero" />

### 2. Foreplay — The gold standard for creative strategy and agency workflow

<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay isn't a "spy tool" in the traditional sense of scraping a database for keywords. It is a workflow tool. For agencies, the value is in the "Swipe File" and the ability to save ads permanently before they are deleted or paused by the advertiser.

Their UX is the best in the category, making it easy to build mood boards for clients and creative briefs for designers. The limitation is that they don't provide the deep historical data or landing page archives that a hard-core media buyer needs.

The Agency tier is priced at $199/mo (as of April 2026).

<Screenshot slug="foreplay" variant="hero" />

### 3. BigSpy — Broad multi-platform reach with a scalable enterprise tier

<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

BigSpy is a solid generalist tool. They cover Meta, TikTok, Twitter/X, and Pinterest. For an enterprise team that needs a "bird's eye view" of the global landscape across different geographies, BigSpy provides a reliable, if slightly shallow, data set.

The VIP Enterprise tier is designed for those who need higher limits and broader access. That said, if you are doing deep CPA affiliate work, you'll find the lack of affiliate-network classification frustrating.

The VIP Enterprise tier costs $249/mo (as of April 2026).

### 4. AdRecon — High-efficiency Meta intelligence for CPA-focused mid-market teams

<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

Where we win for mid-market teams is the "Affiliate Stack." We classify ads by network (ClickBank, MaxWeb, etc.) and provide a Landing Page Ripper that lets you download the full HTML/CSS of a winning page. This is a feature the larger enterprise tools typically ignore. We also don't have AI features—just raw, filtered data.

For teams that live and die by Meta, the $599 lifetime plan (as of April 2026) is a significant cost-saver over monthly subscriptions.

### 5. AdSpy — The deepest historical Meta archive for data-heavy research

<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

AdSpy is the legacy giant. If you need to know what was working on Meta in 2018 to find a pattern for a 2026 campaign, this is the tool. Their Boolean search (AND/OR/NOT) is still the most powerful way to filter through millions of ads.

The downside is the UX, which feels like a relic of the last decade, and the lack of landing page archives. You can see the ad, but you can't easily "rip" the funnel.

The recurring cost is $149/mo (as of April 2026).

## How to pick
Your choice depends on your primary traffic source. If you are an omnichannel agency running Native and Search, PowerAdSpy is the only real option. If you are a creative shop focusing on aesthetics and briefs, Foreplay is the winner. If you are a CPA affiliate focused exclusively on Meta and want to clone winning funnels, AdRecon is the most efficient path.
