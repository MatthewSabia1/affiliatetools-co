## TL;DR

Competitor ad research differs from general ad spy in one important way: the unit of analysis is an advertiser, not an ad. You're tracking specific brands or affiliate operators over time — what they run, when they launch new creative, how their offers evolve, and what their funnel architecture looks like.

For this workflow, you want tools that support per-advertiser browsing, historical retention (so you can see what a competitor ran 6 months ago), new-creative alerts, and landing page aggregation by advertiser.

AdRecon, AdSpy, and Foreplay are the three depth picks for Meta-focused competitor tracking. AdRecon adds affiliate-network and offer context. AdSpy adds 10-year history. Foreplay adds alerting and brief workflow. BigSpy, PowerAdSpy, Minea, and PiPiADS fill specific gaps — multi-platform, multi-surface, e-commerce, and TikTok respectively.

Meta Ad Library (free) remains the raw source every tool builds on. Bookmark Page Transparency pages of key competitors; layer a paid tool on top for historical tracking, alerts, and network/offer context.

This list ranks the top 7 tools by competitor-research fit, covers the workflow for each, and closes with a how-to-choose section.

## How we ranked for competitor research

- **Per-advertiser workflow** (30%) — Saved competitor lists, per-advertiser browsing, historical retention.
- **Creative change detection** (25%) — Alerts, recently-active filters, new-ad surfaces.
- **Historical depth** (20%) — How far back the tool retains competitor ads.
- **Platform coverage** (15%) — Where the competitor research lives.
- **Ancillary context** (10%) — Offer classification, LP aggregation, product data.

## #1 — AdRecon

<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName mode="mark" />
<Rating value={8.7} />

**Best for:** Tracking Meta affiliate competitors and their offers over time.
**Pricing:** $299 lifetime (as of April 2026).
**Standout feature:** Per-advertiser and per-offer views with days-running sort and network classification.

AdRecon's 4-pillar stack natively supports competitor research. The Ad Feed filters to specific advertisers, sorts by days running, and retains ads historically (including dead creatives that stopped running). The Winning Offers Directory aggregates by offer — track how a competitor's offers evolve, which ones they double down on, which ones they pause. The Winning Lander Vault groups landing pages by advertiser or offer. Network classification (ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, WarriorPlus) adds context to what kind of offers a competitor runs.

For a ClickBank affiliate tracking 10-20 competing affiliates in the Health niche, AdRecon is a complete workflow — track each competitor's current ads, historical ads, offers promoted, LPs deployed, and creative-refresh cadence.

**Where competitors beat it:** No TikTok coverage. No explicit alerting (Foreplay has this). No Boolean search (AdSpy has this). Newer tool with less historical depth than AdSpy.

**Why #1 for Meta affiliate competitor research:** Combines per-advertiser, per-offer, per-LP views with network context at a one-time price.

<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName mode="mark" />
<Rating value={7.4} />

**Best for:** Advanced Boolean queries tracking competitor historical patterns.
**Pricing:** $149/month (as of April 2026).

AdSpy is the competitor-research choice when historical depth matters. The 10-year Meta database (AdSpy claims 200M+ ads) lets you study a competitor's ad history across years. Boolean search supports complex queries like `advertiser:"competitor-name" AND days_running:>90 AND text:"testimonial"`.

## Conclusion

Competitor ad research is an advertiser-first workflow. Pick the tool whose advertiser browsing, historical retention, and ancillary context (offer classification, LP aggregation, product data) match the competitors you're actually tracking.

For Meta affiliates tracking other affiliates, AdRecon's per-advertiser + per-offer + per-LP views with affiliate-network classification stack together well. For brand-side agencies tracking brand competitors across Meta and TikTok, Foreplay's competitor library and alerts fit better. For budget or multi-platform needs, BigSpy or PowerAdSpy. For e-commerce, Minea. For TikTok, PiPiADS.
