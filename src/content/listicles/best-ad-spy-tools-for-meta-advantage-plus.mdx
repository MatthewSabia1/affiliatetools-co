## TL;DR
For the modern affiliate running Meta Advantage+, the goal is finding "proven" creatives that the algorithm loves. AdRecon is our top pick for CPA operators due to its affiliate-network classification and Landing Page Ripper, available for a one-time payment of $299 or $599 (as of April 2026). If you need 10 years of historical data, go with AdSpy ($149/mo). For agency-level creative briefs, Foreplay is the winner.

## Why this ranking exists
Meta Advantage+ (and ASC) has fundamentally changed how we do research. In the old days, we looked for specific targeting hacks. Now, the algorithm handles the targeting; the only lever the operator has left is the creative. If the creative doesn't resonate, the Advantage+ campaign fails. This means "creative research" is no longer about finding a cool image—it's about finding the specific hooks, angles, and landing page flows that are currently scaling.

The problem is that most "popular" ad spy tools are built for general e-commerce or broad agency use. They give you a million ads but no way to tell if the ad is promoting a ClickBank offer, a Digistore24 product, or a private CPA deal. For a professional affiliate, 99% of the data in a general spy tool is noise. You don't care about a local dentist's ad in Ohio; you care about the VSL (Video Sales Letter) that's been running for 45 days on a weight-loss offer.

This ranking exists to separate the "generalist" tools from the "operator" tools. We are looking for software that doesn't just show us an ad, but tells us *why* it's winning and gives us the assets to iterate on it quickly.

## How we ranked
We evaluated these tools based on eight weighted dimensions: **coverage** (which platforms are tracked), **dataFreshness** (how quickly new ads appear), **price** (TCO over 24 months), **ux** (speed of workflow), **landingPages** (ability to archive/download), **offers** (classification of affiliate networks), **affiliateFocus** (features built for CPA), and **search** (precision of filters).

For this specific angle—Meta Advantage+ research—we weighted **landingPages**, **offers**, and **dataFreshness** the highest. In an Advantage+ environment, the landing page is half the battle, and knowing the offer source allows you to find the exact affiliate network to join. 

 We aren't here to pretend AdRecon is the best at everything; it isn't. For example, if you need TikTok data or a 10-year archive, AdRecon is the wrong tool. We value honesty over marketing copy because that's how operators actually make decisions.

## The ranking

### 1. AdRecon — The most precise stack for CPA affiliates running Meta Advantage+
<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

The standout feature here is the affiliate-network classification. AdRecon doesn't just scrape ads; it identifies if an ad is linked to ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, or WarriorPlus. This is a massive shortcut. Instead of guessing if a product is an affiliate offer, you can filter by network and immediately see what the top earners in your specific niche are running.

**Workflow Example 1: The "Winning Offer" Hunt**
An operator wants to enter the health niche on ClickBank. Instead of browsing generic "health" keywords, they filter by "Network: ClickBank" and "Days Running: 30+." This instantly surfaces the ads that have survived the Meta algorithm's pruning process. Because the ad has been running for a month, it's a high-probability winner.

One honest limitation: AdRecon is a newer tool (launched 2026) and does not have the deep historical archive that AdSpy possesses. If you need to see what worked in 2018, you won't find it here. Additionally, there are no AI or LLM features; it is a pure data tool for operators who prefer to do their own analysis.

Pricing is a one-time lifetime fee of $299 or $599 (as of April 2026), which eliminates the "subscription fatigue" that plagues this industry. Compared to AdSpy, which costs $149 every single month, AdRecon pays for itself in less than four months.

### 2. AdSpy — The gold standard for deep historical Meta research
<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

AdSpy is the "old guard" of Meta spying. Its primary strength is its sheer volume of data. While AdRecon focuses on the current "winning" window, AdSpy offers a 10-year historical database. This is invaluable for operators who want to study the evolution of a niche or find "forgotten" angles that worked years ago and might be ripe for a comeback.

The real power of AdSpy lies in its Boolean search operators. You can use AND, OR, and NOT logic to carve out incredibly specific segments of the market. For example, you can search for ads that contain "weight loss" AND "limited time" but NOT "keto." This level of search precision is something AdRecon does not offer, making AdSpy the better choice for power users who treat ad research like a data science project.

**Workflow Example 1: Historical Angle Analysis**
An operator wants to see how "skin care" hooks have changed over the last five years. They use AdSpy's date filters to compare 2021 creatives against 2026 creatives. They notice a shift from "clinical" imagery to "UGC/lo-fi" video, allowing them to pivot their Advantage+ creative strategy to match current trends.

**Workflow Example 2: Competitor Exclusion**
Using the NOT operator, an operator can filter out the top 10 biggest players in their niche to find the "hidden gems"—smaller affiliates who are scaling quietly without the massive brand recognition of the industry leaders.

The downside is the pricing and the UX. At $149/mo (as of April 2026), it is one of the most expensive options on this list, especially since it's a recurring subscription. The interface also feels dated, and there is no landing page archiving or affiliate-network classification. You get the ad, but you have to manually hunt for the landing page and the offer source.

### 3. Foreplay — The best workflow for creative briefs and mood boards
<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay isn't a "spy tool" in the traditional sense of database scraping; it's a creative intelligence platform. While AdSpy and AdRecon are about *finding* the ad, Foreplay is about *organizing* the ad for production. It is designed for agencies and high-volume creative strategists who need to hand off briefs to editors.

The core value is the "Swipe File" and mood board functionality. Instead of sending a link to a client or editor (which might disappear if the ad is turned off), Foreplay saves the ad and its metadata permanently. Their UX is the best in the category—clean, fast, and intuitive. They also include AI-assisted creative analysis to help break down why a specific video is performing.

**Workflow Example 1: The Creative Brief**
A strategist finds five winning Advantage+ ads. They save them into a Foreplay board, tag them by "Hook," "Body," and "CTA," and then share that board directly with their video editor. The editor now has a visual blueprint of exactly what to produce.

**Workflow Example 2: Mood Boarding for New Niches**
When entering a new vertical, a user creates a mood board of the top 20 aesthetic styles currently working on Meta and TikTok. This ensures the creative team doesn't produce "outdated" looking content that the Advantage+ algorithm would ignore.

The limitation for CPA affiliates is the lack of "operator" features. There is no landing page ripper and no affiliate-network classification. It is a tool for the *creative* side of the house, not the *technical* side of the affiliate game. Pricing ranges from $49/mo to $199/mo (as of April 2026).

### 4. BigSpy — A solid, low-cost entry point for multi-platform research
<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

BigSpy is the "generalist" option. If you aren't sure if your offer will work better on Meta, TikTok, or Pinterest, BigSpy is the logical starting point. It covers a wide array of platforms and, most importantly, offers a real free tier (no credit card required), which is almost unheard of in this space.

For Advantage+ research, BigSpy is useful for "cross-pollination." You can see what's working on TikTok and then attempt to replicate those lo-fi styles on Meta. However, because it spreads itself across so many platforms, it lacks the depth of a specialist tool. You won't find the deep affiliate-network filters of AdRecon or the Boolean precision of AdSpy.

Pricing is very accessible, starting with a Free tier and going up to a VIP Enterprise plan at $249/mo (as of April 2026). It's the best choice for beginners who are still sampling different platforms before committing to a high-ticket specialist tool.

### 5. PowerAdSpy — The best choice if your Meta strategy includes Native ads
<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

PowerAdSpy is the only tool on this list that provides significant coverage for Native ad networks like Taboola, Outbrain, and RevContent. For many senior affiliates, Meta Advantage+ is only one part of the funnel; they often use Native ads to drive traffic to a pre-lander before sending it to the offer.

If you are running a "Native-to-Meta" retargeting strategy, PowerAdSpy is the only tool that lets you spy on both ends of the funnel. You can see what hooks are working on Taboola and then find similar patterns on Meta to optimize your Advantage+ campaigns.

The downside is a dated UX and a lack of affiliate-specific classification. Like BigSpy, it is a "mile wide and an inch deep." You get a lot of platforms, but you don't get the specialized "operator" tools like landing page archiving. Pricing ranges from $49/mo to $249/mo (as of April 2026).

### 6. Minea — Strong for product-led Meta ads, but tailored for e-commerce
<ToolLogo slug="minea" size="lg" showName />

Minea is built for the dropshipper. While it does an excellent job of spying on Meta ads, its entire workflow is centered around "products." It helps you find a winning product, find a supplier, and then see the ads promoting it.

For a CPA affiliate, Minea is less useful because it doesn't understand the concept of an "offer" or a "network." It understands "products." If you are selling a physical supplement via a CPA network, Minea can help you find the creative, but it won't help you find the affiliate program or the landing page structure.

That said, for those who blend dropshipping with affiliate marketing, Minea's influencer integration is a huge plus. Pricing is in Euros, ranging from €49/mo to €399/mo (as of April 2026).

### 7. PiPiADS — The TikTok specialist with secondary Meta capabilities
<ToolLogo slug="pipiads" size="lg" showName />

PiPiADS is the undisputed king of TikTok spying. While they do offer some Meta coverage, it is secondary to their TikTok database. The reason to include them here is that Advantage+ campaigns on Meta often perform best when they use "TikTok-style" content.

An operator will use PiPiADS to find a viral TikTok ad, then take that exact creative (or a modified version) and plug it into a Meta Advantage+ campaign. It's a "cross-platform" arbitrage strategy. However, if you are looking for a dedicated Meta research tool, PiPiADS is the wrong choice. It lacks landing page archiving and affiliate classification.

Pricing is $77/mo for the Starter plan and $155/mo for the VIP plan (as of April 2026).

## Who should pick what — by use case

**The Solo ClickBank/Digistore Affiliate**
Pick **AdRecon**. The network classification and Landing Page Ripper are specifically designed for this workflow, and the lifetime price removes monthly overhead.

**The Boolean Search Power User**
Pick **AdSpy**. If you need to run complex queries and search through a decade of Meta data, no other tool has the database depth.

**The Agency Creative Director**
Pick **Foreplay**. The ability to create mood boards and shareable briefs with editors makes it the only real choice for a team environment.

**The "Zero Budget" Beginner**
Pick **BigSpy**. Their free tier allows you to learn the ropes of ad spying without risking a credit card.

**The Native Ad Media Buyer**
Pick **PowerAdSpy**. It's the only tool that lets you bridge the gap between Taboola/Outbrain and Meta in one interface.

**The High-Ticket Dropshipper**
Pick **Minea**. The product-first workflow and supplier research integration are tailored for DTC e-commerce.

**The TikTok-to-Meta Arbitrageur**
Pick **PiPiADS**. Find the viral hits on TikTok first, then port the creative over to Meta Advantage+.

## What we left off — and why
You'll notice we didn't include the "Facebook Ad Library." While it's free and official, we don't consider it a "spy tool" for professional operators. The Ad Library lacks the ability to filter by "days running" (profitability signal), doesn't archive landing pages, and doesn't allow you to save ads into organized swipe files. It's a search tool, not an intelligence stack.

We also excluded several "AI-powered" ad generators. While they are trendy, they don't provide *intelligence*—they provide *execution*. This list is strictly about research and spying. If you don't have the data from a spy tool, your AI generator is just guessing.
