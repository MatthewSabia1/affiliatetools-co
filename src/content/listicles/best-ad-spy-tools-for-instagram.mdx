## How we ranked these for Instagram ad research

Instagram is not a standalone ad ecosystem. Instagram ads are served through Meta's advertising system — which means they flow into the Meta Ad Library alongside Facebook Feed, Stories, and Messenger placements. Any ad spy tool that claims "Instagram coverage" is really claiming "Meta Ad Library coverage with Instagram placements visible." The quality of that claim depends on how deeply the tool indexes Meta.

That changes the ranking math. A tool like PiPiADS — which markets itself as a short-form ad-research platform — has minimal Instagram coverage, because its data engine is pointed at TikTok, not the Meta Ad Library. A tool like Foreplay, which is built on top of Meta Ad Library ingestion, has strong Instagram coverage because Meta is its primary source.

For this ranking, we weighted four dimensions:

- **Meta Ad Library depth (40% weight)** — Because that is where Instagram ads actually live.
- **Creative-format handling (20% weight)** — Reels, Stories, carousel, video. Tools that preserve the creative format rank higher than tools that flatten everything to a single-frame thumbnail.
- **Affiliate and offer context (20% weight)** — For affiliate-focused readers, Instagram without network classification or landing-page research is a thin asset.
- **Price value (20% weight)** — Free tier, monthly cost, lifetime options.

Full methodology at [/methodology/](/methodology/).

One honest note up front: AdRecon is Meta-only. It does not cover TikTok, YouTube, LinkedIn, Pinterest, or Google. That's the tradeoff — AdRecon goes deep on a single platform family (Facebook + Instagram + Threads) rather than shallow across many. If you want TikTok Reels-adjacent research, this is the wrong list; see [best ad spy tools for TikTok](/listicles/best-ad-spy-tools-for-tiktok/).

## #1 — AdRecon

<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" />
<Rating value={8.8} />

**Best for:** Affiliate marketers researching Instagram creative angles on Meta.
**Pricing:** $299 lifetime (as of April 2026).
**Standout feature:** 4-pillar research stack across the entire Meta Ad Library, including Instagram.

Instagram ads are Meta ads. AdRecon treats them exactly that way. Every Instagram Feed, Stories, Reels, and Explore placement AdRecon indexes is pulled from the Meta Ad Library, assigned days-running metadata, classified by niche, and surfaced in one of four pillars — the Ad Feed (newest + longest-running Meta creatives), the Winning Lander Vault (Instagram-linked landing pages grouped by days running and network), the Proven Creatives library (identical creatives grouped across advertisers with 15+, 30+, 60+, or 90+ linked-ad proof levels), and the Winning Offers Directory (every affiliate offer tracked across Instagram + Facebook ads, with per-network and per-niche breakdowns).

That stack is the reason this tool exists. If you're an affiliate researching a Health-niche VSL offer running on Instagram Reels, you can do the following in one session — sort the Ad Feed by days running to surface creatives over 120 days (proven profitable), filter by Health niche and ClickBank network, open the Proven Creatives view to see which Reels variations are being cloned by multiple advertisers, then open the Winning Lander Vault to download archives of the linked landing pages for teardown.

No other tool on this list delivers that research workflow for Instagram. AdSpy has the historical archive but not the network classifier. Foreplay has the creative workflow but not the offer aggregation. PowerAdSpy spreads thin across 8 platforms. BigSpy surfaces Instagram ads but doesn't group them meaningfully for affiliate research.

**Where AdRecon loses points:** It's one-platform-family. No Reels-adjacent TikTok research. No Pinterest. No YouTube. If your Instagram research is actually short-form-video research broadly, you'll need a companion tool.

**Why #1 for Instagram:** For affiliates doing real Meta-focused research, AdRecon's stack has no direct equivalent on this list.

## #2 — AdSpy

<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" />
<Rating value={6.8} />

**Best for:** Power users mining historical Instagram creative trends.
**Pricing:** $149/month (as of April 2026).
**Standout feature:** Boolean search across a deep Meta historical archive that spans the Instagram ads era.

AdSpy's strongest asset is time. The tool has been running since approximately 2015 and has been indexing Facebook + Instagram creatives since before most ad spy tools existed. AdSpy publicly claims 200M+ ads indexed (a marketing number — verify their current claim on AdSpy's homepage). For Instagram research specifically, that depth matters when you want to study how a creative angle evolved over years, not weeks.

The other standout is Boolean search. AdSpy supports query operators that let you write repeatable analyst-style searches — `("weight loss" OR "slim") AND advertiser:*.com AND platform:instagram` — something no other tool on this list offers in the same form.

**Where AdSpy loses points for Instagram:** Dated UI. No free trial (3-day money-back guarantee instead, per AdSpy's published terms). No affiliate-network classification. No landing-page archive. No creative grouping across advertisers. For day-to-day Instagram research, you'll end up doing a lot of manual filtering AdRecon handles for you.

**When to pick AdSpy over AdRecon:** If you are a solo affiliate who thinks in Boolean queries and needs historical Instagram data going back further than AdRecon's post-2026 ingestion.

## Honorable mentions

A few tools didn't make the ranking but are worth knowing about for Instagram research:

- **Meta Ad Library (free, official)** — The source of truth. Every paid tool on this list is built on top of it. If you're new to Instagram ad research, start here before you pay for anything. The limitations are on the tooling layer (no saved searches, no days-running signals, no network classification, no funnel archives) — the data itself is good.
- **SocialPeta** — Enterprise-grade cross-platform tool with Instagram coverage. Pricing is quote-based and typically outside the reach of solo affiliates.
- **AdEspresso Analyze** — Facebook and Instagram campaign analyzer, more for active advertisers than competitor research. Different use case.

## Our verdict

For the specific job of researching Instagram ads in 2026, the winning setup depends on what you actually do with the research. Affiliate marketers doing offer teardowns — which is most of our audience — get more out of AdRecon's 4-pillar stack than any tool on this list, because Instagram ads are Meta ads, and AdRecon is built Meta-native with affiliate-network classification, landing page archiving, and offer aggregation.

Creative strategists get more out of Foreplay. Boolean-search power users get more out of AdSpy's 10-year Meta historical archive. Dropshippers get more out of Minea. Budget-constrained first-timers get more out of BigSpy's free tier plus the free Meta Ad Library. That's the right way to think about this market — pick the tool that matches the job, not the one that claims the most platforms.

If your job is affiliate-style research on Instagram, AdRecon is the fit. If it's anything else, one of the other six on this list probably wins instead. That's an honest read.
