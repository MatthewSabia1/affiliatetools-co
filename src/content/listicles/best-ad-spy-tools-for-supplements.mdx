## TL;DR
For most supplement affiliates, AdRecon is the top pick due to its network-specific tagging and LP ripper. If you need a decade of historical data, go with AdSpy; for Native traffic expansion, PowerAdSpy is the logical choice.

## Why this list exists
Supplement (Nutra) marketing is a game of attrition. You are fighting a constant battle against Meta's compliance bots and the rapid decay of creative fatigue. The real problem isn't finding *an* ad—it's finding an ad that is actually profitable and identifying the exact presell page that makes the conversion happen before the link dies.

## How we ranked
We didn't just look at feature lists. We ran these tools through a Nutra-specific gauntlet, scoring them on eight dimensions: platform coverage, data freshness, pricing structure, UX efficiency, landing page archiving capabilities, offer identification, affiliate-specific focus, and search granularity.

### 3. PowerAdSpy — Best for scaling Nutra offers into Native traffic

<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

Many supplement affiliates start on Meta but eventually move to Native (Taboola, Outbrain) to avoid the strictness of social media compliance. PowerAdSpy is the only tool on this list that gives you a serious look at Native ad surfaces alongside Meta.

This allows you to see how a supplement offer is being framed for a "news-style" audience versus a "social-scroll" audience. The downside is that because they cover so many platforms, the depth of data on any single platform is thinner than what you'll find in a specialist tool.

### 4. BigSpy — A low-cost entry point for multi-platform sampling

<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

If you aren't sure if your supplement offer will work better on Pinterest, TikTok, or Meta, BigSpy is a decent place to start. They offer a real free tier (no credit card required), which is rare in this industry.

It's a "jack of all trades, master of none" situation. You get broad geographic reach and multi-platform visibility, but you lose the affiliate-specific tools like network classification or LP archiving. It's a sampling tool, not a professional Nutra stack.

Plans range from Free to $249/mo (as of April 2026).

### 5. Foreplay — Superior for creative mood-boarding and agency briefs

<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay isn't a "spy tool" in the traditional sense of hunting for offers; it's a workflow tool for creative production. If you have a dedicated designer building high-end supplement videos, Foreplay's ability to save ads into mood boards and create briefs is unmatched.

The UX is the best in the category. However, for the gritty work of CPA affiliate marketing—finding the winning offer and ripping the funnel—it lacks the necessary infrastructure. There is no network tagging and no LP archiving.

Pricing starts at $49/mo (as of April 2026).
