## TL;DR
For Meta-only affiliates, AdRecon is the smartest financial move due to its lifetime pricing. If you need multi-platform coverage on a shoestring budget, BigSpy is the go-to. Creative agencies should look at Foreplay.

## Why this list exists
Most ad spy tools are priced for agencies with massive budgets, charging $150 to $500 per month. For a solo affiliate or a beginner, that overhead kills your ROI before you've even launched a campaign. You need a tool that provides actionable intelligence without eating your entire daily ad spend.

## How we ranked
We don't rely on marketing brochures. We tested these tools using a senior operator's lens, scoring them across eight dimensions: platform coverage, data freshness, price (TCO), UX, landing page availability, offer classification, affiliate focus, and search granularity.

## The ranking

### 1. AdRecon — The lowest total cost of ownership for Meta affiliates

<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

Instead of a monthly bill, AdRecon offers a lifetime license starting at $299 (as of April 2026). If you were paying for a mid-tier spy tool at $99/mo, AdRecon pays for itself in roughly three months. It is a Meta-only stack, meaning it doesn't touch TikTok or Google, but it dominates in affiliate-specific utility. It can classify ads by network (ClickBank, MaxWeb, etc.) and includes a Landing Page Ripper to download full assets.

The trade-off is that AdRecon is a newer tool (launched 2026). It doesn't have the decade-deep historical archive that a giant like AdSpy possesses. If you need to see what worked in 2018, this isn't the tool for you.

<Screenshot slug="adrecon" variant="hero" />

### 2. BigSpy — The most accessible entry point for multi-platform research

<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

If you are just starting out and aren't ready to commit to a lifetime license or a high monthly fee, BigSpy is the logical choice. It is one of the few tools in the space that offers a legitimate free tier without requiring a credit card upfront.

Their paid plans start as low as $9/mo (as of April 2026), which is practically unheard of in this category. They cover a wide range of platforms, including Meta, TikTok, and Pinterest. It's a great "sampling" tool to see which platforms your offers actually resonate with.

However, the depth is shallow. Because they try to cover everything, they aren't as specialized as a Meta-only or TikTok-only tool. You won't find affiliate-network classification or landing page archiving here.

<Screenshot slug="bigspy" variant="hero" />

### 3. Foreplay — High-end creative organization for under $100

<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay isn't a "spy tool" in the traditional sense of hunting for winning offers; it's a creative research and workflow tool. If your primary struggle is organizing "swipes" and building briefs for designers, this is the best in class.

Starting at $49/mo (as of April 2026), it provides a polished UX that makes building mood boards effortless. It's designed for the agency workflow, allowing you to save ads and analyze them with AI-assisted tools.

The downside for a hard-core CPA affiliate is the lack of technical data. There is no landing page ripping and no network-level filtering. You are paying for the organization and the UX, not the raw data mining.

<Screenshot slug="foreplay" variant="hero" />

### 4. PowerAdSpy — The best value for those needing Native ad data

<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

If your strategy involves Taboola, Outbrain, or RevContent, PowerAdSpy is the most cost-effective way to get that data. While most "budget" tools focus on social, PowerAdSpy maintains a strong footprint in Native advertising.

With plans starting at $49/mo (as of April 2026), it gives you a broad view across almost every major ad surface, including LinkedIn and Quora. It's an excellent choice for media buyers who run omni-channel campaigns.

The interface feels dated, and like BigSpy, it lacks the deep affiliate-specific filters (like network classification) that make a tool truly "affiliate-first."

### 5. Minea — Solid product research for e-commerce operators

<ToolLogo slug="minea" size="lg" showName />

Minea is built for a different crowd—dropshippers and DTC brands. If you are running physical products rather than CPA offers, Minea's workflow is superior. It integrates supplier research and influencer content, which is vital for the e-com model.

Pricing starts at €49/mo (as of April 2026). It covers Meta, TikTok, and Pinterest effectively. 

For a traditional affiliate marketer running digital offers or lead gen, Minea is overkill in the wrong areas and lacking in the right ones. It doesn't offer the affiliate-network intelligence needed to quickly find winning ClickBank or BuyGoods offers.

## How to pick
If you are a Meta-focused affiliate, stop paying monthly subscriptions and go with a lifetime deal like AdRecon. If you are a generalist who needs to see what's happening on TikTok and Pinterest without spending more than a few bucks, BigSpy is your best bet. For those running Native ads, PowerAdSpy is the only budget-friendly option that covers the necessary surfaces.
