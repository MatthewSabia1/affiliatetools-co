## TL;DR
LinkedIn is the "black box" of ad spying. PowerAdSpy is the only tool on this list that provides direct LinkedIn scraping. If you can't find what you need there, your best bet is using the native LinkedIn Ad Library combined with a Meta spy tool for funnel research.

## Why this list exists
Most "best ad spy tool" lists lie to you about LinkedIn. They claim a tool has "multi-platform coverage" and leave it at that. As an operator, I can tell you that LinkedIn data is notoriously thin across the entire industry. B2B affiliates and media buyers are often left guessing because LinkedIn's walled garden is much harder to crack than Meta's. This list is meant to clear the air: there is no "magic bullet" for LinkedIn, but there are a few workarounds.

## How we ranked
We didn't just look at feature lists. We tested these tools based on eight specific dimensions: platform coverage, data freshness, price, UX, landing page archiving, offer identification, affiliate focus, and search granularity. For this specific list, "coverage" was weighted highest, as most tools simply don't touch LinkedIn.

## The ranking

### 1. PowerAdSpy — The only viable option for direct LinkedIn scraping

<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

If you absolutely must have a third-party dashboard for LinkedIn, PowerAdSpy is the choice. They claim the broadest platform coverage in the category, and they are one of the few that actually index LinkedIn ads alongside Native networks like Taboola and Outbrain.

For a B2B operator, this is useful because LinkedIn campaigns rarely exist in a vacuum. You can see what's running on LinkedIn and then check if those same offers are scaling on Native. However, the UX is dated and the depth of the LinkedIn database isn't nearly as impressive as what you'll find for Facebook.

Pricing starts at $49/mo for the Basic plan (as of April 2026).

<Screenshot slug="poweradspy" variant="hero" />

### 2. BigSpy — A decent entry point for multi-platform sampling

<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

BigSpy is a generalist tool. It covers LinkedIn, but don't expect a deep historical archive. It's more of a "sampling" tool than a deep-dive intelligence stack. 

The real draw here is the price. They offer a real free tier that doesn't require a credit card, which is rare in this space. If you're a beginner just trying to see if a specific B2B angle is working across different platforms, BigSpy is a low-risk way to start. Just be aware that the free tier has hard limits that will hit you quickly.

Pricing ranges from Free to $249/mo for VIP Enterprise (as of April 2026).

<Screenshot slug="bigspy" variant="hero" />

### 3. AdRecon — No LinkedIn data, but the best for the Meta side of B2B funnels

<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

 We didn't build LinkedIn coverage because, frankly, the data quality across the industry is poor. We focused entirely on Meta (Facebook, Instagram, Threads).

Why put it on a LinkedIn list? Because most B2B affiliates use LinkedIn for top-of-funnel awareness and Meta for the actual conversion via retargeting. While AdRecon won't show you the LinkedIn ad, our Landing Page Ripper allows you to download the full HTML/CSS of the B2B landing pages your competitors are using. If you find a winning B2B ad on LinkedIn, you can use AdRecon to see if that same offer is scaling on Meta and then rip the page to study the conversion architecture.

Pricing is $299 / $599 lifetime (as of April 2026).

<Screenshot slug="adrecon" variant="hero" />

### 4. AdSpy — Deep Meta archives that help you reverse-engineer B2B hooks

<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

AdSpy doesn't cover LinkedIn. However, it has a 10-year historical database for Meta. In my experience, B2B hooks that work on LinkedIn almost always have a mirrored version on Facebook, usually targeting a slightly broader "interest" group.

If you use AdSpy's Boolean search operators (AND/OR/NOT), you can find high-performing B2B creatives from years ago that are still relevant. It's a way to find "evergreen" B2B angles that you can then port over to LinkedIn. The downside is the $149/mo recurring cost (as of April 2026), which is steep if you're only using it for research.

### 5. Foreplay — Great for saving B2B inspiration, not for scraping LinkedIn

<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay is a workflow tool, not a scraper. It doesn't "spy" on LinkedIn in the traditional sense, but it is the best tool for agencies to build mood boards.

If you are manually browsing the LinkedIn Ad Library and find something you love, Foreplay's browser extension allows you to save those creatives into a permanent swipe file. It solves the problem of ads disappearing once a campaign is paused. It's a "manual" spy process, but the UX is the best in the category.

Pricing starts at $49/mo for the Inspiration plan (as of April 2026).

## How to pick
If you need raw LinkedIn data right now, go with PowerAdSpy. If you are on a budget and just want to browse, try BigSpy. But if you are actually trying to build a profitable B2B funnel, I recommend a hybrid approach: use the free LinkedIn Ad Library for creative research and a specialist tool like AdRecon to analyze the landing pages and Meta retargeting angles.
