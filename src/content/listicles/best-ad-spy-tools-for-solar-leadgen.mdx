## Why this ranking exists
Solar lead-gen is a brutal niche. The Cost Per Lead (CPL) is high, the competition is fierce, and the "big players" have highly optimized funnels that have been tested over years. If you are a solo affiliate entering this space, you cannot afford to "guess" your way to a winning funnel. You need to know exactly what the top-performing offers are using to qualify their leads.

The problem with most "Top 10" lists is that they treat all ad spy tools the same. They focus on the number of platforms covered or the "AI" features. But for a solar operator, a tool that covers Pinterest is useless. What actually matters is: *Can I see the ad? Can I see how long it's been running? And most importantly, can I see the landing page it leads to?*

In solar, the "ad" is just the hook. The "money" is made in the lead-capture flow—the quizzes, the eligibility checkers, and the urgency-driven landing pages. This ranking prioritizes tools that give you a window into that backend process, rather than just a gallery of images.

## How we ranked
We evaluated these tools based on eight weighted dimensions: coverage (platforms), dataFreshness, price, ux, landingPages (archiving capabilities), offers (affiliate-specific classification), affiliateFocus, and search (filtering depth).

For solar lead-gen, we weighted **landingPages** and **offers** highest. In this niche, knowing that an ad is running is secondary to knowing it's promoting a specific high-converting solar offer from a network like MaxWeb or BuyGoods. We also heavily weighted **dataFreshness** because solar regulations and "hooks" change rapidly by state.

### 1. AdRecon — The affiliate marketer's Meta-only ad intelligence stack
<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

AdRecon is built specifically for the CPA affiliate, which makes it the most surgical tool for solar lead-gen. While other tools try to be everything to everyone (TikTok, Google, Pinterest), AdRecon doubles down on Meta. For solar, this is the correct bet.

The standout feature for solar operators is the Landing Page Ripper. In solar lead-gen, the "Quiz" is the industry standard. These quizzes qualify the homeowner (credit score, roof type, monthly bill) before the lead is sent to the buyer. AdRecon doesn't just show you a screenshot of the page; it allows you to download the full LP HTML, CSS, and assets as a ZIP file. This eliminates hours of manual rebuilding and allows you to analyze the exact conversion triggers being used.

Another critical workflow for solar is the "Days-Running" signal. Solar ads are expensive. If you see a solar ad that has been active for 45 days, it is almost certainly profitable. AdRecon allows you to filter for these "proven" creatives, so you aren't wasting your budget testing hooks that the market has already rejected.

For example, a solar operator can use the "Winning Offers Directory" to see which specific solar offers are currently scaling across the network. Instead of searching for the keyword "solar," you can filter by affiliate network classification (e.g., MaxWeb or BuyGoods). This reveals the exact offers that are paying the highest payouts and the creatives that are driving the volume.

One honest limitation: AdRecon is a newer tool (launched 2026) and does not have the 10-year historical archive that AdSpy possesses. If you need to see what solar ads looked like in 2018, AdRecon isn't the tool. Furthermore, it has no AI features and no coverage outside of Meta.

Pricing is a one-time fee of $299 or $599 for lifetime access (as of April 2026), which is a massive advantage over the monthly "tax" imposed by most spy tools. Compared to AdSpy, AdRecon offers significantly more value for the affiliate specifically, primarily due to the LP Ripper and network classification.

### 2. AdSpy — The 10-year Meta ad archive with Boolean search
<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

AdSpy is the "old guard" of Meta spying. Its primary strength is the sheer volume of its database. While AdRecon is better for current affiliate workflows, AdSpy is superior for deep-dive historical research.

The power of AdSpy lies in its Boolean search operators. For a solar lead-gen specialist, this means you can run highly complex queries. You can search for ads that contain "Solar" AND "Tax Credit" but NOT "Free," allowing you to isolate specific angles (like the federal tax credit angle) while filtering out the "free solar" hooks that often attract low-quality leads.

A typical workflow in AdSpy involves digging into the archives to see how the top solar players transitioned their messaging over the last few years. Because they have a 10-year history, you can see the evolution of solar hooks—from the early "save money" ads to the current "government program" and "energy independence" angles.

However, AdSpy has a glaring weakness for the solar niche: it lacks landing page archives. You can see the ad, but once you click through, you are seeing the page as it exists *now*, not as it existed when the ad was first launched. If a competitor changes their funnel, the historical context is lost.

The UX is dated, and the pricing is a recurring $149/mo (as of April 2026). This recurring cost can be a burden for solo affiliates. Compared to AdRecon, AdSpy is a better "library" but a worse "toolkit." It tells you what happened in the past, but it doesn't give you the assets (like the LP ZIP files) to execute in the present.

### 3. PowerAdSpy — Broadest multi-platform coverage, including Native
<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

If your solar strategy extends beyond Meta, PowerAdSpy is the only serious contender. Solar lead-gen is one of the few niches where Native advertising (Taboola, Outbrain, RevContent) still performs exceptionally well. These "advertorial" style ads—which look like news articles about solar energy—often convert at a higher quality than standard Facebook ads.

The workflow here is about breadth. You can track a solar offer across YouTube, Google Display, and Native simultaneously. This allows you to see if a particular offer is "burning out" on Meta but still scaling on Native, giving you a signal to shift your budget.

The downside is that PowerAdSpy is a generalist. Because it covers so many platforms, its depth on any single platform is thinner than the specialists. It lacks the affiliate-network classification found in AdRecon and the deep Boolean search of AdSpy. The UX also feels like a relic of the mid-2010s.

Pricing starts at $49/mo for the Basic plan and goes up to $249/mo for Premium (as of April 2026). It is the best pick for the media buyer who views Meta as just one part of a larger solar acquisition engine.

### 4. BigSpy — Multi-platform ad spy with a real free tier
<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

BigSpy is the "entry-level" option. For a solar affiliate who is just starting and doesn't want to drop $300+ upfront, BigSpy provides a viable way to get their feet wet.

The most significant advantage is the real free tier (as of April 2026). While the free tier has hard limits on searches and views, it allows you to see the general landscape of solar ads without entering a credit card. This is useful for initial keyword research to see if a specific solar angle is even being tested in a certain geographic region.

For solar research, BigSpy is best used as a "discovery" tool. You use it to find a few winning hooks, and then you move to a more specialized tool to analyze the funnels. It covers Meta, TikTok, and Google Display, which gives you a broad overview of where solar ads are appearing.

However, BigSpy is shallow. It doesn't offer landing page archiving or affiliate network classification. If you are trying to scale a professional solar lead-gen operation, you will quickly outgrow BigSpy. It is a sampling tool, not a scaling tool.

Pricing ranges from Free to $249/mo for VIP Enterprise (as of April 2026).

### 5. Foreplay — Creative research + brief workflow for agencies
<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay isn't a "spy tool" in the traditional sense of searching a database; it's a workflow tool for creative strategists. For solar agencies managing multiple clients, Foreplay is indispensable for organizing "swipe files."

In solar, you often need to create different creative sets for different states (e.g., Florida vs. California). Foreplay allows you to save ads into mood boards and create detailed briefs for designers. Instead of sending a link to a Facebook ad that might be turned off tomorrow, you save the ad permanently in your Foreplay library.

The UX is the best in the category. It feels like a modern SaaS product. If your solar business relies on a high volume of fresh video creatives (UGC style), Foreplay's ability to organize and analyze these assets is superior to any other tool on this list.

The trade-off is that Foreplay lacks the "intelligence" side of spying. There is no affiliate network classification and no landing page ripping. It's a place to store and organize inspiration, not a place to reverse-engineer a competitor's technical funnel.

Pricing starts at $49/mo for the Inspiration plan (as of April 2026).

<ToolLogo slug="pipiads" size="lg" showName />

While solar is traditionally a Meta/Native game, TikTok is becoming a viable channel for "educational" solar lead-gen. PiPiADS is the undisputed leader for TikTok spying.

If you are testing a "Solar Secrets" or "Energy Hack" angle on TikTok, PiPiADS is the only tool with a deep enough database to be useful. It integrates with the TikTok Creative Center and allows you to see which solar videos are actually getting engagement.

The weakness is that PiPiADS is almost useless for traditional solar lead-gen on Meta. It doesn't have the landing page tools or the network filters that a CPA affiliate needs. It is a niche tool for a niche channel.

## What we left off — and why
You will notice we didn't include generic SEO tools or "all-in-one" marketing suites. While tools like SEMRush can show you some ad data, they are not "spy tools." They don't give you the granular, real-time creative data that a CPA affiliate needs to survive in the solar niche.

We also excluded several "AI-powered" ad generators. In solar lead-gen, "AI-generated" ads often look generic and fail to convert. The most successful solar ads are usually based on raw, human-centric hooks or highly specific regulatory triggers. We believe in spying on *what is actually working* rather than letting an LLM guess what might work.
