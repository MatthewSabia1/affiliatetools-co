## How we ranked these for Meta ad research

Meta is the most-covered platform in the ad-spy category. Every generalist tool claims Meta coverage. The question is whether the coverage is deep and structured enough to actually answer the questions affiliate marketers, creative strategists, and product operators are asking.

We weighted four dimensions:

- **Meta Ad Library depth (35% weight)** — Raw index coverage, update cadence, and placement handling (Feed, Stories, Reels, Threads).
- **Affiliate-relevant context (25% weight)** — Network classification, offer aggregation, landing-page archive, days-running signals.
- **Creative handling (20% weight)** — Video preservation, carousel handling, thumbnail quality, creative grouping across advertisers.
- **Price value (20% weight)** — Monthly or lifetime cost vs. depth delivered.

Full methodology at [/methodology/](/methodology/).

One scope note: this list is specific to Meta research, which is why AdRecon ranks #1 here. The same editorial framework still calls out where other tools win — AdSpy on Boolean search and historical depth, Foreplay on creative workflow, Minea on DTC e-commerce framing. If any of those jobs matches your day-to-day work more closely, follow those sections rather than the headline rank.

## #1 — AdRecon

<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName mode="mark" />
<Rating value={9.0} />

**Best for:** Affiliate marketers running Facebook, Instagram, and Threads offers.
**Pricing:** $299 lifetime (as of April 2026). $599 tier available.
**Standout feature:** 4-pillar research stack built Meta-native for affiliate workflows.

AdRecon's single-platform focus is the feature, not a limitation. Every engineering decision — the scraper, the classifier, the landing-page ripper, the deduplication logic, the proof-based creative grouping — is tuned for the Meta Ad Library. The result is a stack that answers affiliate research questions nothing else on this list answers cleanly.

The four pillars work together:

1. **Ad Feed** — Meta ads sorted by days running. A creative running over 120 days on Meta is a strong profitability signal, because Meta's auction economics will pause losing creatives fast. Filter by network (ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, WarriorPlus), niche (Health, Wealth, BizOpp, Relationship, Survival), advertiser, or creative format.
2. **Winning Lander Vault** — Landing pages grouped by the Meta ads linking to them, with days-running rollups and network tags. Use Landing Page Ripper to download a complete funnel (HTML, CSS, assets, up to 100MB) for offline teardown.
3. **Proven Creatives** — Identical creatives grouped across multiple advertisers with proof tiers (15+, 30+, 60+, 90+ linked ads). A creative being run by 30+ different advertisers is strong social proof it converts.
4. **Winning Offers Directory** — Every affiliate offer tracked across Meta ads, with per-network and per-niche breakdowns, ad counts, active-ad counts, days running, and linked landing pages.

Combine those four pillars and the Meta affiliate research workflow collapses from a multi-tool spreadsheet to a single session. Classifiers are regex-based pattern matching against known URL signatures — not AI, not ML, not GPT — which makes the behavior deterministic and predictable. Rate limits on Landing Page Ripper are 10 rips per 15 minutes per user, with 100MB max per capture and a 110s timeout.

**Where AdRecon loses points:** Meta-only. No TikTok, YouTube, Pinterest, LinkedIn, or Google. Shorter historical archive than AdSpy — AdRecon launched in 2026. Not an agency creative-workflow tool — Foreplay is better for that job.

**Why #1 for Meta:** For affiliate research on Meta specifically, AdRecon's stack has no direct equivalent on this list.

## #2 — AdSpy

<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName mode="mark" />
<Rating value={7.2} />

**Best for:** Boolean-search power users with $150/month budget.
**Pricing:** $149/month (as of April 2026). 3-day money-back guarantee per AdSpy's terms. No free trial.
**Standout feature:** Boolean operators across a roughly decade-long Meta historical archive — AdSpy claims 200M+ ads indexed.

AdSpy is the incumbent. It has been running since approximately 2015 and has been indexing Facebook and Instagram creatives since before most tools in this category existed. The two things AdSpy does genuinely better than everything else are time depth and query expressiveness.

Time depth: you can research how a creative angle evolved from 2017 to 2024 in the same interface. For longitudinal pattern research — studying Health-niche VSL evolution, for example — AdSpy's archive is a real advantage.

Query expressiveness: AdSpy supports Boolean operators (`("weight loss" OR "slim") AND country:US AND platform:facebook`) — an analyst-friendly query language that no other tool on this list replicates.

**Where AdSpy loses points:** Dated UI. No free trial. No affiliate-network classification (manual URL filtering required). No landing-page archive. No creative grouping across advertisers. No offer aggregation. For current-day workflow, AdRecon saves more time.

**When to pick AdSpy over AdRecon:** If you think in Boolean queries and your research leans historical rather than current-day. If you want a running-cost subscription instead of a one-time lifetime license.

## Our verdict

Meta is the most-covered platform in the ad-spy category and the most important for affiliate marketers. The depth separation between specialists (AdRecon, AdSpy) and generalists (BigSpy, PowerAdSpy, Minea as a secondary Meta tool) is significant.

For affiliate-focused Meta research with network classification, offer aggregation, and landing-page archiving, AdRecon wins this list. For historical or Boolean-query research, AdSpy wins. For agency creative workflow, Foreplay wins. For DTC and dropshipping product research on Meta, Minea wins. BigSpy and PowerAdSpy fit specific price and consolidation jobs.

Pick by job, not by brand. That's always the honest answer in this category.
