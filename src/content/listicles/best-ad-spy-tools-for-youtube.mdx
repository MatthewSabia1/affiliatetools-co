## TL;DR
If you need the deepest index of YouTube ads, PowerAdSpy is the top pick for its sheer breadth. For those on a budget, BigSpy is the best starting point. AdRecon does not cover YouTube, as we focus exclusively on Meta intelligence.

## Why this list exists
YouTube is a different beast than Meta. You aren't just looking for a static image or a 15-second clip; you're looking for hooks, storytelling structures, and long-form sales arguments. Most "multi-platform" spy tools claim to cover YouTube, but the actual depth of their index varies wildly. I've put this list together to separate the tools that actually provide actionable YouTube data from those that just check a box on their features page.

## How we ranked
We didn't just look at feature lists. We ran actual searches for current affiliate offers across these platforms. We scored each tool based on eight dimensions: platform coverage, data freshness, price, UX, landing page archiving, offer identification, affiliate focus, and search functionality.

## The ranking

### 1. PowerAdSpy — The most comprehensive YouTube and Native coverage

<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

When it comes to YouTube, PowerAdSpy is the most logical choice for a serious media buyer. They don't just index YouTube; they index the surrounding ecosystem, including Native platforms like Taboola and Outbrain. This is critical because many high-ticket affiliate offers run a "sandwich" strategy: YouTube for awareness and Native for retargeting.

The depth of their YouTube database is superior to the generalists. However, the UX feels like it's from 2015. It's not pretty, and it takes a few tries to get the filters exactly right, but the data is there. 

Pricing starts at $49/mo for the Basic plan (as of April 2026).

<Screenshot slug="poweradspy" variant="hero" />

### 2. BigSpy — A solid entry point with a real free tier

<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

BigSpy is where I usually send beginners. They offer a legitimate free tier that doesn't require a credit card, which is rare in this industry. Their YouTube coverage is decent, though not as deep as PowerAdSpy. It's enough to see what's trending and get a feel for the creative direction of a niche.

The limitation here is the "shallow" nature of the data. You'll find the big hits, but you won't find the hidden gems that a specialist tool might uncover. Still, for $9/mo on the Basic plan (as of April 2026), it's an easy pill to swallow.

<Screenshot slug="bigspy" variant="hero" />

### 3. Minea — Strong for e-commerce and dropshipping video research

<ToolLogo slug="minea" size="lg" showName />

Minea is built for the DTC crowd. If your "YouTube" strategy is actually just running YouTube Shorts to sell a physical product, Minea is excellent. Their workflow is tuned for product discovery and supplier research, which makes the video research feel more integrated.

The downside is that it's not built for the CPA affiliate. You won't find tools here to help you track ClickBank or MaxWeb offers specifically. It's a product tool, not an offer tool. Pricing starts at €49/mo for the Starter plan (as of April 2026).

<Screenshot slug="minea" variant="hero" />

### 4. PiPiADS — TikTok-first, but useful for Shorts

<ToolLogo slug="pipiads" size="lg" showName />

PiPiADS is the undisputed king of TikTok. Why is it on a YouTube list? Because the "Shorts" economy is almost entirely driven by TikTok trends. If you see a winning creative on PiPiADS, there is a 90% chance someone is mirroring it on YouTube Shorts.

If you are focusing on long-form YouTube ads, this tool is useless. But for vertical video affiliates, it's an essential piece of the puzzle. Pricing starts at $77/mo (as of April 2026).

### 5. Foreplay — Excellent for saving and briefing, not deep indexing

<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay isn't a "spy tool" in the traditional sense of having a massive, searchable database of every ad ever run. Instead, it's a workflow tool. It allows you to save ads you find manually into mood boards and create briefs for your editors.

It's the best UX in the category by a mile. If you have a team of creators and you need to organize your YouTube research into a cohesive strategy, this is the tool. It starts at $49/mo (as of April 2026).

### 6. AdSpy — The gold standard for Meta, but irrelevant for YouTube

<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

I'm including AdSpy here to be honest: if you are searching for YouTube ads, do not buy AdSpy. It is a powerhouse for Meta (Facebook and Instagram) with a 10-year archive and incredible Boolean search, but it simply does not cover YouTube.

It's a great tool, but it's the wrong tool for this specific job. Pricing is $149/mo (as of April 2026).

 We built it specifically for the solo affiliate running Meta campaigns. Because we focused on doing one thing perfectly—classifying affiliate network offers on Meta—we do NOT cover YouTube, TikTok, or Google.

If you need YouTube research, use PowerAdSpy. If you've already found your winning YouTube offer and now you want to scale it on Facebook using our Landing Page Ripper and network classification, then Pricing is $299 / $599 lifetime (as of April 2026).

If you are running a full-funnel campaign across YouTube and Native, go with PowerAdSpy. If you are a beginner just wanting to see what's working without spending a dime, start with BigSpy. For those doing short-form vertical video, PiPiADS is the play.
