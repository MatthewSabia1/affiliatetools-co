## TL;DR
For most MaxWeb affiliates, AdRecon is the clear choice due to its native network classification. If you need deep historical archives, go with AdSpy; if you are scaling into Native traffic, PowerAdSpy is the move.

## Why this list exists
MaxWeb offers are high-converting, but the competition is fierce. The problem with most ad spy tools is that they treat a MaxWeb offer the same as a random Shopify store. You waste hours searching for keywords like "weight loss" or "keto" only to find 90% of the results are irrelevant. You need a tool that understands the affiliate ecosystem and can isolate MaxWeb-specific traffic.

## How we ranked
We didn't just look at feature lists. We ran hands-on tests using active MaxWeb offer IDs and known affiliate footprints. We scored each tool across eight dimensions: platform coverage, data freshness, price, UX, landing page archiving, offer classification, affiliate focus, and search capability.

## The ranking

### 1. AdRecon — The only tool that auto-classifies MaxWeb offers

<ToolLogo slug="adrecon" size="lg" showName />

AdRecon is built specifically for the CPA affiliate. While other tools make you guess which ads are affiliate offers, AdRecon automatically classifies creatives from networks like MaxWeb, ClickBank, and BuyGoods. This removes the guesswork—you can simply filter for MaxWeb and see exactly what is running right now.

The real edge here is the Landing Page Ripper. Instead of just seeing a URL that might be dead by the time you click it, you can download the full HTML/CSS and assets as a ZIP. It's the fastest way to analyze a competitor's funnel.

That said, we have to be honest: AdRecon is a Meta-only specialist. It does not cover TikTok, YouTube, or Google. It's also a newer tool (launched 2026), so it doesn't have the decade-long archive that the legacy players possess. It costs $299 / $599 for a lifetime license (as of April 2026).

 

<Screenshot slug="adrecon" variant="hero" />

### 2. AdSpy — The 10-year Meta ad archive

<ToolLogo slug="adspy" size="lg" showName />

If you need to see how a specific MaxWeb offer evolved over the last five years, AdSpy is the only tool with the depth to do it. Their database is massive, and their Boolean search (AND/OR/NOT) is the most powerful in the industry for narrowing down specific niches.

However, AdSpy is a generalist tool. It has no idea what "MaxWeb" is; you have to hunt for the offers using keywords or known affiliate domains. It also lacks landing page archiving, meaning you'll often find "404 Not Found" pages when trying to see the funnel.

It's a recurring cost of $149/mo (as of April 2026), which can feel steep for solo operators.

<Screenshot slug="adspy" variant="hero" />

### 3. PowerAdSpy — Broadest coverage for Native scaling

<ToolLogo slug="poweradspy" size="lg" showName />

MaxWeb offers often perform exceptionally well on Native networks like Taboola and Outbrain. PowerAdSpy is the best choice here because it actually covers those surfaces, whereas most "ad spies" are just Meta wrappers.

The downside is that the depth per platform is thinner. You won't find as many niche Meta ads here as you would in AdRecon or AdSpy. The UX also feels like it's from 2015, and there is no affiliate-network classification to help you find MaxWeb offers quickly.

Pricing starts at $49/mo (as of April 2026).

<Screenshot slug="poweradspy" variant="hero" />

### 4. BigSpy — A low-cost entry point

<ToolLogo slug="bigspy" size="lg" showName />

For someone just starting with MaxWeb and who isn't ready to drop hundreds of dollars, BigSpy is a reasonable starting point. They offer a real free tier that doesn't require a credit card, allowing you to get a feel for what's running across Meta, TikTok, and Pinterest.

The trade-off is the "shallow" data. The free and basic tiers have hard limits that will frustrate a professional operator. Like the others, it lacks MaxWeb-specific classification and landing page archiving.

Plans range from Free to $249/mo (as of April 2026).

### 5. Foreplay — Creative research for agencies

<ToolLogo slug="foreplay" size="lg" showName />

Foreplay isn't really a "spy tool" in the traditional CPA sense—it's a workflow tool. It's incredible for saving ads into mood boards and creating briefs for designers. If you have a creative team building your MaxWeb ads, this is the best UX in the category.

But for a solo affiliate, it's missing the "intelligence" part. There is no network classification and no way to rip landing pages. It's about the *look* of the ad, not the *mechanics* of the offer.

Pricing starts at $49/mo (as of April 2026).

## How to pick
If you are running MaxWeb on Meta and want to see exactly what's working without spending hours searching, AdRecon is the only tool designed for that workflow. If you are a power user who needs 10 years of data, go with AdSpy. If you are scaling into Taboola or Outbrain, PowerAdSpy is your only real option.
