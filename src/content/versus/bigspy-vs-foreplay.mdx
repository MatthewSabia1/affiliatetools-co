## Where BigSpy wins

**Free tier and lowest entry cost.** BigSpy publishes a genuine free tier with limited searches and saved ads, and Basic starts at $9/mo (as of April 2026). Foreplay's free trial is typically ~7 days, and paid Inspiration is $49/mo. For operators testing the category on zero or tight budget, BigSpy is the only realistic first step. Over a year, Basic costs $108 vs Foreplay Inspiration's $588 — a meaningful gap at small scale.

**Six-platform coverage in one seat.** Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, Twitter, TikTok, Pinterest. Foreplay covers Meta and TikTok only. For researchers who need Twitter, YouTube, or Pinterest ad visibility, BigSpy is the only option between these two — and one of the only single-seat options in the category for that breadth.

**Generalist research works without training.** BigSpy's UI is filter-and-browse: platform picker, date range, keyword, country, done. No boards to curate, no brief structure to learn. For casual researchers who just want to see ads, the learning curve is minimal.

**Higher-volume search limits at VIP tier.** BigSpy VIP Enterprise ($249/mo) offers generous query and export caps that suit larger research teams. Foreplay's tiering is oriented toward creative seats and agency features, not raw research throughput.

**Decent for brand-ad visibility.** If your research question is "what are major brands running on TikTok this week?", BigSpy's cross-platform index answers it quickly. Foreplay's curated-board model is less suited to rapid market scans.

## Where Foreplay wins

**Modern agency-grade UX.** Foreplay is one of the most visually polished products in the ad-spy category — drag-and-drop boards, swipe files, clean responsive design, dark mode, keyboard shortcuts. BigSpy's UI is functional but feels dated by comparison. For teams that live inside the tool all day, Foreplay's UX pays back in time saved.

**Brief builder and creative-ops features.** Foreplay turns saved ads into structured creative briefs you can hand to designers or video editors. Design-tool integrations let you push references straight into production workflows. BigSpy has none of this — it's a research tool, not a creative-operations platform.

**Better for TikTok creative work.** Foreplay's TikTok coverage is deliberately tuned for creative strategists — video-first browsing, creator context, short-form-friendly layout. BigSpy indexes TikTok but treats it more like another data column than a native creative surface.

**Sharing and collaboration built in.** Shared boards, client review flows, and team organization are core to Foreplay. BigSpy is more of an individual-researcher tool. For agency-client workflows, Foreplay's collaboration features save real friction.

**Quality over quantity.** Foreplay's curated-library model prioritizes creative depth on Meta and TikTok over cross-platform breadth. For operators who live on those two surfaces and care about creative reference quality, Foreplay is the sharper tool.

## Pricing head-to-head

**BigSpy:** Free tier + Basic $9/mo + Pro $99/mo + VIP Enterprise $249/mo (as of April 2026). Year-one: $0 (free) to $2,988 (VIP Enterprise annual).

**Foreplay:** Inspiration $49/mo + Agency $99/mo + higher tiers for larger teams (confirm current lineup on Foreplay's pricing page — they iterate). Free trial ~7 days. Year-one: $588 (Inspiration) to $1,188 (Agency).

At the lowest tier, BigSpy Basic ($9/mo) undercuts Foreplay Inspiration ($49/mo) by $40/mo — a $480 annual gap. That's meaningful for solo operators. But Basic's search and save limits are tight; most serious researchers graduate to Pro quickly.

At the mid tier, BigSpy Pro ($99/mo) and Foreplay Agency ($99/mo) are the same price, which is the cleanest apples-to-apples comparison. At that price point, BigSpy gives you six platforms with standard research features. Foreplay gives you three platforms with creative-ops features (boards, briefs, collaboration). Which tier of research you value decides the fit.

Over three years at steady prices, BigSpy Pro totals $3,564. Foreplay Agency totals $3,564. Identical spend, different products. The choice is scope.

## The affiliate marketer question — is either the right fit?

The honest answer is no — not for CPA affiliate research specifically.

Both tools answer different questions than the one affiliate operators ask. BigSpy answers "show me ads across six platforms matching my filter." Foreplay answers "help me turn ad references into creative briefs." Neither answers "which ClickBank offer is paying right now, and which landing page is converting for it on Meta?"

Neither auto-classifies affiliate networks. ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, WarriorPlus — none appear as filterable fields in either tool. You end up pasting domain strings into search and watching them break when networks rotate.

Neither archives complete landing pages. BigSpy stores the ad creative and destination URL. Foreplay stores the ad creative with creative-ops context. If the LP goes down tomorrow or the funnel changes, the historical page is gone in both.

Neither groups creative concepts across advertisers with proof thresholds. If fifteen independent advertisers run the same creative, both tools show you fifteen ads. There's no "proven concept" filter surfacing creatives validated by multiple operators — the signal affiliate buyers want most.

## Final verdict

BigSpy and Foreplay don't really compete — they address different jobs. BigSpy is a cross-platform research database priced for generalists and first-time buyers. Foreplay is a creative-operations platform priced for agencies and in-house creative teams working on Meta and TikTok.

If you want platform breadth at the lowest entry cost in the category, BigSpy is the obvious choice. The free tier is the easiest on-ramp to the whole category, and six-platform coverage at $99/mo Pro is genuinely hard to replicate.

If your team's job is turning ad references into production-ready creative briefs, Foreplay is the obvious choice. The boards, brief builder, and collaboration features serve creative ops natively — and BigSpy won't replicate that workflow at any tier.
