## TL;DR

**Choose AdRecon if:** You run CPA affiliate campaigns on Meta, research offer-driven creatives (ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods), or need landing-page archives for teardown workflows. AdRecon's 4-pillar stack is built around affiliate research — research-first, not inspiration-first.

**Choose Foreplay if:** You work at an agency or brand team where the deliverable is creative briefs, mood boards, or client-ready swipe files. Foreplay's modern UX, tag/folder workflow, brief builder, and design-tool integrations are category-best for this job.

**Neither is directly replaceable with the other.** They serve different jobs — research vs inspiration — and both do their respective jobs well. Some teams run both.

## Where Foreplay wins

**Best-in-class UX.** Foreplay's interface is genuinely the most polished in the ad spy category. Keyboard shortcuts, filter chips, tag management, dark mode — everything feels modern. AdRecon has modern UX too but optimizes for different workflows.

**Swipe files + tags + folders.** Save ads to folders. Tag by client, campaign, angle. Reorganize with drag-and-drop. This workflow matches how creative strategists actually work. AdRecon has Saved Ads + Projects but not with this depth of organization.

**Brief builder.** Foreplay's Brief Builder generates client-ready creative briefs from swipe files. Pull 10 ads into a brief document. Export for client delivery. Unique to Foreplay — no other ad spy tool has this.

**Design-tool integrations.** Integrates with Figma, Adobe Creative Cloud. Hand off creative references directly to design teams. Again — agency-first positioning.

**Team collaboration.** Shared swipe files, team workflows, seat management. Agency and Teams tiers are built for collaborative work. AdRecon has an Agency tier but with less emphasis on creative-brief workflows.

**TikTok + Meta.** Foreplay covers both. AdRecon is Meta-only. For brand-side teams researching cross-platform creative, Foreplay's broader (but still focused) coverage is a match.

## Where AdRecon wins

**Affiliate-network classification.** ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, WarriorPlus auto-detected. Foreplay doesn't classify affiliate networks because Foreplay isn't built for CPA affiliates.

**Landing Page Ripper.** Downloads complete HTML/CSS/asset archives of landing pages. Foreplay shows URLs only. For LP teardown workflows, AdRecon is the only option between these two tools.

**Winning Offers Directory.** Aggregate view of all tracked affiliate offers with per-network breakdowns. Foreplay is ad-centric, not offer-aware.

**Proven Creatives (cross-advertiser grouping).** AdRecon groups identical creatives across multiple advertisers with proof levels (15+, 30+, 60+, 90+ linked ads). Foreplay shows ad-by-ad.

**Dead-creative tracking.** AdRecon's `is_active` flag surfaces dead creatives. Foreplay's data-freshness handling is less explicit.

**Deeper Meta coverage.** 300,000+ records focused exclusively on Meta. Foreplay's database is smaller and split across Meta + TikTok.

**Lifetime pricing.** $299 one-time vs Foreplay Agency's $1,188/year recurring. Over 3 years, AdRecon saves $3,265 vs Foreplay Agency.

## Can you use both?

Yes — and some teams do. Foreplay for creative inspiration + client briefs. AdRecon for affiliate-offer research + landing page archives. Combined Year 1 cost: Foreplay Agency ($1,188) + AdRecon ($299 lifetime) = $1,487 total. Still less than a single year of AdSpy.

This works well for hybrid teams — agencies that also run some CPA campaigns, or brand marketers moonlighting as affiliates. For pure specialists in one direction or the other, pick one tool.

## Verdict

If you're a creative agency or brand team whose deliverables are mood boards, briefs, and client-ready swipe files: Foreplay. Full stop. The UX and workflow are category-best for that job.

If you're a CPA affiliate or performance marketer doing offer-driven research: AdRecon. The affiliate-network classification, Landing Page Ripper, and Winning Offers Directory fit your workflow in ways Foreplay wasn't built to.

If you do both: stack them. The combined cost is reasonable for multi-workflow operators.
