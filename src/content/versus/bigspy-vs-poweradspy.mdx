## Where BigSpy wins

**Free tier exists.** BigSpy publishes a genuine free tier with limited searches and saved ads. PowerAdSpy doesn't. For operators testing the category without committing budget, BigSpy is the only option between these two. That accessibility matters — first-time buyers can evaluate the product before paying.

**Lowest entry price.** BigSpy Basic at $9/mo (as of April 2026) vs PowerAdSpy Basic at $49/mo — a 5x gap. For small operators or affiliates running lean budgets, BigSpy Basic is genuinely affordable. PowerAdSpy's $49 floor is a real blocker for hobbyists.

**Pinterest and Twitter coverage.** BigSpy indexes Pinterest (useful for e-commerce and lifestyle verticals) and Twitter (useful for SaaS and B2B research). PowerAdSpy doesn't cover either. Niche but real — certain verticals rely on these platforms.

**More modern UX in recent builds.** BigSpy has iterated its interface more aggressively than PowerAdSpy over the last two years. PowerAdSpy's UX remains closer to 2019-era patterns — functional but visually dated. New users typically find BigSpy faster to learn.

**Cleaner data-quality reputation.** Affiliate communities report fewer indexing complaints about BigSpy than about PowerAdSpy, particularly around TikTok and newer surfaces. Reports are anecdotal and shift with product updates, but the general sentiment tilts toward BigSpy on this axis.

## Where PowerAdSpy wins

**Native and Google Display coverage.** PowerAdSpy indexes native ad networks (Taboola, Outbrain-style placements) and Google Display inventory. BigSpy doesn't touch either. For affiliates running native campaigns alongside Meta, or media buyers analyzing Google Display competition, PowerAdSpy is the only option between these two.

**Reddit and Quora ads.** PowerAdSpy covers Reddit and Quora ads — useful for SaaS, fintech, and B2B research where those platforms carry real budget. BigSpy doesn't index either. Niche but real for specific verticals.

**Eight-platform surface area.** PowerAdSpy covers Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, Google Display, Native, Quora, Reddit, TikTok. BigSpy covers six. Numerically, PowerAdSpy wins — and the platforms it adds (display, native, Reddit, Quora) are ones no other generalist covers cleanly.

**Mid-tier pricing fits serious researchers.** PowerAdSpy's mid-range plans ($99-$149/mo) suit researchers who need higher query volumes than BigSpy's Basic allows but don't need VIP Enterprise-level caps. The tier structure accommodates growth more gradually.

**Longer category presence.** PowerAdSpy is one of the older multi-platform ad-spy tools, which means mature integrations and broader community familiarity in certain affiliate circles. Team onboarding is easier if your operators already know PowerAdSpy's workflow.

## Pricing head-to-head

**BigSpy:** Free tier + Basic $9/mo + Pro $99/mo + VIP Enterprise $249/mo (as of April 2026). Year-one: $0 to $2,988.

**PowerAdSpy:** Basic $49/mo, mid-tier plans through Ultimate at $299/mo (as of April 2026). Some tiers include a 7-day trial. Year-one: $588 (Basic) to $3,588 (Ultimate).

At entry, BigSpy Basic ($9/mo) vs PowerAdSpy Basic ($49/mo) is a $40/mo gap — $480/year saved. But the value comparison depends on whether BigSpy Basic's search and save limits fit your use case. Most serious researchers graduate to Pro or higher within a quarter.

At mid tier, BigSpy Pro ($99/mo) vs PowerAdSpy mid-tier plans (~$99-$149/mo) is comparable. Pro gives six platforms with general research. PowerAdSpy mid-tier gives eight platforms including native and display. If you need native or display, PowerAdSpy wins that comparison. If not, BigSpy Pro is slightly cheaper.

At the top tier, BigSpy VIP Enterprise ($249/mo) vs PowerAdSpy Ultimate ($299/mo) is a $50/mo gap in BigSpy's favor — $600/year. Ultimate gets you higher query caps and more seats, plus the native/display coverage BigSpy can't match.

Over three years at steady prices, BigSpy Pro totals $3,564. PowerAdSpy mid-tier totals roughly similar. The pricing conversation is mostly a scope conversation — which platform mix matches your buying motion.

You're stuck with domain-string search that breaks when networks rotate subdomains or cloak through redirectors. Every serious CPA operator hits this wall within their first research week.

Neither groups creative concepts across independent advertisers with proof thresholds. ## Final verdict

BigSpy and PowerAdSpy are two of the oldest multi-platform ad-spy tools in the category, and they've aged into different roles. BigSpy has iterated faster, positioned cheaper, and holds better UX mindshare. PowerAdSpy has held onto native and display coverage that no other generalist matches, and its platform count is the highest in the category.

If your research is broadly cross-platform and you want the cheapest entry to the category, BigSpy wins. The free tier is genuine, Basic is $9/mo, and six-platform coverage gets the job done for most research.

If you buy or research ads on native, display, Reddit, or Quora, PowerAdSpy is the only option between these two. The $49 entry floor is higher, but the platform coverage is real and differentiated.

For CPA affiliates on Meta, we keep recommending AdRecon — neither tool above classifies networks or archives landing pages, and those are the two jobs that actually move affiliate research forward.
