## TL;DR

**Choose AdRecon if:** You run affiliate campaigns on Meta (Facebook, Instagram, Threads). AdRecon's Meta-specialist features — affiliate-network classification for ClickBank/Digistore/BuyGoods/MaxWeb/WarriorPlus, Landing Page Ripper, Winning Offers Directory — are what CPA affiliates actually need. $299 lifetime.

## Where BigSpy wins

**Platform breadth.** Seven platforms: Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Twitter/X, Pinterest, Yahoo. AdRecon is Meta-only. For anyone researching cross-platform campaigns, BigSpy's coverage is structurally broader.

**Real free tier.** BigSpy's free plan is permanent (not a trial). Good for first-time researchers evaluating whether ad-spy produces signal before paying. AdRecon has no free tier — purchase + 7-day money-back is the entry path.

**Lowest paid entry price.** BigSpy Basic starts at $9/mo. AdRecon's $299 one-time is higher upfront. For budget-constrained solopreneurs, BigSpy is easier to start with.

**Multi-platform generalist view.** If an advertiser runs cross-platform campaigns, BigSpy surfaces them all in one interface. AdRecon shows you only the Meta creatives for the same advertiser.

**Faster first-research cycle.** Free tier + broad platform coverage means faster answers to "what are they running where?" before deciding whether to specialize.

## Where AdRecon wins

**Meta depth.** AdRecon invests everything in Meta. Affiliate-network classification, Landing Page Ripper, Winning Offers Directory, Proven Creatives grouping, dead-creative tracking — these are Meta-specific depth features BigSpy doesn't build because BigSpy spreads attention across 7 platforms.

**Affiliate-network classification.** Auto-detects ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, WarriorPlus. BigSpy doesn't. For CPA affiliates, this is the single biggest research-workflow improvement.

**Landing Page Ripper.** Downloads complete landing page archives as ZIPs. BigSpy provides URLs only.

**Winning Offers Directory.** Aggregate view of all tracked affiliate offers with per-network breakdowns. BigSpy is ad-centric, not offer-aware.

**Proven Creatives.** When the same creative runs across multiple advertisers, AdRecon groups them with proof levels. BigSpy shows each as a separate ad.

**Data quality consistency.** Single-platform focus means more dedicated attention per platform. BigSpy's multi-platform index has documented data-quality variance (stale creatives, dedup issues on less-popular platforms).

**Lifetime pricing.** $299 one-time. BigSpy Pro ($99/mo) costs $1,188/year recurring.

**Dead-creative tracking.** AdRecon's `is_active` flag surfaces dead creatives as first-class signal. BigSpy's active/inactive handling is less explicit.

## Stack both?

This is genuinely a reasonable setup for many affiliates:
- BigSpy free tier: multi-platform spot-checks. Costs $0.
- AdRecon $299 lifetime: Meta affiliate depth, network classification, LP archives, offer intelligence.

Combined cost: $299 one-time. Covers Meta deeply + multi-platform lightly. Compared to BigSpy VIP ($2,988/year) for "everything in BigSpy" or AdSpy + BigSpy Pro ($2,976/year), the AdRecon + BigSpy-free stack is dramatically cheaper and covers the critical research workflows.

## Verdict

For Meta-focused affiliate marketers: AdRecon. The depth features are what affiliate workflows need, and $299 lifetime is unbeatable pricing.

For first-time researchers or multi-platform generalists: BigSpy free tier is the right start. Upgrade to Basic ($9/mo) as you grow, or jump to AdRecon if you commit to Meta-affiliate research specifically.

For anyone comfortable stacking tools: BigSpy free + AdRecon $299 gives you the broadest useful coverage at the lowest sustainable cost.
