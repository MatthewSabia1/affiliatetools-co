## Where AdSpy wins

**Deep historical Meta archive.** AdSpy has crawled Facebook and Instagram since approximately 2015. That decade of continuous crawling gives AdSpy's Meta database a depth Minea doesn't match — Minea is a newer tool with a broader charter, and its per-platform Meta depth is shallower as a result. For retrospective research on Meta, AdSpy wins.

**Boolean search operators.** AdSpy's query language — AND, OR, NOT across text, advertiser, CTA, and keyword fields — is the most expressive search in the category. Minea's search is built around visual browsing, niche tags, and product filters. For researchers building complex repeatable queries, AdSpy's language is genuinely hard to replace.

**Performance-marketing heritage.** AdSpy's user base is weighted toward affiliate marketers, performance teams, and solo operators running CPA campaigns. The UI assumes you know what Geo targeting, CTA split-tests, and advertiser stacking mean. Minea's UX assumes e-commerce context — SKU research, supplier sourcing, dropshipping. Fit matters.

**Predictable flat pricing in USD.** $149/mo, single tier (as of April 2026). No EUR conversion, no tier-upgrade pressure. For US-based operators, that predictability is useful — Minea's EUR pricing (€49-€399) creates currency friction and conversion math for every renewal.

**Meta specialization shows in edge cases.** A decade of single-platform focus means AdSpy handles advertiser page merges, carousel variants, and dedupe logic well on Facebook and Instagram. Generalist tools that scrape four platforms rarely match that polish in any single one.

## Where Minea wins

**Multi-platform coverage in one seat.** Facebook, TikTok, Pinterest, and influencer ads from a single login. AdSpy is Meta-only. For dropshippers and DTC operators who need to watch product trends across short-form video and visual-discovery platforms, Minea is the only option between these two.

**E-commerce and dropshipping features AdSpy doesn't attempt.** Product research, supplier hints, Shopify-adjacent tooling, influencer-ad tracking — none of this is in AdSpy's scope. AdSpy is an ad database. Minea is an e-commerce research platform with ad spy bolted in. For product hunters, that lens matters.

**Free tier and lower entry price.** Minea publishes a limited free tier, and Starter at €49/mo is roughly one-third of AdSpy's $149/mo. For operators testing the category or running small dropship budgets, the economics are meaningfully better. AdSpy offers only a 3-day money-back guarantee — harder to evaluate before committing.

**Better UX for visual browsing.** Minea's interface is more modern and more visual than AdSpy's 2018-era patterns. Product cards, media-first layouts, and niche-tag filtering serve e-commerce research better than AdSpy's text-heavy filter pane. For teams that value visual browsing over Boolean queries, Minea is the better day-to-day tool.

**TikTok and Pinterest.** If your product research relies on spotting trends on TikTok or Pinterest before they saturate Meta, AdSpy doesn't help. Minea does. For dropshippers hunting winning products, that coverage is the entire point.

**AdSpy:** $149/mo flat (as of April 2026). No free trial. 3-day money-back guarantee. Year-one cost: $1,788 in USD.

**Minea:** Starter €49/mo, Premium €99/mo, Business €399/mo (as of April 2026). Free tier available for tasting. Year-one cost: €588 (Starter) to €4,788 (Business) — roughly $635 to $5,200 at recent exchange rates.

Minea Starter vs AdSpy is roughly a $100/mo gap in Minea's favor. But you're buying different products — Minea's multi-platform e-commerce lens vs AdSpy's Meta-only depth. Premium at €99/mo ($107) lands just below AdSpy's $149/mo and is the closest apples-to-apples tier in feature surface area.

Over three years at steady prices, AdSpy totals $5,364. Minea Premium totals roughly $3,852. That's a ~$1,500 delta — real money for solo operators, noise for funded teams.

For dropshippers, Minea is obviously the right buy. For Meta-focused affiliates, AdSpy is obviously the right buy. Cross-over cases — an affiliate experimenting with dropshipping, or a dropshipper scaling into Meta CPA — often run both. That's ~$200/mo combined and is a reasonable stack if you truly straddle both verticals.

## The affiliate marketer question — is either the right fit?

Neither tool answers the CPA affiliate's core question cleanly: which offers on ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, or WarriorPlus are paying right now, and what landing pages are converting for them on Meta?

Neither auto-classifies affiliate networks. AdSpy doesn't have a ClickBank filter. Minea doesn't have a Digistore24 filter. Both force affiliate teams into manual URL tagging, which breaks when networks rotate subdomains or cloak traffic through redirectors.

Neither archives complete landing pages. AdSpy stores the ad creative and destination URL. Minea stores the ad creative with product-context enrichment. Neither captures the full HTML/CSS/assets of the funnel as a downloadable archive. When the LP goes down tomorrow or the advertiser changes the funnel next week, the historical page is lost.

Neither groups creative concepts across independent advertisers with proof thresholds. If fifteen advertisers run substantially the same creative on Meta, both tools show you fifteen ads. There's no "proven concept" filter surfacing creatives validated by multiple independent operators — the exact signal affiliate buyers want.

## Final verdict

AdSpy and Minea aren't really competing for the same buyer. AdSpy is a Meta-only research database priced for affiliate and performance operators — $149/mo for Boolean depth and a decade of crawl history. Minea is a multi-platform e-commerce research platform priced for dropshippers and DTC operators — €49-€399/mo for product hunting across Meta, TikTok, Pinterest, and influencer surfaces.

If you're running Meta CPA offers, AdSpy is the stronger of the two — with the honest caveat that no Boolean syntax substitutes for actual affiliate-network classification, which neither tool ships.

If you're running a Shopify store or dropship operation, Minea is decisively the better choice. TikTok trends, Pinterest discovery, supplier research, and product-context metadata all serve that workflow. AdSpy's Meta-only scope won't even answer the basic question of "what's trending on TikTok this week."

For affiliate-focused Meta research specifically, we keep pointing teams to AdRecon for that job. It's the only tool built to classify affiliate networks and archive landing pages at depth — and those are the parts AdSpy and Minea both leave on the table.
