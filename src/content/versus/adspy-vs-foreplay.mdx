Best for solo operators and research-heavy roles who want the most expressive search language in the category on the platform AdSpy specializes in.

## Where AdSpy wins

**Boolean search operators.** AdSpy's query language — AND, OR, NOT across text, advertiser, CTA, and keyword fields — is the most expressive search in the ad-spy category. Foreplay's search is built around visual browsing, boards, and tags rather than complex queries. For researchers running structured queries on Meta (e.g., all supplement ads with "transformation" in body copy that do NOT come from known competitors), AdSpy is genuinely hard to replace.

**Historical Meta depth.** AdSpy has been crawling Facebook and Instagram since approximately 2015. That decade of continuous crawling means historical campaigns — the 2019 fitness surge, the 2021 wellness wave, the 2023 AI-product rush — are documented. Foreplay's library is newer. For retrospective research, AdSpy wins.

**Affiliate and performance-marketing heritage.** AdSpy has been the reference tool in affiliate marketing circles for nearly a decade. If your team already reads AdSpy screenshots without onboarding, that's a real productivity lever. Foreplay's user base is weighted toward agencies and creative teams, not direct-response operators.

**Single-platform specialization.** Because AdSpy focuses only on Meta, its dedupe logic, advertiser page merges, and carousel handling are mature for Facebook and Instagram. Foreplay covers Meta and TikTok and spreads engineering effort across both surfaces plus the creative-ops layer. Specialization shows up in edge cases.

**Predictable flat pricing.** $149/mo is one number for one tier. No seat expansion, no upsell pressure for "agency features." For solo operators who want budget predictability, that's valuable.

## Where Foreplay wins

**Modern agency-grade UX.** Foreplay is one of the most visually polished products in the ad-spy category — boards, swipe files, tags, brief builder, and clean responsive design. AdSpy's interface remains closer to 2018 patterns. For teams that live inside the tool all day, Foreplay's UX compounds into real hours saved.

**TikTok coverage.** Foreplay indexes TikTok ads alongside Meta. AdSpy is Meta-only. If your creative strategy spans short-form video across both surfaces, Foreplay consolidates the view into a single workflow.

**Creative-operations features AdSpy doesn't attempt.** Brief builder (turn saved ads into a creative brief for your production team), design-tool integrations, shared boards for agency-client review — none of this is in AdSpy's scope. AdSpy is a research tool. Foreplay is a creative-ops platform with a research layer attached.

**Entry price is meaningfully lower.** Foreplay Inspiration at $49/mo (as of April 2026) is roughly one-third of AdSpy's $149/mo. A 7-day free trial lets you test the product before paying. AdSpy offers only a 3-day money-back guarantee — harder to evaluate honestly.

**Better for brand and agency contexts.** If your deliverable is "a monthly creative report for a client" or "a new brief for the in-house studio," Foreplay's workflow features do that job natively. AdSpy forces export to spreadsheets and external tools.

## Pricing head-to-head

**AdSpy:** $149/mo flat (as of April 2026). No free trial. 3-day money-back guarantee. Year-one cost: $1,788.

**Foreplay:** Inspiration $49/mo, Agency $99/mo, higher tiers for larger teams (confirm current lineup on Foreplay's pricing page — they iterate frequently). Free trial typically 7 days. Year-one cost: $588 (Inspiration) or $1,188 (Agency).

At the entry level, Foreplay costs $100/mo less than AdSpy. Over one year, that's a $1,200 gap. Over three years, $3,600. But the comparison isn't purely about price — you're buying different products. Foreplay's swipe-file workflow and brief builder aren't on AdSpy's roadmap, and AdSpy's Boolean search and historical depth aren't on Foreplay's.

For a solo creator or small agency running weekly creative sprints, Foreplay Inspiration at $49/mo is the correct choice. For a research analyst or media buyer who runs structured Meta queries daily, AdSpy's $149/mo is the correct choice. These are complements more than substitutes.

Both tools are recurring SaaS. Over 3 years, AdSpy totals $5,364 and Foreplay Agency totals $3,564. AdRecon's $299 lifetime sits outside both models — mentioned in the context of affiliate-specific research below.

## The affiliate marketer question — is either the right fit?

The honest answer: not really, if you're running CPA offers at scale.

Both tools answer different questions than the one affiliate operators ask. AdSpy answers "what Meta ads exist that match this complex query?" Foreplay answers "what creative references should I put in front of my production team?" Neither answers "which ClickBank offer is paying right now, and which landing page is converting for it?"

Neither auto-classifies affiliate networks. ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, WarriorPlus — these don't appear as filterable fields in either tool. You end up pasting domain strings into search and hoping they survive the next network rotation.

Neither archives complete landing pages. Foreplay saves the ad creative and metadata. AdSpy saves the ad creative and destination URL. If the LP goes down tomorrow or the advertiser changes funnel, the page is gone. For affiliate teardowns, that's a structural gap.

Neither groups creative concepts across advertisers with proof thresholds. If ten independent advertisers run substantially the same creative, both tools show you ten ads. There's no "proven concept" filter that surfaces creative ideas validated by multiple operators — which is exactly the signal affiliate buyers want.

## Final verdict

AdSpy and Foreplay are in the same category only by loose definition. AdSpy is a research database for Meta — priced and built for operators who query ads the way a SQL analyst queries a warehouse. Foreplay is a creative-operations platform with an ad library inside it — priced and built for teams that turn inspiration into briefs into production.

If your day is structured queries, historical audits, and Boolean search, AdSpy is the right buy. The $149/mo is the price of the most expressive search language in the category, and Foreplay's board-based UX won't replicate that workflow.

If your day is creative sprints, swipe-file curation, and briefing production teams, Foreplay is the right buy. The $49-$99/mo range is reasonable for a modern workflow product, and AdSpy's research-first UX will slow you down.

If your day is running ClickBank offers on Meta, we keep recommending AdRecon for that specific job. Neither tool above was built to classify networks or archive landing pages — and those are the two hardest parts of affiliate research.
