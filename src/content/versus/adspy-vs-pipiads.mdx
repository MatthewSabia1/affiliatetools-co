## TL;DR

**Choose AdSpy if:** You run Meta-focused affiliate or performance campaigns on Facebook and Instagram and want the deepest historical Meta ad database with Boolean search. AdSpy has crawled Meta since approximately 2015 and claims 200M+ ads indexed (AdSpy's marketing claim). $149/mo (as of April 2026). No TikTok coverage. Best for Meta specialists.

**Choose PiPiADS if:** You run TikTok affiliate offers, TikTok Shop products, or UGC-style short-form campaigns. PiPiADS is the deepest TikTok Ads Library in the category and layers on TikTok Shop product research. Starter $77/mo, VIP $155/mo, Pro $263/mo (as of April 2026). Best for TikTok specialists and short-form creative operators.

These tools don't really compete — they complement. Meta affiliates pick AdSpy. TikTok affiliates pick PiPiADS. Operators running both surfaces often run both tools.

If your core job is Meta affiliate research with ClickBank/Digistore offer classification and landing-page archives, neither ships that capability. AdRecon is built for that specific job on Meta, and we mention it honestly at the end.

## Where AdSpy wins

**Boolean search language.** AdSpy's AND/OR/NOT operators across advertiser, text, CTA, and keyword fields are the most expressive search in the ad-spy category. PiPiADS uses category-and-tag filters, which work well for TikTok browsing but don't replicate AdSpy's query power. For researchers building structured complex queries on Meta, AdSpy is genuinely hard to replace.

**Deep historical Meta database.** AdSpy has been crawling Facebook and Instagram since approximately 2015. That decade of Meta-specific crawling gives AdSpy a historical depth PiPiADS doesn't attempt to match — because PiPiADS isn't crawling Meta at all. For retrospective Meta research, AdSpy is the category leader.

**Meta specialization.** A decade of single-platform focus means AdSpy's dedupe logic, advertiser page merges, and carousel handling are mature. Facebook Ad Library edge cases are handled. For operators who only care about Meta, that specialization shows up in data quality.

**Predictable flat pricing.** $149/mo, one tier (as of April 2026). Simple annual budget math. PiPiADS's three tiers (Starter/VIP/Pro at $77/$155/$263) create upgrade-path pressure AdSpy doesn't have — you'll feel the ceiling of Starter's query limits within weeks and face a Pro upsell soon after.

**Text-heavy ad library.** Meta still runs substantial image and text-led ad formats alongside video. AdSpy's UX treats text search, CTA filtering, and advertiser page analysis as first-class. PiPiADS's video-first UX deprioritizes these, which makes non-video Meta research awkward. For research that's not purely short-form video, AdSpy's lens is correct.

**Affiliate-marketing heritage.** AdSpy has been the default Meta ad-spy reference in affiliate marketing courses and forums for close to a decade. Team familiarity and onboarding speed are real productivity levers.

## Where PiPiADS wins

**TikTok coverage AdSpy cannot match.** PiPiADS indexes TikTok ads at depth — creator videos, paid placements, ad creative breakdowns, and TikTok Shop product data. AdSpy has zero TikTok coverage. If your offers convert on TikTok, PiPiADS is the only option between these two tools. There's no contest on this axis.

**TikTok Shop product research.** PiPiADS layers in product-level research for TikTok Shop — best-sellers, creator pairings, product metadata. Affiliate operators running TikTok Shop offers get structured data AdSpy doesn't have and wouldn't know how to expose.

**Short-form creative focus.** PiPiADS's UI is built around video playback, creator context, and TikTok's native creative language. AdSpy's UX assumes static image and video placements in Meta's format. For creative strategists building TikTok ad concepts, PiPiADS's context is the right one.

**Free tier exists.** PiPiADS publishes a limited free tier for tasting (confirm current limits on pipiads.com). AdSpy has no free trial — only a 3-day money-back guarantee. For buyers auditing the category, PiPiADS is the easier first step.

**Cheaper entry price.** PiPiADS Starter at $77/mo (as of April 2026) undercuts AdSpy's $149/mo by roughly half. For operators scaling into TikTok at small budget, Starter is approachable where AdSpy's $149/mo is a real blocker.

## Pricing head-to-head

**AdSpy:** $149/mo flat (as of April 2026). No free trial. 3-day money-back guarantee. Year-one: $1,788.

**PiPiADS:** Starter $77/mo, VIP $155/mo, Pro $263/mo (as of April 2026). Limited free tier. Year-one: $924 (Starter) to $3,156 (Pro).

Starter is cheaper than AdSpy. VIP is slightly more. Pro is substantially more. But the pricing comparison is misleading because you're buying different platforms. Meta affiliates cannot use PiPiADS in place of AdSpy — and TikTok affiliates cannot use AdSpy in place of PiPiADS. Coverage is binary.

For operators running both Meta and TikTok, the combined monthly lands near $226 (AdSpy + PiPiADS Starter) to $412 (AdSpy + PiPiADS Pro). Over three years, that's roughly $8,100 to $14,800. Real money, but often justified inside performance-marketing agencies running multi-surface campaigns.

Over three years at steady prices, single-tool spend: AdSpy totals $5,364, PiPiADS VIP $5,580 — roughly equivalent. The choice isn't about price. It's about which platform your offers run on.

## The affiliate marketer question — is either the right fit?

For a TikTok-first affiliate, PiPiADS is close to ideal within its scope. For a Meta-first affiliate, AdSpy is the reference tool within its scope. The caveats start when you actually sit down to research offers.

Neither tool auto-classifies affiliate networks. ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, WarriorPlus — these don't appear as filters in either interface. You're left pasting domain strings into search and watching them break when networks rotate subdomains or cloak traffic.

Neither tool archives complete landing pages. AdSpy shows you a destination URL. PiPiADS shows you the ad creative and product context. If the landing page goes down tomorrow or the funnel changes next week, the historical page is gone. For affiliate teardowns, this is a structural gap on both sides.

Neither groups creative concepts across independent advertisers with proof thresholds. If fifteen different Meta advertisers run substantially the same creative, AdSpy shows you fifteen ads. If fifteen different TikTok creators push the same concept, PiPiADS shows you fifteen videos. The "proven concept" layer — the signal affiliate buyers want most — isn't present.

Meta-only (Facebook, Instagram, Threads — no TikTok), 300,000+ records across ads, landing pages, creatives, and offers, regex-based auto-classification of the major CPA networks, Landing Page Ripper that captures complete HTML/CSS/assets as ZIP, and proven-creative groupings at 15+/30+/60+/90+ linked-ad thresholds. $299 lifetime, one payment.

Honest tradeoff: AdRecon won't help with TikTok (PiPiADS's job) or Boolean Meta queries across a 2015-present archive (AdSpy's job). If your research is Meta affiliate intelligence — networks, landers, offers, proven creatives — AdRecon is the direct fit.

## Final verdict

AdSpy and PiPiADS aren't substitutes. They're platform-specialists that happen to share the "ad spy tool" label. AdSpy owns the Meta depth story. PiPiADS owns the TikTok depth story. Operators who only care about one platform buy the specialist for that platform.

If your offers run on Facebook and Instagram, AdSpy is the right single-tool buy. The decade of crawl history and Boolean search are genuinely unmatched on Meta, and $149/mo is the category tax for that capability.

If your offers run on TikTok — especially TikTok Shop — PiPiADS is the right single-tool buy. Nothing else covers TikTok at this depth, and the product-research layer serves affiliate workflows directly.

If you run both platforms, run both tools. It's the honest answer. For Meta-focused affiliates who need network classification and landing-page archives on top of the Meta research layer, AdRecon is the complement we keep naming — because nobody else ships it.
