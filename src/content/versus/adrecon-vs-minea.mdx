## TL;DR

**Choose AdRecon if:** You run CPA affiliate campaigns. ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, WarriorPlus — those are your networks. Meta is your primary platform. You need affiliate-offer research, landing-page archives, and creative grouping.

**Choose Minea if:** You run dropshipping or DTC e-commerce on Shopify. Product research matters more than offer research. You need TikTok, Pinterest, and influencer campaign data alongside Meta.

**These are non-competing tools.** AdRecon explicitly doesn't track e-commerce or brand ads. Minea explicitly targets dropshipping. If you're clearly one or the other, the choice is easy.

## Where Minea wins

**Dropshipping-specific workflow.** Products → ads → sellers → suppliers → margins. This is the research flow Minea is built around. No generalist ad spy tool replicates it because no generalist tool is built for this persona. For a dropshipper spending hours per week on product research, Minea is the workflow match.

**Multi-platform e-commerce coverage.** Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest, plus influencer ads. AdRecon is Meta-only. For e-commerce research spanning multiple surfaces, Minea has the breadth.

**TikTok + Pinterest coverage.** Pinterest especially is a real e-commerce traffic channel that most ad spy tools skip. Minea indexes it.

**Influencer ad tracking.** Sponsored posts, branded content, creator partnerships — Minea tracks these as first-class data. AdRecon doesn't.

**Product-to-supplier mapping.** Minea surfaces supplier information (AliExpress links, product sourcing) for dropshipping products. Unique to Minea.

**Free tier.** Minea Lite gives you limited previews at $0. Low-friction evaluation.

**Shopify-adjacent tooling.** Store research, margin calculations, product trending — all purpose-built for Shopify dropshipping.

## Where AdRecon wins

**Affiliate-network classification.** ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, WarriorPlus auto-detected via regex. Minea doesn't — the networks aren't first-class in a dropshipping-focused tool.

**Landing Page Ripper.** Downloads complete landing page archives. Minea shows URLs and some context but no complete archive.

**Winning Offers Directory.** Aggregate CPA offer research with per-network breakdowns. Minea's equivalent is product research, which is different.

**Proven Creatives (cross-advertiser).** Groups identical creatives across multiple advertisers with proof levels. Minea is ad-by-ad.

**CPA-workflow fit.** AdRecon is built for affiliate-offer research end-to-end. For CPA campaigns, the workflow match is structurally cleaner.

**Dead-creative tracking.** `is_active` flag surfaces dead creatives. Minea's handling is less explicit.

**Lifetime pricing.** $299 one-time vs Minea Premium €99/mo recurring.

**Meta depth.** 300,000+ records focused on Meta. Minea's Meta index is narrower than its overall scope.

## Not really competitors

AdRecon's landing page explicitly says "We don't track e-commerce or brand ads." Minea's landing page explicitly targets dropshipping and DTC e-commerce. These are complementary tools in different verticals.

Most affiliates don't need both. Pick the one that matches your campaign model. The rare operator running both affiliate + dropshipping can stack them — but that's an edge case.

## Verdict

For dropshippers and DTC e-commerce operators: Minea. Full stop. The dropshipping-specific workflow is genuinely unique and purpose-built.

For CPA affiliate marketers: AdRecon. Affiliate-network classification, Landing Page Ripper, and Winning Offers Directory fit the CPA workflow in ways Minea wasn't built to.

For everyone else: depends on campaign model. If you're testing both dropshipping and affiliate approaches, start with whichever one is primary and add the other later.
