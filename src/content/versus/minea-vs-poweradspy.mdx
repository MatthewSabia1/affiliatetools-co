## TL;DR

**Choose Minea if:** You run dropshipping or DTC e-commerce and need ad visibility across Facebook, TikTok, Pinterest, and influencer surfaces with supplier hints and product research. Minea Starter €49/mo, Premium €99/mo, Business €399/mo (as of April 2026). Free tier for tasting. Best for multi-surface product hunters, Shopify operators, and DTC brands.

**Choose PowerAdSpy if:** You run or research ads across eight platforms — Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, Google Display, Native, Quora, Reddit, TikTok — and need generalist breadth rather than e-commerce-specific tooling. PowerAdSpy Basic $49/mo scaling to Ultimate $299/mo (as of April 2026). Best for multi-channel media buyers and performance generalists.

These tools address different audiences. Minea sells e-commerce depth on four product-adjacent surfaces. PowerAdSpy sells platform breadth across eight surfaces including native and display that nobody else covers cleanly.

If your actual job is CPA affiliate research on Meta — ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods — neither tool is built for it. AdRecon is, and we flag it honestly.

## Where Minea wins

**E-commerce and dropshipping features PowerAdSpy doesn't attempt.** Product research, supplier hints, Shopify-adjacent tooling, niche-level filtering for DTC verticals. PowerAdSpy is a generalist ad-spy tool without these features. For dropshippers hunting winning products, Minea's lens is the correct one.

**Pinterest coverage.** Minea indexes Pinterest ads — useful for home decor, fashion, lifestyle, and visual-discovery verticals. PowerAdSpy doesn't cover Pinterest. For e-commerce operators in these niches, Minea is the only option between these two.

**Influencer ad surface.** Minea tracks influencer-driven ads as a distinct category. PowerAdSpy treats creator content as part of general platform indexing. For DTC brands running creator campaigns, Minea's dedicated surface is meaningfully useful.

**Better UX for product browsing.** Minea's product cards, niche tags, and media-first layout are tuned for scanning dozens of candidate products per session. PowerAdSpy's UX is closer to 2019-era research patterns — functional but slower for pure product research.

**Free tier generosity.** Minea's free tier is more functional than PowerAdSpy's trial options for most users testing the category. For operators evaluating tools without committing budget, Minea's free tasting path is smoother.

## Where PowerAdSpy wins

**Eight-platform coverage.** Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, Google Display, Native, Quora, Reddit, TikTok. Minea covers four surfaces (Facebook, TikTok, Pinterest, influencer). For research that touches native, display, Reddit, Quora, or YouTube, PowerAdSpy is the only option between these two.

**Native and Google Display inventory.** PowerAdSpy is one of the only tools in the category indexing native-ad networks (Taboola, Outbrain-style placements) and Google Display. Minea doesn't touch either. For affiliates running native campaigns or media buyers analyzing Google Display competition, PowerAdSpy is the category standard.

**Reddit and Quora ads.** PowerAdSpy covers Reddit and Quora — useful for SaaS, fintech, and B2B research. Minea doesn't. For operators whose verticals rely on these platforms, PowerAdSpy's coverage is uniquely valuable.

**USD-native pricing.** $49-$299/mo in USD (as of April 2026). Minea's EUR pricing introduces currency friction and conversion math for US-based operators. Small but real — renewal costs fluctuate with exchange rates.

**Longer category presence in affiliate/performance circles.** PowerAdSpy is one of the older multi-platform ad-spy tools with broader familiarity in certain affiliate and performance-marketing circles. Team onboarding is easier if your operators already know the workflow.

## Pricing head-to-head

**Minea:** Starter €49/mo + Premium €99/mo + Business €399/mo (as of April 2026). Free tier for tasting. Year-one: €588 to €4,788 — roughly $635 to $5,200 at recent exchange rates.

**PowerAdSpy:** Basic $49/mo, mid-tier plans, Ultimate at $299/mo (as of April 2026). Trial on select tiers. Year-one: $588 (Basic) to $3,588 (Ultimate).

At entry, PowerAdSpy Basic ($49) vs Minea Starter (~$52-55) is within $3-6/mo of each other — basically tied. You're buying different products: eight-platform generalist research (PowerAdSpy) vs four-surface e-commerce research (Minea).

At mid tier, PowerAdSpy's mid-tier plans (~$99-$149) and Minea Premium (~$107) are comparable. PowerAdSpy gives broader platform coverage with general research features. Minea gives e-commerce-specific tooling on fewer surfaces.

At upper tiers, Minea Business (€399, ~$430) targets serious e-commerce teams with specific feature needs. PowerAdSpy Ultimate ($299) targets generalist research at volume. Minea is more expensive but differentiated for DTC workflows.

Over three years at steady prices, PowerAdSpy mid-tier totals roughly $3,564. Minea Premium totals ~$3,852. Close enough that pricing rarely drives the decision — use case does.

## The affiliate marketer question — is either the right fit?

For CPA affiliate research specifically, neither tool is built for the job.

Neither auto-classifies affiliate networks. ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, WarriorPlus — none appear as filterable fields in either product. Minea's filters assume e-commerce context. PowerAdSpy's filters assume broad platform research. Neither serves the CPA affiliate's core "which network is this offer on?" question.

Neither archives complete landing pages. Minea stores destination URLs with e-commerce metadata. PowerAdSpy stores destination URLs across platforms. Neither captures the full HTML/CSS/assets of the funnel as a downloadable archive. When the LP goes down or the advertiser changes the funnel, the historical page is lost.

Neither groups creative concepts across independent advertisers with proof thresholds. If fifteen different Meta advertisers run the same creative, both tools show you fifteen separate ads. There's no "proven concept" filter surfacing creatives validated by multiple independent operators — the signal affiliate buyers want.

## Final verdict

Minea and PowerAdSpy address different buyers. Minea is an e-commerce research specialist for dropshippers and DTC operators. PowerAdSpy is a generalist multi-platform research tool for performance marketers and multi-channel affiliates.

If you're running a Shopify store, hunting dropship products, or producing DTC campaigns, Minea is the correct buy. The e-commerce-specific tooling, supplier hints, and Pinterest/influencer coverage all serve that workflow.

If you buy or research ads across native, display, Reddit, Quora, YouTube, and Meta, PowerAdSpy is the correct buy. The eight-platform breadth — especially the native and display coverage — isn't matched elsewhere at this price point.

For CPA affiliates on Meta, we keep recommending AdRecon for the reasons above. Network classification and landing-page archives are the two jobs neither tool above ships, and those are the jobs affiliate research actually runs on.
