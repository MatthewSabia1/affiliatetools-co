## Where AdSpy wins

**Boolean search operators.** AdSpy's AND/OR/NOT query language across advertiser, text, CTA, and keyword fields is the most expressive search in the ad-spy category. PowerAdSpy's search has matured but doesn't replicate AdSpy's query power. For researchers building complex repeatable queries, AdSpy is hard to replace.

**Deep Meta history.** AdSpy has focused on Meta exclusively since approximately 2015. That decade of single-platform crawling yields historical depth PowerAdSpy doesn't match — PowerAdSpy's engineering effort is spread across eight surfaces, and Meta is one of them rather than the main one. For retrospective Meta research, AdSpy wins.

**Mature Meta-specific handling.** Advertiser page merges, carousel ad variants, dedupe logic, creative-level metadata — a decade of Meta-only focus shows up in how AdSpy handles Facebook and Instagram edge cases. Generalist tools rarely match that polish on any one platform.

**Affiliate-community heritage.** AdSpy has been the default "ad spy tool" reference in affiliate marketing since roughly 2015. Team familiarity is real. If your operators already read AdSpy screenshots without explanation, the switching cost to PowerAdSpy is a line item.

**Predictable flat pricing.** $149/mo, one tier (as of April 2026). Simple annual math. PowerAdSpy's five tiers ($49 to $299) introduce upgrade-path pressure AdSpy doesn't have.

## Where PowerAdSpy wins

**Broader platform coverage.** Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, Google Display, Native, Quora, Reddit, TikTok. AdSpy covers only the first two. For media buyers running display, native, or short-form campaigns alongside Meta, PowerAdSpy is the only option between these two — and often the only single-seat option in the category for native + display specifically.

**Lower entry price.** PowerAdSpy Basic at $49/mo (as of April 2026) is one-third of AdSpy's $149/mo. For operators scaling slowly or running small budgets, Basic is approachable where AdSpy is a blocker. Mid-tier plans line up close to AdSpy's flat rate, so the value comparison depends on which tier you need.

**Native and display coverage AdSpy doesn't attempt.** Native-ad networks (Taboola, Outbrain, Revcontent-style placements) and Google Display inventory are in PowerAdSpy's scope. AdSpy doesn't touch either. For multi-channel affiliates running native alongside Meta, PowerAdSpy consolidates the research.

**TikTok coverage.** PowerAdSpy indexes TikTok ads. AdSpy doesn't. If TikTok is even a secondary channel in your stack, PowerAdSpy reduces tool sprawl.

**Tier flexibility.** Five pricing tiers let you buy only the platforms and limits you need. AdSpy's flat single-tier model means you either pay $149/mo or don't buy at all. For budget-constrained buyers, PowerAdSpy's tier structure is more accommodating.

## Pricing head-to-head

**AdSpy:** $149/mo flat (as of April 2026). No free trial. 3-day money-back guarantee. Year-one: $1,788.

**PowerAdSpy:** Basic $49/mo, and higher tiers scaling up to Ultimate at $299/mo (as of April 2026). Some tiers offer a 7-day trial. Year-one: $588 (Basic) to $3,588 (Ultimate).

At Basic, PowerAdSpy costs $100/mo less than AdSpy. That's $1,200/year saved — but Basic's ad limits and platform access may be tighter than what a serious operator needs. Mid-tier PowerAdSpy lands close to AdSpy's $149/mo with eight platforms vs AdSpy's two, which is the headline-value comparison.

Ultimate at $299/mo is twice AdSpy's price. It buys higher query limits and more seats, not better Meta data. If you only need Meta depth, AdSpy at $149/mo delivers more Meta-specific value per dollar than PowerAdSpy Ultimate's broader inventory spread thin.

Over three years at steady prices, AdSpy totals $5,364. PowerAdSpy mid-tier lands similar. The pricing conversation is really a scope conversation — are you buying depth on Meta or breadth across eight platforms?

## The affiliate marketer question — is either the right fit?

Neither tool solves the CPA affiliate's core research problem: which ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, or WarriorPlus offers are paying on Meta right now, and what landing pages are converting for them?

Neither auto-classifies affiliate networks. Both tools ask you to paste URLs and hope. When networks rotate subdomains or cloak through redirectors, domain-based filtering breaks. This is the single most common complaint affiliate operators raise about both products.

Neither archives complete landing pages. AdSpy shows a destination URL. PowerAdSpy shows a destination URL plus some platform context. Neither captures the full HTML/CSS/assets of the funnel as a downloadable archive. When the LP goes down or the advertiser changes the funnel, the historical page is lost.

Neither groups creative concepts across independent advertisers. If fifteen advertisers run the same Meta creative concept, both tools show you fifteen ads. There's no proof-threshold layer that surfaces creative ideas validated by multiple independent operators — the signal affiliate buyers actually want.

## Final verdict

AdSpy and PowerAdSpy compete on different axes. AdSpy is the Meta depth play — Boolean search, decade of crawl history, single-platform polish. PowerAdSpy is the breadth play — eight platforms in one seat, native and display coverage, mid-tier pricing flexibility.

If you only care about Meta, AdSpy is the cleaner buy. The Boolean search language and historical depth compound into real productivity, and you won't pay for platform coverage you don't use.

If you actually buy or research ads across multiple surfaces — Meta plus YouTube, plus native, plus Reddit — PowerAdSpy consolidates the view into one seat. Data quality varies by platform, so a trial is worth doing before committing.
