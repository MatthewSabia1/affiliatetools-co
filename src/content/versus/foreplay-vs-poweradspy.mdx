## TL;DR

**Choose Foreplay if:** You're a creative strategist, agency, or in-house team building swipe files and creative briefs across Meta and TikTok. Foreplay Inspiration $49/mo, Agency $99/mo (as of April 2026), ~7-day free trial. Best for creative-ops workflows with the most modern UX in the category.

**Choose PowerAdSpy if:** You run or research ads across multiple platforms including native, Google Display, Reddit, Quora, and TikTok alongside Meta. PowerAdSpy Basic $49/mo scaling to Ultimate $299/mo (as of April 2026). Best for multi-channel performance marketers and generalists.

These tools address fundamentally different jobs. Foreplay is a creative-operations platform. PowerAdSpy is a multi-platform research database. The overlap is small — a solo agency doing both creative work and multi-channel research might run both, but most buyers pick one.

If your real job is CPA affiliate research on Meta — ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods — neither tool ships the features you need. AdRecon is built for that specific workflow, and we flag it honestly.

## Where Foreplay wins

**Modern agency-grade UX.** Foreplay is one of the most visually polished products in the ad-spy category — drag-and-drop boards, dark mode, keyboard shortcuts, clean responsive design. PowerAdSpy's UX feels closer to 2019-era patterns — functional but dated. For teams working inside the tool daily, Foreplay's polish compounds into real hours saved over a year.

**Creative-operations features PowerAdSpy doesn't attempt.** Brief builder, shared boards, design-tool integrations, and collaboration workflows. PowerAdSpy is a research tool; Foreplay is a creative-ops platform with a research layer. If your deliverable is "a creative brief by Friday," Foreplay handles that workflow natively.

**Swipe-file model as a first-class feature.** Saved ads aren't just saved — they're organized into tagged, annotated boards ready for team review. PowerAdSpy saves ads into a flat list. For teams that curate swipe files as core process, Foreplay is the purpose-built tool.

**Better for team collaboration.** Shared boards, team seats, client review permissions. PowerAdSpy is more of an individual-researcher tool. For agency-client workflows, Foreplay handles the multi-stakeholder dynamics natively.

**Cleaner data-quality reputation.** PowerAdSpy has periodic complaints about indexing gaps in affiliate communities, particularly around TikTok and native networks. Foreplay's narrower platform scope (Meta + TikTok) shows better per-platform quality. Depth beats breadth for creative-reference work.

## Where PowerAdSpy wins

**Eight-platform coverage.** Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, Google Display, Native, Quora, Reddit, TikTok. Foreplay covers three (Facebook, Instagram, TikTok). For multi-channel research, PowerAdSpy is the only option between these two — and one of the only tools in the category that touches native networks and Google Display.

**Native and Google Display inventory.** PowerAdSpy indexes native-ad networks (Taboola, Outbrain, Revcontent-style placements) and Google Display. Foreplay doesn't attempt either. For affiliates running native campaigns or media buyers analyzing Google Display competition, PowerAdSpy is the only option here.

**Reddit and Quora ads.** Useful for SaaS, fintech, and B2B research where those platforms carry real budget. Foreplay's Meta + TikTok focus excludes these entirely. For SaaS and B2B operators, PowerAdSpy's breadth includes surfaces Foreplay doesn't.

**Research throughput at higher tiers.** PowerAdSpy's upper tiers ($199-$299/mo) support higher query volumes suited to research-heavy teams. Foreplay's tiers are seat-based for creative ops; if you need raw research volume, PowerAdSpy's tiering accommodates it better.

**More established in affiliate/performance community.** PowerAdSpy is one of the older multi-platform ad-spy tools, which means broader familiarity in certain affiliate and performance-marketing circles. Team onboarding is easier if your operators already know PowerAdSpy's workflow.

**Tier flexibility for scaling research budgets.** PowerAdSpy's five tiers let you upgrade gradually as research needs grow. Foreplay's tiering assumes team seats and creative-ops features are what you're buying more of — if your growth is in raw research volume, PowerAdSpy's structure matches that trajectory better.

**Useful for affiliates researching non-creative signals.** PowerAdSpy surfaces advertiser-level stats (ad count, platform mix, geo spread) across eight platforms that Foreplay's creative-ops UX doesn't prioritize. For operators asking "is this advertiser scaling?", PowerAdSpy's data density serves that question directly.

## Pricing head-to-head

**Foreplay:** Inspiration $49/mo + Agency $99/mo + higher tiers for larger teams (confirm current lineup on foreplay.co). Free trial ~7 days. Year-one: $588 (Inspiration) to $1,188 (Agency).

**PowerAdSpy:** Basic $49/mo, mid-tier plans, Ultimate at $299/mo (as of April 2026). Trial on select tiers. Year-one: $588 (Basic) to $3,588 (Ultimate).

At entry, Foreplay Inspiration ($49/mo) and PowerAdSpy Basic ($49/mo) are the same price. You're buying different products — Foreplay is creative ops for three platforms, PowerAdSpy is research across eight. Use case decides fit.

At mid tier, Foreplay Agency ($99/mo) and PowerAdSpy mid-tier plans (~$99-$149/mo) are close. Agency gives you creative-ops with team seats. PowerAdSpy mid-tier gives you eight platforms with higher query limits.

PowerAdSpy Ultimate ($299/mo) targets serious multi-channel research teams with highest-volume needs. Foreplay's equivalent higher tiers are team-scale creative-ops seats. Different buyers.

Over three years at steady prices, Foreplay Agency totals $3,564. PowerAdSpy mid-tier totals roughly similar. Scope divergence matters more than price.

## The affiliate marketer question — is either the right fit?

Neither tool was built for CPA affiliate research specifically, and both show the same gaps.

Neither auto-classifies affiliate networks. ClickBank, Digistore24, BuyGoods, MaxWeb, WarriorPlus — not filterable fields in either product. Manual domain tagging is the only workaround, and it breaks when networks rotate subdomains or cloak through redirectors.

Neither archives complete landing pages. Foreplay stores ad references with creative-ops metadata. PowerAdSpy stores destination URLs across platforms. Neither captures the full HTML/CSS/assets of the funnel as a downloadable ZIP archive. When the LP goes down or the funnel changes, the historical page is gone in both.

Neither groups creative concepts across independent advertisers with proof thresholds. If fifteen different advertisers run the same Meta creative, both tools show you fifteen separate ads. There's no "proven concept" layer that surfaces creatives validated by multiple independent operators — the signal affiliate buyers want most.

## Final verdict

Foreplay and PowerAdSpy aren't really competing for the same buyer. Foreplay is a modern creative-operations platform for agencies and brand teams working on Meta and TikTok. PowerAdSpy is a multi-platform research database for performance marketers buying across native, display, Meta, and other surfaces.

If your deliverables are creative briefs and swipe files, Foreplay is the correct buy. The agency workflow features and modern UX compound into real productivity for creative-driven teams.

If your deliverables are cross-platform research reports or multi-channel media buys, PowerAdSpy is the correct buy. The breadth — especially native, display, Reddit, Quora — isn't matched elsewhere at this price point.

For CPA affiliates on Meta specifically, we keep recommending AdRecon for the reasons above. Network classification and landing-page archives are neither tool's strength, and those are the two jobs affiliate research actually runs on.
